# Pricing Benchmarks for TTRPG Products on DriveThruRPG

This reference gives **practical price anchors** by product type and
page count. All figures are USD and reflect typical DriveThruRPG
pricing patterns in early 2026. Adjust for brand strength, color
density, art investment, and audience size.

These are benchmarks, not commandments. A creator with a strong brand
and a tight niche can price 20–40% above benchmark. A debut creator
with no audience usually needs to price 10–30% below benchmark to
trigger initial volume.

## 2. Pricing Model: PWYW vs Fixed vs Hybrid

### Pay-What-You-Want with $0 minimum

**Use when:**

- First product, building audience and download volume.
- Free quickstart / lite version of a paid product.
- Promotional release tied to a launch of a paid sibling.
- Brand-building zine or sampler.
- Charity / community contribution.

**Don't use when:**

- The product is genuinely premium and you need revenue from sale 1.
- You have an established audience that will buy at any price; you
  leave money on the table.
- The product is the only one you'll ever publish in this universe.

**Conversion expectation:** in PWYW at $0 minimum, typically 20–40% of
downloaders pay something. Of those, the average payment hovers around
30–50% of the suggested price (when a suggested price is set). Free
downloads still count toward badge thresholds.

### Pay-What-You-Want with suggested price

A middle ground. Functions like "name your price, but here's our
recommendation."

**Use when:**

- You want PWYW visibility benefits but signal value.
- Sequel or expansion to a paid product (where the audience already
  knows the brand).
- You're testing price elasticity.

**Common pattern:** suggested price set ~20% above what a fixed price
would be (because half the audience will pay less).

### Fixed Price

**Use when:**

- Established audience trusts the brand.
- Dense, premium product (full-color, art-heavy, long).
- Sequel in a successful series.
- You need predictable per-unit revenue.

**Don't use when:**

- Debut product, no audience, no list, no cross-promotion path.
- You're competing in a saturated category (PWYW differentiates).

### Hybrid: Free Quickstart → Paid Main

The single most reliable launch pattern for a debut creator:

1. Publish a **free quickstart / sampler** (8–24 pages) as PWYW $0.
2. Use the free product to build downloads, badges, and audience.
3. Link the free product to the **paid main product** in its
   description and final page ("If you enjoyed this, the full
   product is here").
4. Publish the paid main product as fixed price.

This bypasses the visibility cold-start problem.

## 3. Badge-Targeted Pricing

If the strategic goal is to badge fast (for the conversion lift):

| Target badge | Approximate units | Pricing implication |
|---|---|---|
| Copper (~50) | Sell 50 copies | At $4.99 = $250 gross. PWYW $0 with strong promotion can hit this in 1–2 months. |
| Silver (~100) | Sell 100 copies | At $4.99 = $500 gross. Most first-year goals top out here. |
| Electrum (~250) | Sell 250 copies | At $4.99 = $1,250 gross. Realistic with consistent promotion + 2nd product cross-pollination. |
| Gold (~500) | Sell 500 copies | At $4.99 = $2,500 gross. Requires audience, cross-promotion, or platform attention (front-page feature, bundle inclusion). |
| Platinum (~1,000) | Sell 1,000 copies | Requires significant momentum or a viral moment. |

**Strategic note:** badges compound. A product with a Gold badge sells
faster than the same product with no badge. Front-loading promotion to
reach the first badge accelerates everything after.

## 4. Sale Cadence Calendar

DriveThruRPG runs platform-wide sales several times a year. Publishers
can also discount independently at any time. A product with no sale
plan misses 20–40% of yearly revenue.

| Period | Theme | Typical discount | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| March (around 4 March) | GMs Day | 25–50% off | Strongest platform-wide GM-targeted sale. |
| April (around 4 May) | May the 4th / Sci-Fi | 25–40% off | Sci-Fi focused; relevant only for sci-fi catalog. |
| Mid-June | Free RPG Day (around 3rd Saturday) | Free promo products | Submit a free product if applicable. |
| July | Christmas in July | 25–50% off | Strong general TTRPG sale. |
| Late August | Back-to-School / GenCon week | 25–40% off | Tie to GenCon attention. |
| October | Halloween | 25–50% off | Strongest for horror, gothic, Halloween-themed products. |
| Late November | Black Friday / Cyber Monday | 30–60% off | Highest discounts of the year; high volume. |
| December | Holiday season | 25–40% off | Steady throughout December. |

### Discount tier guidance

- **25% off**: introductory promo, low friction, doesn't damage anchor price.
- **33% off**: standard sale tier, good visibility on the deals page.
- **50% off**: aggressive, used for clear-out or competitive promo.
- **60–75% off**: discounts this deep can devalue the brand if used
  routinely. Reserve for end-of-life products or bundle context.

### Independent sales

You can discount any product at any time outside platform-wide sales.
Useful for:

- Launching a new product and discounting prior products in the same
  catalog (lifts both via cross-promotion).
- Recovery from a poor launch (drop 30% for a month, gather reviews,
  re-test pricing).
- Birthday / anniversary of brand or product launch.

## 5. Bundles

A bundle is a single listing that includes multiple products at a
combined discount. DriveThruRPG supports several bundle mechanics.

### Standard Bundle

- Creator picks 2+ products from their catalog.
- Sets a bundle price below the sum of individual prices (typical
  discount: 20–35%).
- Buyer gets all included products on purchase.

**Use when:**

- Catalog has 2+ thematically related products (e.g., adventure +
  bestiary in the same setting).
- Existing audience would buy individually anyway; bundle lifts
  average order value.
- Discount is ≥ 20% (smaller discounts don't motivate).

### "Customers Who Bought" Boost

DriveThruRPG cross-recommends products. A creator with 3+ products
in the same niche should ensure each product cross-links the others
in the blurb. The platform's recommendation engine amplifies natural
co-purchase patterns.

### Bundle of Holding (external, separate site)

A separate site (`bundleofholding.com`) runs curated time-limited
bundles. Participation is invite-only and curated by the BoH team.
Significant exposure when accepted. Not a regular revenue lever,
but worth noting as an audience-building event.

## 6. POD Pricing Logic

When adding Print on Demand:

### Margin math (simplified, B&W softcover digest, 64 pages)

- Base production cost: ~$5–6 per unit
- Retail price suggested: $14.99
- Publisher revenue per unit: ~$8–9 (before DriveThruRPG fee)
- Net publisher take: ~$6–7 per unit after platform cut

### Margin math (full-color hardcover, 200 pages)

- Base production cost: ~$25–30 per unit
- Retail price suggested: $44.99
- Publisher revenue per unit: ~$14–18
- Net publisher take: ~$10–13 per unit

### Pricing principle

- POD price should be **2× to 3× the base cost** for meaningful
  margin while staying competitive.
- POD price should be **2× to 4× the PDF price** for the same
  product (e.g., $9.99 PDF → $19.99–$29.99 print).
- A POD that costs $30 against peer products at $24.99 is fighting
  the category.

### When to add POD vs when to wait

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| PDF has sold 100+ units in 6 months | Add POD now |
| Product is print-ready (margins, bleed, cover wrap correct) | Add POD now |
| Product needs design work to be print-ready | Wait until invested |
| Product is digital-native (battlemaps, tokens, audio) | Skip POD |
| Product is < 32 pages | Likely too thin for softcover; consider digital-only |

## 7. Currency and Geography

DriveThruRPG primary currency is USD. Other currencies are converted
at checkout.

### Implications

- French-language products targeting the FR market still price in USD.
  An FR audience compares to USD pricing of FR competitors (mostly Black
  Book Editions PDFs, SDEN community content, etc.) — typically slightly
  above USD-equivalent for FR-specific products.
- The euro / USD rate fluctuates ±10% over a year — pricing decisions
  rarely need to optimize for this, but extreme rate shifts can be
  noted as "good time to launch."

### FR market specifics

- The FR JDR audience on DriveThruRPG is smaller than EN but loyal.
- FR products often perform better on **Lulu.com**, **Black Book
  Editions store**, **SDEN community**, or directly via Patreon/Ko-fi
  than on DriveThruRPG.
- A bilingual product (FR + EN in same PDF) gets the widest audience
  but adds layout cost; usually only viable for major releases.

## 8. Adult Content Pricing Patterns

Adult-tagged products on DriveThruRPG follow a different pricing
curve due to gating and audience characteristics:

| Product Type | Typical adult-content range |
|---|---|
| Adult adventure / scenario | $4.99–14.99 |
| Adult setting / sourcebook | $7.99–24.99 |
| Adult zine | $4.99–9.99 |
| Adult character supplement / NPC pack | $2.99–9.99 |
| Adult illustration pack / portrait set | $4.99–19.99 |

**Why higher than equivalent SFW products:**

- Smaller audience requires higher per-unit revenue.
- Adult buyers are typically older, higher-disposable-income, and
  expect production polish.
- Less competition at the high end of the price range.

**Pitfalls:**

- Pricing adult content like SFW equivalents leaves money on the table.
- Pricing too high without an established adult brand depresses volume
  beneath the badge threshold; no badge means no algorithmic boost.

## 9. Brand-Strength Multipliers

Apply these multipliers to the benchmark table based on brand standing:

| Brand position | Multiplier | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Debut creator, no audience | 0.7–0.9× | First product, no list |
| Returning creator, growing audience | 1.0× | 2nd or 3rd product, ~500 followers |
| Established niche creator | 1.1–1.3× | 5+ products, ~2,000+ followers, known brand |
| Recognized imprint | 1.3–1.6× | Ludomancien, Fantasy Vixens-tier brand |
| Major publisher | 1.5–2.0× | Free League, Chaosium, etc. |

A debut adventure at 0.8× the $5.99–$11.99 range = $4.79–$9.59 → round
to $4.99 or $6.99 or $7.99 depending on page count.

## 10. Price-Setting Workflow

When evaluating or setting a price:

1. **Identify product type and page count** → find the row × column
   in Section 1.
2. **Adjust for production quality** → low end if B&W minimal art,
   high end if dense full color.
3. **Apply brand multiplier** (Section 9).
4. **Round to nearest psychological price point**: $X.99 or $X.49 are
   the strongest anchors. $5.00 underperforms $4.99 by a measurable
   margin.
5. **Cross-check against direct competitors** in the same niche on
   DriveThruRPG → are you within ±20% of comparable products?
6. **Decide PWYW vs Fixed** (Section 2).
7. **Set initial promotion plan** (Section 4) → tie launch to next
   relevant platform sale if within 60 days.
