# Mechanical Conversion Tables

Cross-system tables for XP, treasure, AC, save DCs, ability score
mapping, damage scaling, monster HP, spell levels, encounter
difficulty.

Use these tables alongside the path-specific reference files.

### PF2e XP (encounter-based, relative to party level)

PF2e XP is awarded relative to creature level vs party level:

| Creature vs Party Level | XP per Creature |
|---|---|
| -4 or lower | 0 (trivial) |
| -3 | 10 |
| -2 | 20 |
| -1 | 30 |
| equal | 40 |
| +1 | 60 |
| +2 | 80 |
| +3 | 120 |
| +4 or higher | 160 (boss) |

PF2e encounter budgets per 4-PC party:
- **Trivial:** 40 XP
- **Low:** 60 XP
- **Moderate:** 80 XP
- **Severe:** 120 XP
- **Extreme:** 160 XP

## AC Conversion

### Ascending vs Descending AC

For 5e → OSR (B/X with descending) conversion:

| 5e AC (ascending, higher = better) | B/X AC (descending, lower = better) |
|---|---|
| 10 | 9 |
| 11 | 8 |
| 12 | 7 |
| 13 | 6 |
| 14 | 5 |
| 15 | 4 |
| 16 | 3 |
| 17 | 2 |
| 18 | 1 |
| 19 | 0 |
| 20 | -1 |
| 21 | -2 |
| 22 | -3 |

Formula: **B/X AC = 19 - 5e AC**

For 5e → Shadowdark (ascending), AC values are nearly identical
(±1 typically).

For 5e → PF2e, AC is approximately **+2 higher** at same level
due to PF2e's level-scaled bonuses. A 5e PC at level 5 with
medium armor has AC ~16; equivalent PF2e PC at level 5 has AC
~18.

## Save DC Conversion

### 5e → PF2e DC

PF2e DCs are level-scaled. Conversion:

| 5e DC | PF2e DC (assuming task at party level) |
|---|---|
| DC 10 (Easy) | Party level baseline -2 |
| DC 12 | Party level baseline -1 |
| DC 15 (Medium) | Party level baseline |
| DC 17 | Party level baseline +1 |
| DC 20 (Hard) | Party level baseline +2 |
| DC 25 (Very Hard) | Party level baseline +5 |
| DC 30 | Party level baseline +10 |

(PF2e baseline DCs by level: 14, 15, 16, 18, 19, 20, 22, 23, 24,
26, 27, 28, 30, 31, 32...)

### 5e → OSR

OSR rarely uses DCs. Convert as:
- DC 10: success likely → "if PC declares the action and is
  reasonable, succeeds."
- DC 15: ability check (roll-under stat on d20, or 1d6 chance
  per relevant attribute).
- DC 20: difficult ability check (high stat helps, or rare success
  on d6).
- DC 25+: nearly impossible without magic.

## Damage Scaling

### 5e Damage by Spell/Attack Level

| Spell Level | Single-Target Damage | AoE Damage |
|---|---|---|
| Cantrip (lvl 1) | 1d10 | 1d6 |
| 1st | 4d6 (Magic Missile area) | 3d6 |
| 2nd | 4d8 (Scorching Ray) | 4d6 |
| 3rd | 8d6 (Fireball) | 8d6 |
| 4th | 6d10 | 6d8 |
| 5th | 8d8 | 9d6 (Cone of Cold) |

### PF2e Damage Scaling

PF2e damage scales similarly but uses fixed dice + level-based
ranks:

| Spell Rank | Single Target | AoE |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | 4d6 | 3d6 |
| 2nd | 5d6 | 4d6 |
| 3rd | 6d6 (Fireball baseline 6d6) | 6d6 |
| 4th | 7d6 | 7d6 |
| 5th | 8d6 | 8d6 |

PF2e tends to use **smaller dice with more of them**, scaling
predictably per rank above base.

### OSR Damage

Generally lower:

| Spell Level / Caster Lvl | Damage |
|---|---|
| 1st (Magic Missile) | 1d4+1 per missile (1 missile per 5 levels) |
| 3rd (Fireball at lvl 5 caster) | 5d6 |
| 3rd (Fireball at lvl 10 caster) | 10d6 (max) |

OSR uses caster level to scale damage; rounds, not bonus actions.

## Encounter Difficulty Mapping

### 5e Encounter Difficulty

| 5e Difficulty | XP Budget per PC (party of 4) |
|---|---|
| Easy | 25-100 |
| Medium | 50-150 |
| Hard | 100-200 |
| Deadly | 150-400+ |

### PF2e Encounter Difficulty

| PF2e Difficulty | Total XP (party of 4) |
|---|---|
| Trivial | 40 |
| Low | 60 |
| Moderate | 80 |
| Severe | 120 |
| Extreme | 160 |

### OSR Encounter Difficulty

OSR doesn't have "difficulty levels" in the same way. Calibration
is by HD:

| OSR Encounter Type | HD Total (party of 4 1st-lvl PCs) |
|---|---|
| Trivial (random) | 1-2 HD |
| Standard (planned) | 3-5 HD |
| Hard | 6-8 HD |
| Deadly | 10+ HD |
| TPK-likely | 15+ HD |

Note: at higher PC levels, scale up proportionally.

### Conversion Rule

| 5e | PF2e | OSR |
|---|---|---|
| Easy | Trivial-Low | Trivial-Standard |
| Medium | Low-Moderate | Standard |
| Hard | Moderate-Severe | Hard |
| Deadly | Severe-Extreme | Deadly to TPK |

## Ability Score Comparison

### 5e Ability Modifiers

| Score | Modifier |
|---|---|
| 8 | -1 |
| 10 | +0 |
| 12 | +1 |
| 14 | +2 |
| 16 | +3 |
| 18 | +4 |
| 20 | +5 |
| 24 | +7 (some giants/dragons) |
| 28 | +9 (extreme creatures) |
| 30 | +10 |

### PF2e Ability Modifiers

Same scoring: 8 = -1, 10 = +0, 12 = +1, etc.

PF2e PCs cap at +4 base ability mod at lvl 1, with boosts +1 per
several levels. Mathematically nearly identical to 5e.

### OSR Ability Scores

Used for **direct ability score targets**, not modifiers. A roll-
under STR check rolls d20 against the STR score (e.g., STR 15 means
1-15 succeeds).

| 5e Modifier | OSR Score Range |
|---|---|
| -3 | 3 |
| -2 | 4-5 |
| -1 | 6-8 |
| +0 | 9-12 |
| +1 | 13-15 |
| +2 | 16-17 |
| +3 | 18 (max for PCs without magic) |

OSR PCs almost never have stats > 18. Magic items may give bonuses
to score directly.
