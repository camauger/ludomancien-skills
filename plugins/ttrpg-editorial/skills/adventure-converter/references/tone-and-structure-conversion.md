# Tone and Structure Conversion

Beyond the numbers: design philosophy shifts between systems,
audience expectations per community, conversion sidebars, when to
keep read-aloud vs adjust, what to cut/add.

A converted module that has correct numbers but wrong tone will
fail at the target audience's table. This document covers the
non-mathematical half of conversion.

---

## System Design Philosophies

### 5e (2014/2024)

- **Heroic tone.** PCs start as capable adventurers; lethality is
  moderate.
- **Long-rest reset.** Resources refresh daily. Each day's
  encounters can be approached fresh.
- **Skill-based resolution.** DCs and ability checks govern most
  non-combat outcomes.
- **Narrative pacing.** Modules often have act structure, three-
  clue redundancy, paced reveals.
- **Inclusivity.** 2024 explicitly broadened character options
  (species choice, alignment flexibility, etc.).

### PF2e

- **Tactical precision.** Action economy, conditions, and traits
  reward planning and engagement.
- **Granular balance.** Encounter math is more reliable; both
  TPK risk and trivial encounters are by design.
- **Build complexity.** PCs choose ancestry feats, class feats,
  skill feats, general feats — many decisions.
- **Multiple progression axes.** Untrained / Trained / Expert /
  Master / Legendary skill ranks tier proficiency.
- **Crit success/failure narrative.** The 10-point swing creates
  more dramatic moments per encounter.

### OSR

- **Mortal stakes.** Lethality is the design. PCs die. Players
  treat their characters as expendable until they survive.
- **GM rulings, not rules.** Most resolution is GM-mediated.
- **Resource attrition.** Light, rations, ammunition, HP — all
  resources matter and deplete.
- **Treasure-driven motivation.** XP-for-gold incentivizes risk-
  vs-reward choices about treasure.
- **Procedural exploration.** Random encounter rolls, reaction
  rolls, morale checks create emergent narrative.
- **Player skill over character build.** Combat tactics, resource
  management, social cunning matter more than class abilities.

### Narrative-Light (PbtA, FitD)

- **Fiction-first.** What's happening in the fiction drives
  mechanics, not the other way around.
- **Moves and triggers.** PCs activate moves by doing things in
  fiction; moves resolve outcomes.
- **Shared authorship.** Players contribute to setting, NPCs,
  details.
- **No "rolls without consequence."** Every roll either succeeds
  with cost, fails forward, or fails with consequence.
- **Limited prep.** Modules are sparse; GM improvises within
  procedural prompts.

## Audience Expectations per Community

### 5e Community

- **Broad and casual.** Most readers are casual players or moderate
  GMs. Don't assume deep rules knowledge.
- **Polish appreciated.** Well-edited prose, evocative art, clear
  layout matter.
- **Combat dominant.** Combat encounters are the most-played and
  most-publicized content.
- **System assumed.** Don't explain 5e rules; assume the reader
  has the PHB.

**Conversion implication:** A 5e adventure should read smoothly to a
5e regular. Don't add "what is initiative" sidebars; do add "this
encounter is calibrated for 4 PCs of level X."

### PF2e Community

- **Crunch-loving.** Readers appreciate precision, optimization,
  granular rules. Less hand-holding.
- **Action-economy literate.** Readers understand 3-action turns,
  MAP, sustained spells.
- **Build-focused.** NPCs are expected to be statted with class +
  level + ancestry + class feats (a real PF2e NPC build, not just
  a stat block).
- **Tactical maps appreciated.** PF2e tends toward map-based
  tactical combat.

**Conversion implication:** A 5e adventure converted to PF2e should
"feel native." Add tactical maps, build NPCs with class+ancestry
backgrounds, document creature trait combinations clearly.

### OSR Community

- **Procedure-focused.** Readers want random encounter tables,
  hex maps, dungeon procedure, factions with reaction rolls.
- **Rulings appreciated.** "Here's a situation; here's what
  happens if PCs do X" — clear procedural prompts.
- **Brevity preferred.** OSR modules are zines, not coffee-table
  books. Tight, dense, evocative.
- **Death expected.** GM doesn't pull punches; modules don't
  apologize for hard fights.

**Conversion implication:** A 5e adventure converted to OSR should
become **denser and more procedural.** Add reaction rolls, random
encounter tables, treasure-as-XP totals, morale notes. Cut
"act 1/2/3" narrative pacing in favor of location-based
exploration.

### Narrative-Light Community

- **Fiction-first.** Modules describe situations, factions, stakes,
  characters — not encounters and stat blocks.
- **Player agency.** Readers expect PCs to have meaningful choice
  about scene direction.
- **Light prep.** Modules are sparse; GMs need flexible toolkit
  with prompts.

**Conversion implication:** A 5e adventure becomes "adaptation
notes" — usually not a full conversion. Convert key encounters to
*situations with stakes*, not statted fights.

## Conversion Sidebars

For audiences coming from the source system to the target system,
sidebars explain mechanical shifts. Include where useful:

### 5e 2014 → 5e 2024 Sidebars

Less needed — most readers know both editions or quickly adapt.

| When to include | Sidebar topic |
|---|---|
| Encounter uses Weapon Mastery heavily | Brief explanation of Weapon Mastery system |
| Adventure uses Hide/Search frequently | Note the 2024 explicit Hide/Search actions |
| Background-tied feature is referenced | Explain 2024 Background ability score + Origin Feat |

### 5e → PF2e Sidebars

Much needed; readers may be new to PF2e.

| Sidebar Topic | When |
|---|---|
| Three-action economy basics | If adventure features tactical combat heavily |
| MAP and how multiple Strikes work | If adventure encounters involve combat optimization |
| Critical success/failure 10-point rule | Always in PF2e modules; saves and traps depend on this |
| Conditions are graded | If conditions imposed; explain Frightened 1 → Frightened 2 progression |
| Heightened spells vs higher slot | If spell tactics involve heightening |

### 5e → OSR Sidebars

Helpful for 5e converts entering OSR.

| Sidebar Topic | When |
|---|---|
| Reaction rolls | If any encounter uses reaction; show 2d6 table |
| Random encounter rolls | If dungeon has wandering monsters |
| Morale checks | If combat happens; show flee thresholds |
| XP for gold | Always — fundamental design shift |
| Saving throw categories vs ability saves | Always (B/X target) |
| Resource tracking (torches, rations, ammo) | If adventure depends on attrition |
| Reading the dungeon procedurally | Always |

### Narrative-Light Sidebars

Less mechanical, more conceptual.

| Sidebar Topic | When |
|---|---|
| How moves trigger from fiction | Always |
| Stakes negotiation | If adventure has dramatic moments |
| Failing forward | Always |
| GM principles (PbtA / FitD specific) | Always |

## Read-Aloud Preservation Rules

Read-aloud (boxed text describing scenes, locations, NPCs) is
**generally preserved across all conversions**. Atmospheric prose
is system-agnostic.

### When to Preserve

- Sensory descriptions of locations (rooms, terrain, atmosphere).
- NPC introductions (appearance, voice cues, mannerism).
- Dramatic openings and closings.
- Discovery moments (what the party sees when entering).

### When to Adjust

- **Mechanical interjections:** Remove or rephrase "make a DC 15
  Perception check" embedded in read-aloud — move to GM-facing
  text.
- **System-specific vocabulary:** "Roll a Wisdom save" in read-
  aloud is bad style anyway; remove or rephrase regardless of
  conversion direction.
- **Tone-mismatched cues:** A heroic-tone 5e read-aloud may need
  cooler/grittier tone for OSR. Adjust adjectives.
- **Pacing:** Long read-aloud (300+ words) might be cut for OSR
  modules (which favor brevity); kept for 5e and PF2e modules.

### Example Read-Aloud Conversion

**5e Original:**
> "You step into the chamber. Dust motes dance in the shaft of
> moonlight from the cracked ceiling. The body lies face-down,
> arms outstretched, as if reaching for the cold iron lock that
> dangles half-open. You can hear, faintly, the sound of water
> dripping somewhere below. Make a Perception check to notice the
> details — DC 15."

**Adjusted (any conversion):**
> "You step into the chamber. Dust motes dance in the shaft of
> moonlight from the cracked ceiling. The body lies face-down,
> arms outstretched, as if reaching for the cold iron lock that
> dangles half-open. You can hear, faintly, the sound of water
> dripping somewhere below."

GM notes (separate from read-aloud):
- 5e: "DC 15 Perception to notice the bloodstain trail."
- PF2e: "DC 18 Perception to notice the bloodstain trail."
- OSR: "Anyone investigating the body finds the bloodstain trail.
  If players move quickly without investigating, they miss it."

## What to Cut

When converting, some source content should be cut.

### Common Cut Reasons

| Reason | Examples |
|---|---|
| Doesn't translate cleanly | 5e Battle Master maneuver lists for an OSR conversion |
| Padding | Multiple paragraphs explaining a simple concept |
| System-specific tutorial | "How to read a stat block" in a 5e adventure converted to PF2e (PF2e readers know stat blocks) |
| Bloat | Long NPC backstories that don't affect play |
| Outdated mechanics | 5e 2014 mechanics that 2024 replaced or removed |

### Cut Discipline

- Cut **mechanics, not flavor.** Cutting prose loses atmosphere.
- Cut **dependencies, not encounter content.** Cutting an encounter
  affects pacing.
- Document **what was cut and why** in conversion notes.

## What to Add

Some additions are essential to make a converted module feel
native.

### Common Additions

| Conversion Direction | Common Additions |
|---|---|
| 5e 2014 → 2024 | Origin Feat assignment for NPCs; Weapon Mastery tactics in combat; updated initiative procedures |
| 5e → PF2e | Tactical maps (PF2e community expects them); MAP awareness in NPC tactics; conditions graded (Frightened 2 not just Frightened); ancestry & heritage assignments for NPCs |
| 5e → OSR | Random encounter tables (1d6 or 1d8); reaction roll table; morale scores per monster; treasure XP totals; turn/torch tracking in dungeon procedure; lethality warning in GM notes |
| 5e → Narrative-light | Stakes for each scene; clocks for time-pressured situations; player-facing principles; alternative move triggers |

### Add Discipline

- Add **what helps the target audience play smoothly**, not what
  shows off the converter.
- Document **what was added and why** in conversion notes.
- Don't over-add. A conversion shouldn't read like a different
  module — just a same-module-in-different-system.

## Open Issues Documentation

Some conversions involve judgment calls. Document them.

### Examples of Open Issues

- "Source's 'DC 20 Persuasion vs Faction Leader' doesn't have a
  clean PF2e equivalent because PF2e leaders have specific
  Will/Diplomacy DCs. I used DC 27 (PF2e level 8 baseline +5
  Hard). Adjust at your table to taste."

- "Source's encounter calibration is 'Hard' for 5e level 5 party
  (5,000 XP total). PF2e equivalent (Severe for level 5) is 120
  XP. I rebalanced to 120 XP of leveled monsters; encounter
  composition shifted from '4 ogres' to '2 ogres + leader.'
  Verify this matches your party's playstyle."

- "Source's 'Mass Polymorph' as a 4th-level spell. I converted to
  PF2e's 4th-rank Polymorph. PF2e's Polymorph affects 1 target
  unless heightened to 7th rank (mass effects); I kept it at
  4th rank single-target. If you want mass affect, heighten to
  7th rank as a 7th-level spell, but I've kept the original
  intent."

Open issues let the target-system GM know where the conversion
is approximate and where to apply table judgment.

## Tone Matching Anti-Patterns

### "5e-Flavored OSR"

- A 5e-converted-to-OSR module that keeps 5e's heroic tone with
  3-paragraph NPC backstories, evocative read-aloud, and long
  narrative passages.
- **Fix:** Tighten. OSR readers want sparse, evocative, procedural.

### "OSR-Flavored 5e"

- An OSR-converted-to-5e module that keeps tight, procedural
  pacing without atmosphere.
- **Fix:** Add evocative prose, more sensory detail, fewer dry
  procedural references.

### "Crunch-Stripped PF2e"

- A PF2e-converted module that strips tactical detail and reads as
  generic fantasy.
- **Fix:** PF2e readers expect crunch. Add the tactical detail back.

### "Generic Narrative-Light"

- A narrative-light-adapted module that uses PbtA terminology
  without PbtA principles.
- **Fix:** Use specific moves with specific triggers; don't just
  rebrand 5e moves with PbtA names.
