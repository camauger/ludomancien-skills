# Lore and Hook Templates

Templates for the *narrative half* of a magic item: who made it, how
it got here, what state it's in, and how it pulls the campaign
forward.

A magic item without lore is a number bonus. A magic item with lore
becomes a character in the campaign.

---

## 1. Maker Categories

Eleven archetypal categories of magic-item makers. Each shapes the
item's identity in different directions.

### 1.1 — The Artificer-Mage

A solitary scholar or workshop crafter, often working from a fallen
order's traditions or a personal obsession.

**Identity signals:**
- Precise, geometric construction
- Often paired with a maker's mark, signature glyph, or runic seal
- Sometimes self-aware in subtle ways (a quill that "knows" the
  maker's handwriting)

**Typical items:** Wondrous items, wands, well-engineered weapons,
intricate rings. Less common: organic items, divine relics.

**Lore template:**
> Crafted in the [city/workshop] of [Maker], a [order/tradition]
> known for [their distinctive approach]. The maker labored [N years]
> over the piece, integrating [signature material] from [specific
> source]. Their mark is [visible feature].

**Plot hook anchor:** Other items from the same maker exist; rivals
of the maker want the piece; the maker is alive and wants to inspect
their work.

### 1.2 — The Divine Forge

Created at a holy site by a divine being or with explicit divine
sponsorship. Often consecrated to a specific god.

**Identity signals:**
- Symbol or insignia of the deity
- Holy relic aesthetic (gold, silver, sacred wood, sanctified stone)
- Often glows softly in the presence of the faithful
- Sometimes the item *judges* its wielder

**Typical items:** Weapons, armor, holy symbols, rings of the
faithful, divine focuses (rods).

**Lore template:**
> Forged at [holy site / temple] under the eye of [deity], during
> [holy season / ritual]. The metal was tempered in [holy water /
> divine fire] and inscribed with the prayers of [N priests over X
> days]. The item is sacred to [faith].

**Plot hook anchor:** The temple wants it back; a rival faith
considers it heretical and seeks to destroy it; the item rejects
unworthy wielders.

### 1.3 — The Ancient Curse

Created accidentally or by intent through a curse-laying. The item
carries the imprint of the originating event.

**Identity signals:**
- Dark or off-kilter aesthetics (warped material, unsettling
  resonance)
- Often connected to a specific past wrong or tragedy
- May behave differently around certain creatures or in certain
  conditions

**Typical items:** Cursed-flavor items (weapons that whisper, armor
that bleeds), items tied to specific past events.

**Lore template:**
> When [past event] occurred at [place], the rage/grief/betrayal of
> [victim/perpetrator] crystallized into this [item type]. It has
> persisted for [N centuries], occasionally drawing the attention of
> [type of being].

**Plot hook anchor:** The original wrong remains unresolved; the
item's existence prevents the perpetrator's peace; descendants of
the victim seek it.

### 1.4 — The Accidental Creation

Born of an unintended magical convergence — a spell that went wrong,
a planar bleed, a wild surge that imbued an ordinary object.

**Identity signals:**
- Ordinary base item, extraordinary properties
- Sometimes uncertain or shifting (the magic isn't fully stabilized)
- Often the maker doesn't know the item exists

**Typical items:** Wondrous items, potions, anything that looks
mundane.

**Lore template:**
> The [item type] was an ordinary [original use] until [magical
> event] — when [specific surge / spell mishap / planar incursion]
> imbued it with [property]. The original owner never knew; the
> item passed through [N hands] before its magic was recognized.

**Plot hook anchor:** The magical event left other traces (other
items, environmental anomalies); recognizing the item's origin
allows for replication or counter-magic.

### 1.5 — The Monster-Bound

The magical property derives from a creature — its blood, its
breath, its bound spirit, or its preserved part.

**Identity signals:**
- Visible monster-component (fang, scale, eye, finger-bone)
- The bound creature may have lingering will or consciousness
- Properties echo the creature's abilities

**Typical items:** Weapons (dragonfang, bonebow), armor (scale-mail
from a literal dragon), wondrous items (eye gems, finger-rings).

**Lore template:**
> Crafted from the [body part] of [creature], slain by [hunter /
> hero / circumstance]. The creature's [property] persists in the
> item — and some say its spirit too. The item is most active under
> [conditions related to the creature].

**Plot hook anchor:** Other parts of the same creature exist in
other items; the creature's species seeks revenge; the creature's
spirit influences the wielder.

### 1.6 — The Fey Gift / Curse

A bargain with the Feywild, given by a Fey patron or extracted
from the Feywild's logic. The bargain's terms persist within the
item.

**Identity signals:**
- Otherworldly aesthetic (organic, organic-shifted, music-like)
- Sometimes appears to be made of impossible materials (moonlight,
  song, dream)
- Often carries an unfulfilled or unresolved bargain

**Typical items:** Wondrous items, potions, rings, items that
operate on dream / time / memory logic.

**Lore template:**
> Bargained from [Fey court / specific Fey patron] in exchange for
> [promise / gift]. The bargain's terms still bind the item — anyone
> who possesses it owes [obligation]. The Fey side of the bargain
> [is fulfilled / awaits fulfillment / has been forgotten by the
> Fey].

**Plot hook anchor:** The Fey patron returns to collect; the wielder
inherits the obligation; the bargain has a hidden cost yet to
manifest.

### 1.7 — The Cosmic / Planar Touch

Created in or by contact with another plane. Carries the imprint of
that plane's nature.

**Identity signals:**
- Material or color that doesn't fit the material plane
- Properties echo the plane of origin (Shadowfell items absorb light;
  Elysium items radiate calm; Mechanus items behave with cold logic)
- May be unstable in environments not matching the plane

**Typical items:** Wondrous items, ring, rod, sometimes specialty
weapons.

**Lore template:**
> Forged in [plane of origin] by [beings of that plane], the item
> retains [planar property]. It passed to the material plane through
> [specific incursion / planar bargain / ancient travel]. In its
> presence, [environmental anomaly] occurs.

**Plot hook anchor:** Beings of that plane seek to reclaim it;
prolonged exposure shifts the wielder toward that plane's alignment;
the item is a planar key.

### 1.8 — The Elemental Embodiment

Carved or fused from a source of pure elemental matter (fire-eternal
forge, water from the deep, stone of the world-root, lightning from
the storm-eternal).

**Identity signals:**
- Constant minor elemental effect (warm to touch, never freezes,
  faintly hums)
- Connection to a specific elemental site
- Resists or amplifies one element

**Typical items:** Weapons (fire-tempered blade), armor (stone-
forged plate), wondrous items (frost cloak, storm cloak).

**Lore template:**
> Forged at [elemental site] using [primary element]. The item
> retains its connection to [element] and is at its strongest in
> conditions of [element-rich environment].

**Plot hook anchor:** The elemental site is threatened; an elemental
being seeks to reclaim its substance; the item amplifies in specific
environments.

### 1.9 — The Inherited Mantle

The item passes through a lineage — family, order, role. Each owner
adds something or removes something.

**Identity signals:**
- Layered marks of past owners (engravings, modifications, repairs)
- Often part of a recognized lineage that observers can identify
- Sometimes the lineage's expectations weigh on the new wielder

**Typical items:** Weapons (family blades), armor (heritage plate),
heirloom rings, signet items.

**Lore template:**
> The item has passed through [N] generations of [lineage / order /
> role]. Each holder added their mark: [first owner's contribution,
> second owner's contribution, etc.]. The current state reflects the
> accumulated history.

**Plot hook anchor:** Lineage members claim the right to the item;
the lineage has unfinished business with a rival lineage; the
current heir was not the intended one.

### 1.10 — The Cult / Heretical Object

Created or consecrated by a cult, often invoking forbidden powers.
Carries the cult's purpose embedded in its function.

**Identity signals:**
- Forbidden or proscribed iconography
- Often hidden or disguised when not in use
- Wielded openly only by cult members or those who don't recognize
  the symbols

**Typical items:** Daggers, signet rings, ceremonial blades, ritual
implements, dark relics.

**Lore template:**
> The cult of [forbidden entity / heretical doctrine] crafted this
> item to [serve their purpose]. It bears the marks of [cult
> iconography], hidden to outsiders but obvious to initiates.
> Possession by an outsider is [punishable / accidentally meaningful
> / sought after].

**Plot hook anchor:** The cult wants the item back or wants to
prevent its understanding; an inquisitor or rival faith hunts the
item; the item's properties reveal cult secrets.

### 1.11 — The Lost Civilization

Created by a civilization no longer extant. Its mechanics may not
fully fit modern magical understanding, and its full capabilities
may be undiscovered.

**Identity signals:**
- Materials or craftsmanship inconsistent with current standards
- Inscriptions in dead or untranslatable scripts
- Properties that seem to require unknown activation rituals

**Typical items:** Anything — the lost civilization touched all
categories.

**Lore template:**
> Created during the height of [lost civilization] in [historical
> era]. The civilization's mages worked in traditions now lost,
> producing items with [signature property]. Modern scholars can
> identify [recognized features] but [unknown features] remain a
> mystery.

**Plot hook anchor:** Other items from the same civilization exist;
scholars compete to study or claim the item; the civilization's
fate matters (was it cursed? consumed? did it ascend?).

## 2. Journey Patterns

How the item moved from its origin to the current moment.

### Pattern A — The Direct Line

The item passed in an unbroken chain from maker to current state.
Each hand is documented.

**Use when:** the lineage matters for the story.

### Pattern B — The Lost and Recovered

The item was lost (buried, hidden, dropped, abandoned) for a span,
then recovered. Sometimes intentional (a vault), sometimes accidental
(buried in a battle).

**Use when:** the item was *meant* to be found at this moment.

### Pattern C — The Smuggled / Hidden Throughout

The item passed through dangerous hands — smugglers, rebels,
secret-keepers. Its journey is one of risk.

**Use when:** the journey is part of the story; the item is
politically charged.

### Pattern D — The Stolen and Re-stolen

The item changed hands violently. Each loss leaves marks; each
recovery is bloody.

**Use when:** the item is contested; ownership is unsettled.

### Pattern E — The Gift Exchange

The item was given as a gift, sometimes multiple times. The
obligations of gift-giving (debt, bond, alliance) accumulate.

**Use when:** social weight matters; the recipient is bound by the
gift's nature.

### Pattern F — The Accidental Discovery

The item was forgotten by all owners and was found by happenstance
— a tomb-robber, an archaeologist, a curious child.

**Use when:** the discoverer's identity matters for the campaign.

### Pattern G — The Calling

The item *chose* its wielders, manifesting in their hands at
specific moments. Each calling fits a pattern.

**Use when:** the item has minor sentience; the calling is the hook.

## 3. Current State Options

Where the item is *right now* matters as much as its history.

### State 1 — Intact and Functional

The item works as designed. Recently maintained, perhaps recently
used.

### State 2 — Damaged

The item works but with a reduced effect or limitation. A
restoration plot exists.

### State 3 — Drained / Dormant

The item's charges are gone, or its magic has gone quiet. It awaits
re-enchantment, a specific event, or a specific wielder.

### State 4 — Partial

The item is part of a set; the rest is missing. Each piece works
alone but the set provides a bonus.

### State 5 — Awakening

The item has been dormant and is showing signs of returning. The
moment of return is imminent.

### State 6 — Bound / Guarded

The item is in the possession of a creature or place that defends
it. Acquisition requires confrontation, bargain, or theft.

### State 7 — Misidentified

The item is being treated as something else. Its true nature is not
known by the current holder.

### State 8 — Cursed Twin

The item exists in two forms: one is the "right" item, the other a
shadow / counterpart / inversion. Often the protagonist holds the
wrong one without knowing.

## 4. Cultural Significance Tiers

How much the wider world knows about the item.

### Tier 1 — Unknown

No one currently alive knows the item exists. Its discovery is a
revelation.

### Tier 2 — Local Knowledge

A specific community knows of the item (a village, a guild, a
single noble house). Outside that, it's invisible.

### Tier 3 — Faction-Known

One or more factions know of the item, often with conflicting
interpretations. The item is "discussed" but not commonly seen.

### Tier 4 — Regional Legend

Most adventurers in the region know of the item by reputation. Its
discovery is news.

### Tier 5 — Global Legend

The item is named in stories told across many lands. Holding it
brings instant recognition (and consequences).

## 5. Plot Hook Library

Five categories of plot hooks. Each item should have at least one
hook from at least two categories.

### 5.1 — The Bounty / Recovery Hook

Someone wants the item retrieved.

**Patterns:**
- A wealthy patron commissions the party to find the item.
- A faction offers a bounty on the item's current holder.
- The item was stolen recently; the original owner seeks it back.
- A magical compulsion drives the party toward the item.

**Stakes:** payment, faction favor, the patron's wrath if they fail.

### 5.2 — The Maker-Tied Hook

The item's maker (or their successor) has unfinished business.

**Patterns:**
- The maker is alive and wants to inspect or modify the item.
- The maker's apprentice claims rights to the item.
- The maker's enemy seeks to destroy the item to harm the maker
  posthumously.
- The maker's order has internal politics around the item.

**Stakes:** the maker's protection, anger, or aid; learning the
item's true history; modifying the item.

### 5.3 — The Rival-Claim Hook

Multiple parties want the item.

**Patterns:**
- Two factions both claim historical ownership; the party is asked
  to choose.
- A criminal organization wants the item for resale.
- A rival adventuring party arrives at the same site with the same
  goal.
- An auction is announced; the party must outbid or otherwise
  acquire.

**Stakes:** political fallout, violence, lost reputation if they
choose poorly.

### 5.4 — The Item-Demands Hook

The item itself imposes a cost or task.

**Patterns:**
- The item only works for those who complete a specific quest.
- The item demands a sacrifice (gold, blood, an oath) per use.
- The item's bargain remains incomplete; the holder inherits the
  debt.
- The item shifts the wielder's alignment over time.

**Stakes:** moral choices, mounting costs, the wielder's identity.

### 5.5 — The Item-Attracts-Trouble Hook

Carrying the item draws specific threats.

**Patterns:**
- Specific creatures or factions are drawn to the item's magical
  signature.
- The item's regional effects (for Legendary items) attract attention.
- The item is being tracked by a scrying network.
- The item's presence triggers ancient wardings; the wielder is
  marked as a target.

**Stakes:** ongoing combat, surveillance, decisions about whether to
keep carrying the item.

## 6. Combining Lore Elements

A complete lore section combines:

- One **Maker Category** (Section 1)
- One **Journey Pattern** (Section 2)
- One **Current State** (Section 3)
- One **Cultural Significance Tier** (Section 4)
- 2–3 **Plot Hooks** from different categories (Section 5)

### Example combination

**Item:** Verglas, Shard of the Drowned Pact (Frost greatsword, Very Rare)

- **Maker:** The Monster-Bound — forged from the fang of a frost
  wyrm slain in the Saltbound War, by a smith named Khaela.
- **Journey:** The Stolen and Re-stolen — fought over by three
  noble houses across two centuries, last "owned" by Lord Aravell
  who lost it to bandits 80 years ago.
- **Current State:** Damaged — one of the runes on the blade was
  scratched out during the last theft; the sword's full power is
  inaccessible until the rune is restored.
- **Cultural Significance:** Regional Legend — sailors and coastal
  villagers know the name; mainland courts have forgotten.
- **Plot Hooks:**
  - Bounty: Lord Aravell's grandson has set a bounty for the
    blade's return.
  - Maker-Tied: Khaela's descendants still operate the forge
    where the blade was made; they can restore the rune.
  - Item-Attracts-Trouble: the wyrm's frozen-pact still binds; any
    direct descendant of the Saltbound War's villain is drawn to
    the blade.

These three hooks could each generate a session or arc.
