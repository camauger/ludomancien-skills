# Item Categories — D&D 5e 2024

The eight standard magic-item categories with their format conventions,
sub-types, naming patterns, balance considerations, common pitfalls,
and FR vocabulary for stat-block labels.

---

## 1. Armor

Armor magic items modify defensive properties: AC bonus, save bonuses,
damage resistance, or special protective effects.

### Format

```
Armor (Light, Medium, Heavy, or Shield), [Rarity] (Requires Attunement)
```

Sub-type matters mechanically — Light armor allows Dex bonus stacking;
Medium caps Dex at +2; Heavy ignores Dex; Shields stack with armor.

### Sub-types

| Sub-type | Examples | Typical user |
|---|---|---|
| Padded, Leather, Studded Leather | Light armor items | Rogues, monks, bards, sorcerers |
| Hide, Chain Shirt, Scale Mail, Breastplate, Half Plate | Medium armor items | Druids (non-metal), rangers, paladins |
| Ring Mail, Chain Mail, Splint, Plate | Heavy armor items | Fighters, paladins |
| Shield | Shield-only items | Any martial class |
| Robe (technically not armor) | Wizards' robes treated as armor-class items | Wizards, sorcerers |

### Property patterns

- **Static AC bonus** (+1 / +2 / +3 by rarity)
- **Reaction triggers** (advantage on a save once per day, deflect
  a critical hit once per long rest)
- **Damage resistance** (one or more damage types)
- **Movement effects** (no disadvantage on Stealth from heavy armor;
  ignore difficult terrain)
- **Spell-like protective effects** (Sanctuary 1/day, Shield as a
  reaction with a charge cost)

### Naming patterns

- *Armor of [Element]* — Armor of Resistance to fire/cold/lightning
- *[Material] [Armor Type]* — Adamantine Half Plate
- *[Owner / Origin] [Armor Type]* — Plate of the Defender, Vestments
  of the Bone Throne

### Balance considerations

- **+1 to +3 AC is a HUGE bonus.** A +3 plate raises AC by 3 (from 18
  to 21) — that's ~15% miss chance shift in combat.
- Don't stack AC bonuses with other defensive stacking (resistance
  + AC + dodge feature) without scaling the rarity accordingly.
- Heavy armor has the highest base AC (18) and reaches AC 23 with
  shield + magic. That's already near-untouchable; further bonuses
  push into Legendary territory.
- Light armor items are *better* than they look because the user can
  add full Dex bonus.

### Common pitfalls

- "+1 Plate" feels weaker than "+1 Studded Leather" to many players
  because their AC is already capped. Tier the rarity accordingly.
- Adding resistance to many damage types in one item — pick 1–2 types
  for Rare, 2–3 for Very Rare, 3+ for Legendary.
- Forgetting that magical armor's bonus stacks with all natural Dex
  bonuses for light armor.

### FR vocabulary

- *Armor* → *Armure*
- *Light Armor* → *Armure légère*
- *Medium Armor* → *Armure intermédiaire*
- *Heavy Armor* → *Armure lourde*
- *Shield* → *Bouclier*

## 2. Weapons

Weapon magic items modify offensive properties: attack and damage bonuses,
special damage riders, conditional triggers, signature abilities.

### Format

```
Weapon (any [type] or specific weapon name), [Rarity] (Requires Attunement)
```

### Sub-types

| Sub-type | Examples | Property focus |
|---|---|---|
| Simple weapons | Quarterstaff, club, mace | Often utility-focused, not pure damage |
| Martial weapons | Longsword, greatsword, longbow, rapier | Standard combat focus |
| Ranged weapons | Bow, crossbow, throwing | Range-specific properties |
| Special (versatile) | Spear, quarterstaff (both 1H and 2H) | Often paired with class-flexible properties |

### Property patterns

- **Static bonuses** (+1 / +2 / +3 to attack and damage)
- **Damage type riders** (+ Xd6 fire / cold / lightning on hit)
- **Conditional bonus** (advantage on attacks against specific
  creatures; bonus damage in dim light)
- **Critical hit modification** (critical hits ignore resistance;
  critical hits deal max damage; expanded crit range)
- **Spell-storing** (held charge of one spell, releases on hit)
- **Riposte / reaction triggers**
- **Signature ability** (Recharge-based or per-day): e.g., a
  weapon's "Word of Banishment" 1/day

### Naming patterns

- *[Weapon Type] of [Element / Bane]* — Greataxe of Cold, Longbow of
  Demonbane
- *[Distinctive Name], [Optional Subtitle]* — Verglas, Eyebreaker
- *[Material] [Weapon Type]* — Adamantine Greatsword, Silvered Longbow

### Balance considerations

- **+1 weapon = significant.** Adds ~5% hit chance + 1 damage per hit.
  In a 3-hit-per-round multi-attacker, that's ~+5% damage per round.
- **+3 weapon = enormous.** ~15% hit chance + 3 damage per hit. Major
  combat shift.
- **Bonus damage dice (+1d6 fire, etc.)** — these are powerful but
  conditional on type. Don't stack 3 damage rider effects on a Rare
  weapon.
- **Critical hit modifications** are subtle but powerful. Expanded
  crit range (19–20 instead of 20) is roughly equivalent to a +5%
  damage bonus.
- **Spell-storing weapons** are limited but powerful. Treat the
  stored spell's slot-cost as the balance anchor.

### Common pitfalls

- Stacking attack bonus + damage rider + conditional bonus on a Rare
  weapon — that's Very Rare territory.
- Forgetting that some classes (e.g., paladins with Smite) already
  do big damage; a +1d6 rider effectively doubles their per-hit
  damage.
- Underestimating the "always-on" power of even a +1 weapon over
  the campaign's lifetime.

### FR vocabulary

- *Weapon* → *Arme*
- *Simple Weapon* → *Arme courante*
- *Martial Weapon* → *Arme de guerre*
- *Ranged Weapon* → *Arme à distance*

## 3. Potions

Potions are single-use consumables. They occupy no slot and don't
require attunement. They are typically the lowest-rarity magic items.

### Format

```
Potion, [Rarity]
```

(Potions never require attunement.)

### Sub-types

| Sub-type | Effect pattern |
|---|---|
| Healing | Restore HP |
| Resistance | Damage resistance for 1 hour |
| Enhancement | Ability score boost for limited time |
| Movement | Speed, flying, swimming |
| Sensing | Detect magic, detect thoughts, true seeing |
| Polymorph | Animal forms, gaseous form |
| Antidote | Cure poison, cure disease |
| Greater versions | Higher dice, longer duration |

### Property patterns

- **Single-use** (consumed when drunk or applied)
- **Duration-limited effect** (1 hour, 10 minutes, until next short
  rest)
- **Stat boost** (gain temporary HP, advantage on saves, +1d4 to
  attacks)
- **Spell-as-potion** (drinking the potion casts a 2nd or 3rd level
  spell on yourself)
- **Greater tier scaling** (Potion of Healing → Greater → Superior
  → Supreme, each tier doubles HP)

### Naming patterns

- *Potion of [Effect]* — Potion of Climbing, Potion of Fire Resistance
- *Potion of [Effect] (Greater / Superior / Supreme)* — Potion of
  Healing (Greater)
- *[Custom Brewery Name] [Effect]* — Lostbottle Tonic (custom blend
  for haunted-house themed adventures)

### Balance considerations

- **Healing potions** are the most common; their scaling defines the
  baseline:
  - Common (Potion of Healing): 2d4+2 (avg 7)
  - Uncommon (Greater): 4d4+4 (avg 14)
  - Rare (Superior): 8d4+8 (avg 28)
  - Very Rare (Supreme): 10d4+20 (avg 45)
- **Buffs** typically last 1 hour and provide a notable but
  bounded effect.
- **Single-use** is the constraint — don't stack 3+ effects on a
  potion or it becomes a Very Rare swiss army knife.

### Common pitfalls

- Making potions too powerful — a single-use Very Rare potion that
  grants 8th-level spell access for 1 hour is genuinely a Legendary
  effect.
- Forgetting that potions can be saved for the critical moment
  — they're more powerful than their rarity suggests because they're
  used at the right moment.

### FR vocabulary

- *Potion* → *Potion*
- *Greater* → *Supérieure*
- *Superior* → *Suprême*

## 4. Rings

Rings occupy a slot (max 2 rings per character) and almost always
require attunement. They are typically Uncommon to Legendary; rare
to find a Common ring.

### Format

```
Ring, [Rarity] (Requires Attunement)
```

### Property patterns

- **Static ability score / save bonus** (Ring of Protection: +1 to AC
  and saves)
- **Resistance** (Ring of Resistance: one damage type)
- **Spell-storing** (Ring of Spell Storing: 1d10 spell levels)
- **Spell-like ability** (Ring of Invisibility, Ring of X-Ray Vision)
- **Movement / sense** (Ring of Free Action, Ring of Water Walking,
  Ring of Truth)
- **Charge-based** (Ring of Three Wishes — Legendary)

### Naming patterns

- *Ring of [Effect]* — Ring of Invisibility, Ring of Spell Storing
- *Ring of [Element/Type]* — Ring of Cold Resistance, Ring of Demon Bane
- *[Distinctive] Ring* — Ironbond Ring, Knotted Ring

### Balance considerations

- **Ring slot is contested.** A character has max 2 ring slots and
  many Rare+ items want this slot. Be sparing with rings; each one
  must earn its slot.
- **Ring of Protection** (+1 AC + saves) is a benchmark — anything
  more powerful is jumping a rarity tier.
- **Spell-storing rings** are powerful because the stored spell can
  be cast as a reaction or out-of-combat, bypassing normal slot
  economies.

### Common pitfalls

- Making rings too easy to wear simultaneously — players often double
  up on rings for stacked bonuses.
- Forgetting that ring slot scarcity is its own design challenge.

### FR vocabulary

- *Ring* → *Anneau*

## 5. Rods

Rods are short, sturdy implements used by clerical, divine, or
authority figures. They occupy the same slot category as wands and
staves (held implement) but feel different — more solid, more weighty.

### Format

```
Rod, [Rarity] (Requires Attunement [optional])
```

### Property patterns

- **Channel divinity / clerical effect** — Rod of Censure
- **Authority / command** — Rod of Rulership
- **Binding / restraint** — Rod of Binding
- **Spellcasting tool** — Rod of the Pact Keeper (warlock-specific)
- **Recall / dimensional movement** — Rod of Lordly Might (multi-form)
- **Charge-based** with daily recharge

### Naming patterns

- *Rod of [Function]* — Rod of Rulership, Rod of Lordly Might
- *Rod of the [Role]* — Rod of the Pact Keeper, Rod of the High Priest

### Balance considerations

- Rods tend to be class-specific or role-specific (warlock,
  cleric, druid). The prerequisite sharpens the item identity.
- Rods are usually Rare+ — there are few Common or Uncommon rods.

### FR vocabulary

- *Rod* → *Sceptre*

## 6. Scrolls

Scrolls are single-use spell containers. Reading the scroll casts the
spell once, then the scroll is destroyed (consumed). No attunement.

### Format

```
Scroll of [Spell Name], [Rarity]
```

Rarity tracks the spell level:

| Spell level | Scroll rarity |
|---|---|
| Cantrip / 1st | Common / Uncommon |
| 2nd / 3rd | Uncommon / Rare |
| 4th / 5th | Rare |
| 6th / 7th | Very Rare |
| 8th / 9th | Very Rare / Legendary |

### Property patterns

- **Spell-on-scroll:** the scroll contains a specific spell. Reading
  it (action) casts the spell. Spell is lost / scroll consumed.
- **Caster restrictions:** the spell must be on the user's class
  spell list, OR the user must succeed on an Arcana check (DC =
  10 + spell level)
- **Material components:** the scroll provides any material
  components needed (usually no gold cost incurred)

### Naming patterns

- *Scroll of [Spell Name]* — Scroll of Fireball, Scroll of Polymorph

### Balance considerations

- A scroll equals one casting of the spell at the spell's normal level.
- Higher-rarity scrolls scale the spell's power — a 9th-level Wish
  scroll is Legendary because of the spell's breadth.
- Scrolls are often consumables given as quest rewards.

### Common pitfalls

- Designing "scroll of X" but X is a custom spell — make sure the
  custom spell is balanced first.

### FR vocabulary

- *Scroll* → *Parchemin*

## 7. Staves

Staves are wizard-archetype implements, often two-handed, often
spellcasting-focused. They occupy the held-implement slot.

### Format

```
Staff, [Rarity] (Requires Attunement [by a spellcaster])
```

### Property patterns

- **Spellcasting tool** (replaces arcane focus; functions as
  quarterstaff in melee)
- **Spell-storing with charges** (5–10 charges typical, regain
  1d6+4 daily at dawn)
- **Specific spell library** (the staff "knows" specific spells the
  user can cast by expending charges)
- **Defensive bonus** (Staff of the Magi grants +2 AC to wielder)
- **Recall / dimensional / portal** ability

### Naming patterns

- *Staff of [Theme/Element]* — Staff of Frost, Staff of Charming
- *Staff of the [Archetype]* — Staff of the Magi, Staff of Striking
- *[Distinctive] Staff* — Knotted Staff, Bone Staff of Loharn

### Balance considerations

- A staff with charges + spell library is a *significant* power
  source. Rare+ usually.
- **Staff of the Magi** is the iconic Legendary — sets the high-end
  expectation.
- Wizards and warlocks already have arcane foci; adding a magic
  staff stacks on existing power sources.

### Common pitfalls

- Designing staves with too-many-charges-and-too-many-spells. Pick
  a focused spell list (3–5 spells) and a reasonable charge count.

### FR vocabulary

- *Staff* → *Bâton*

---

## 8. Wands

Wands are smaller, lighter implements than staves. They focus on
ranged effects, single spells, or quick utility.

### Format

```
Wand, [Rarity] (Requires Attunement [optional])
```

### Property patterns

- **Spell-storing with charges** (7 charges typical, regain 1d6+1 at
  dawn)
- **Single-spell focus** (Wand of Fireball: casts fireball using
  charges)
- **Detection / utility** (Wand of Magic Missiles, Wand of Detection
  of Magic, Wand of Identify)
- **Recharge crisis** (when the last charge is used, roll d20 — on
  a 1, the wand crumbles)

### Naming patterns

- *Wand of [Spell Effect]* — Wand of Magic Missiles, Wand of Lightning
  Bolts
- *Wand of [Theme]* — Wand of Wonder, Wand of Polymorph
- *[Material] Wand* — Yew Wand, Bone Wand

### Balance considerations

- Wands are the "consumable-with-recharge" of magic items. Charges +
  daily recharge gives sustainability with a cap.
- The "crumble on 1" mechanic is the standard cost mechanism for the
  last charge.

### Common pitfalls

- Wands designed without the crumble mechanic feel less risky to
  use, breaking the design intent.
- Stacking too many spells on a wand — pick 1–3 effects.

### FR vocabulary

- *Wand* → *Baguette*

## 9. Wondrous Items

The catch-all category. Everything that isn't Armor, Weapon, Potion,
Ring, Rod, Scroll, Staff, or Wand is Wondrous: cloaks, boots, helms,
gloves, amulets, bags of holding, marvelous pigments, etc.

### Format

```
Wondrous Item, [Rarity] (Requires Attunement [optional])
```

### Property patterns (huge variety)

- **Body-slot items** (boots, gloves, cloaks, helms) — see
  `rarity-and-attunement-guide.md` Section 5 for slot conventions
- **Pocket items** (Bag of Holding, Wind Fan, Folding Boat)
- **Tools / utilities** (Marvelous Pigments paint real objects)
- **Sense-grants** (Goggles of Night, Hat of Disguise, Stone of
  Good Luck)
- **Movement-grants** (Boots of Speed, Winged Boots, Cloak of the
  Bat)
- **Charge-based wondrous items** (Necklace of Fireballs, Stone of
  Controlling Earth Elementals)

### Naming patterns

- *[Item Type] of [Effect]* — Bag of Holding, Cloak of Elvenkind,
  Boots of Speed
- *[Item Type] of the [Type/Character]* — Cloak of the Bat, Robe of
  the Archmagi, Mantle of the Champion
- *[Distinctive] [Item Type]* — Stone of Good Luck, Wind Fan

### Balance considerations

- Wondrous items vary wildly in power. A Common Wondrous Item might
  be a candle that doesn't go out; a Legendary one (Ioun Stones,
  Robe of the Archmagi) is identity-defining.
- Body-slot rules (informal) constrain stacking — design accordingly.
- Some Wondrous Items grant *spell-like abilities* (the Cloak of the
  Bat lets the wearer cast polymorph as a bat); these are
  significant.

### Common pitfalls

- Wondrous Items are easy to make *too utility-focused* and lose
  combat presence. Mix combat and utility per rarity tier.
- Forgetting slot conventions when designing multiple Wondrous Items
  for a campaign — players can't wear 4 Cloaks.

### FR vocabulary

- *Wondrous Item* → *Objet merveilleux*
- *Boots* → *Bottes*
- *Cloak* → *Cape* or *Manteau*
- *Gloves* → *Gants*
- *Helm* → *Heaume*
- *Bag* → *Sac*
- *Hat* → *Chapeau*

## 10. Category Selection Workflow

When designing a new item, the category usually flows from the
concept:

| Concept | Most likely category |
|---|---|
| "An armor that..." | Armor |
| "A sword / axe / bow / weapon that..." | Weapons |
| "Something you drink" | Potions |
| "Something you wear on your hand" | Rings |
| "A symbol of authority / divine office" | Rods |
| "Something you read once and it's gone" | Scrolls |
| "A wizard's implement / two-handed magical staff" | Staves |
| "A focused single-effect wand" | Wands |
| "Something you wear that isn't armor or a ring, OR a pocket item" | Wondrous Items |

When a concept fits multiple categories (e.g., a wand vs a staff),
choose by:

- **Size:** wands are smaller, staves are full-size implements
- **Power level:** staves carry more, wands are focused
- **User class:** wands are often for any caster; staves are wizard-
  archetype
- **Visual:** wand = ornate stick; staff = walking-staff-or-larger
