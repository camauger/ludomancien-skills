# Portrayal Framework

Guide for creating actionable GM roleplay guidance. Good portrayal cues turn stat blocks into living characters.

## Essential Elements

Every significant NPC (Tier 2+) should include:

1. **Voice/Manner** - How they sound and move
2. **Physical Tells** - Unconscious behaviors that reveal character
3. **Entrance Line** - Memorable first impression dialogue
4. **Comfort vs. Threat Behaviors** - How they shift between states
5. **Verbal Tics/Patterns** - Speech patterns that make them distinctive
6. **GM Tips** - Practical advice for running them at the table

---

## Voice/Manner

**What to specify:**
- Voice quality (pitch, volume, pace, accent)
- Movement style (how they occupy space)
- Energy level (languid, frantic, measured)
- Distinctive speech patterns

**Examples:**

**Confident Authority:**
> Cadence of prayer even in casual conversation—rhythmic, intentional, 
> weighted with meaning. Warm mezzo-soprano voice, moderate volume that 
> invites rather than commands, measured pacing that gives weight to each 
> word. Moves with calm purpose, as though every action is sacred.

**Nervous Academic:**
> Speaks quickly, words tumbling over each other when excited about a 
> topic. High tenor voice that cracks when stressed. Constantly adjusting 
> glasses even when they don't need adjusting. Gestures wildly with hands, 
> knocking things over accidentally.

**Dangerous Calm:**
> Speaks softly—paradoxically harder to hear but more intense. Never raises 
> voice; the quieter they get, the more dangerous. Moves economically, no 
> wasted motion. Makes direct eye contact that feels like being assessed 
> for weaknesses.

**Seductive Warmth:**
> Voice like honey and smoke, naturally falling into lower register that 
> carries intimacy. Speaks slowly, giving each word time to land. Moves 
> with fluid grace, unconsciously sensual without performing seduction. 
> Maintains physical closeness—leans in, touches arms/shoulders casually.

## Physical Tells

**Definition:** Unconscious behaviors that reveal emotional state or character. These give GMs concrete actions to describe.

**Categories:**

### Stress/Anxiety Tells
- Touching specific body parts (throat, wrists, hair)
- Fidgeting with objects (rings, pendants, cuffs)
- Breathing changes (shallow, held, sighing)
- Posture shifts (hunching, straightening, leaning away)

### Emotional Tells
- Eyes closing when listening deeply
- Jaw clenching when angry but controlled
- Hands trembling when afraid but trying to hide it
- Tail movements (for non-human NPCs)
- Wing rustling, ear flattening, etc.

### Professional/Habitual Tells
- Blessing gestures from former priest
- Unconscious combat stances from soldier
- Counting on fingers from accountant
- Ink stains from writer
- Calluses from specific trades

**Example Set (for complex character):**

> - Makes blessing gestures unconsciously (4-5 times per conversation)
> - Touches holy symbols when stressed (reaches for them every 2-3 minutes 
>   during difficult discussions)
> - Speaks with hands—large, expressive gestures
> - Maintains open posture—arms uncrossed, body language welcoming
> - Closes eyes briefly when praying (5-6 times per hour mid-conversation)
> - Smiles sadly when discussing institutional religion
> - Tail droops when speaking of former temple

## Entrance Lines

**Purpose:** Memorable first words that capture character essence and create immediate impression.

**Structure:** Should reveal:
- Core belief or value
- Distinctive voice
- Hook for interaction
- Character's immediate relevance

**Examples by Archetype:**

**Spiritual Guide:**
> "Your body is already holy—not as it should be, but as it is right now, 
> with all its desires. The divine made you this way intentionally. What 
> if you're already blessed?"

**Information Broker:**
> "Everyone in this city is selling something. The honest ones admit what 
> it is. The question you should be asking: what am I buying with this 
> conversation?"

**Corrupt Official:**
> "The law is very clear about this matter. Of course, the law is also 
> very flexible when proper considerations are made. Shall we discuss what 
> considerations might apply to your situation?"

**Desperate Ally:**
> "You don't know me, but I know what you did at the cathedral. I need 
> someone with your particular talents and your particular disregard for 
> certain laws. Can we talk somewhere the Watch won't overhear?"

**Mysterious Patron:**
> "I've been watching you. Don't be alarmed—I watch everyone who shows 
> promise. The question is whether you're ready to understand what that 
> promise might become."

## Comfort vs. Threat Behaviors

**Purpose:** Show how NPC shifts between states, giving GMs guidance for different situations.

**Structure:**

### When Comfortable
- Personality softens/expands
- Professional mask drops
- Shows vulnerability or humor
- Becomes more genuine/less performative

### When Threatened
- Specific defense mechanism activates
- Voice/posture changes
- Particular strategies emerge
- Core values become non-negotiable

**Example:**

> **When Comfortable:** The priestly bearing remains but becomes less 
> formal—she'll joke about religious hypocrisy, swear occasionally, show 
> frustration with institutions. She's more likely to admit doubt, to 
> question her own theology. She becomes peer rather than counselor.
>
> **When Threatened:** Doesn't lose composure but becomes immovable—polite 
> absolute refusal that brooks no argument. Will cite religious principle, 
> defend her theology with passionate intensity. May pray aloud when truly 
> frightened, finding comfort in ritual.

## Verbal Tics/Patterns

**Definition:** Distinctive speech patterns that make NPC immediately recognizable and easy to voice.

**Types:**

### Sentence Starters
- "The thing is..."
- "Listen..."
- "Tell me about..."
- "The divine says..."
- "In my experience..."

### Catch Phrases
- Used as reassurance: "You're not alone in this"
- As greeting/farewell: "Blessed be"
- As deflection: "That's not my concern"
- As emphasis: "Mark my words"

### Speech Patterns
- Frames statements as questions: "It sounds like you're angry?"
- Starts with one idea, circles back: "I was thinking—no, wait, what I mean is..."
- Speaks in threes: "Faith, hope, and courage"
- Uses specific metaphors: "Like trying to hold water"

### Professional Jargon
- Priest: "Sacred," "holy," "blessed," "profane"
- Merchant: "Value," "cost," "investment," "profit"
- Criminal: "Heat," "mark," "score," "clean"
- Scholar: "Theoretically," "evidence suggests," "one could posit"

**Example Set:**

> - Begins statements with "The divine..." or "My god..."
> - Says "Blessed be" as greeting and farewell
> - Frames things as "sacred" or "holy" frequently
> - Asks "What does your soul need?" instead of "What do you want?"
> - Uses "Gift" for things others call curses or burdens
> - Says "You are beloved" as reassurance

## GM Tips Section

**Purpose:** Practical advice for running the NPC at the table.

**Include:**

### Pacing Guidance
- When to pause for effect
- How fast/slow to speak
- When to let silence work

### Emphasis Patterns
- What to stress verbally
- What to describe physically
- When to use specific tells

### Scene-Specific Guidance
- How NPC behaves in combat
- Social interaction defaults
- Unique situation handling

### Red Flags / Things to Avoid
- Common GM mistakes with this NPC
- What breaks character
- Temptations to resist

**Example:**

> ### GM Tips
> - Use religious language naturally, not as affectation
> - Show her praying at unexpected moments—it's reflexive
> - Demonstrate genuine faith, not just performance of it
> - Let her magic work despite excommunication (this matters theologically)
> - Show the cost of being heretic—grief, doubt, isolation
> - Balance absolute conviction with genuine humility
> - Reference her former church and the pain of rejection
> - Show how she's building new theology in real-time

## Quick Portrayal Templates

### The Mentor
- **Voice:** Calm, assured, teaches through questions
- **Tells:** Thoughtful pauses, hand gestures that guide
- **Entrance:** "I've been expecting you. Come, let's discuss why."
- **Tic:** "What does that teach you?" / "Consider..."

### The Rival
- **Voice:** Sharp, competitive, backhanded compliments
- **Tells:** Smirks, crossed arms, evaluating glances
- **Entrance:** "Well, if it isn't [name]. Come to embarrass yourself again?"
- **Tic:** "Interesting choice" (said dismissively)

### The Innocent
- **Voice:** Earnest, quick, no guile
- **Tells:** Wide eyes, open posture, talks with hands
- **Entrance:** "Oh thank goodness! You're here to help, right?"
- **Tic:** "I just thought..." / "Is that okay?"

### The Veteran
- **Voice:** Gruff, economical, seen too much
- **Tells:** Scars, checks exits, hand near weapon
- **Entrance:** "You've got that look. What's the job?"
- **Tic:** "In my day..." / "Kid, let me tell you..."

### The Schemer
- **Voice:** Smooth, measured, says one thing means another
- **Tells:** Calculated smiles, steepled fingers, never rushes
- **Entrance:** "How convenient. I was just thinking we should talk."
- **Tic:** "Between you and me..." / "Hypothetically..."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Over-describing:** Don't narrate every micro-expression. Pick 2-3 distinctive tells and use them consistently.

**Voice acting pressure:** You don't need perfect accents. Consistent speech patterns matter more than "doing the voice."

**Forgetting under stress:** NPCs in combat should still show character. Quick one-line reactions maintain personality.

**Exposition dumping:** Use entrance lines to hook interest, not to explain entire backstory.

**Inconsistency:** If an NPC touches their throat when stressed, they do it EVERY time they're stressed. Consistency makes them memorable.

## Tiered Guidance

**Background NPC (Tier 1):**
- Just voice/manner
- Maybe 1 distinctive tic
- Skip the rest

**Significant NPC (Tier 2):**
- Voice/manner
- 2-3 physical tells
- Entrance line
- Basic verbal pattern
- Quick GM tip

**Major NPC (Tier 3):**
- Full portrayal suite
- Multiple state behaviors
- Rich verbal patterns
- Extensive GM guidance
- Examples of handling specific situations
