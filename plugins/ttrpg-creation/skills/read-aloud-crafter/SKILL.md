# Read-Aloud Crafter

Transform raw TTRPG game text into polished read-aloud narration optimized for table delivery.

## What This Skill Does

Convert source material of any polish level (bullet points, module text, database entries, rough notes) into atmospheric read-aloud text by:
1. Stripping all mechanical elements (DCs, skill checks, stat blocks, hidden secrets)
2. Adding appropriate sensory details for immersion
3. Applying chosen tone consistently
4. Structuring for oral delivery with natural pacing
5. Ending with engagement question to prompt player action

## Available Tones

- **Literary**: Poetic, layered, metaphoric—for noir, gothic, character-focused games
- **Pulpy**: Action-oriented, vivid, urgent—for adventure, fast-paced sessions
- **Epic**: Grand, mythic, elevated—for high fantasy, destiny-driven campaigns
- **Erotica**: Sensual, charged, intimate—for mature content, romance focus, tension-building
- **Mysterious**: Suggestive, ambiguous, elusive—for horror, investigation, Lovecraftian atmosphere
- **Terse**: Minimalist, sharp, efficient—for OSR, combat-focused, experienced players

See `references/tone-examples.md` for detailed examples of each tone applied to the same source material.

## Workflow

### 1. Receive Source Material

Accept any format:
- Published module descriptions
- Personal GM notes (bullet points, fragments)
- Database entries
- Mix of prose and mechanics

### 2. Confirm Transformation Parameters

Ask user (if not specified):
- **Tone**: Which of the six tones to apply
- **Reveal Level**: Full (exploration), Partial (investigation), or Minimal (action)
- **Length Preference**: Standard (3-4 paragraphs) or Brief (1-2 paragraphs)

### 3. Extract Core Information

Identify and preserve:
- Essential location/situation details players must know
- Atmospheric elements and mood indicators
- Key sensory anchors already present

Discard completely:
- All DCs, skill checks, saving throws
- NPC stat blocks and mechanical abilities  
- Secret information players shouldn't know yet
- Meta-commentary about game design
- Any reference to mechanics or stat blocks

### 4. Transform According to Tone

Follow detailed transformation workflow in `references/transformation-workflow.md`:
- Expand sensory details appropriate to tone
- Structure for oral delivery (varied sentence length, paragraph breaks)
- Apply point-of-view alignment (second person present)
- Maintain consistent tone throughout
- End with engagement question

### 5. Deliver Read-Aloud

Present transformed text in clean format:
- No mechanical elements visible
- Structured for easy reading aloud
- Clear paragraph breaks for pacing
- Engagement question prominent at end

## Key Principles

**Conciseness**: Read-alouds over 3-4 paragraphs cause player attention drift. Hit highlights, trust player imagination.

**Sensory Foundation**: Every read-aloud needs at least visual + one other sense (sound, smell, touch, taste).

**Oral Optimization**: Structure for speaking aloud—varied sentence length, natural comma placement, paragraph breaks as breathing space.

**Tone Consistency**: Once chosen, maintain tone throughout the read-aloud. Shifts happen between scenes, not during.

**Player Agency**: Always end with prompt that invites response and returns control to players.

## Usage Examples

**Input (raw notes)**:
```
Old library. Three floors. Books on forbidden magic. 
Librarian = Brother Cassius (secretly corrupt, Insight DC 15).
Restricted section locked (DC 17 Thieves' Tools).
Dusty, quiet, oppressive church vibes.
Hidden: records of entity's manifestation (Investigation DC 20).
```

**Output (Literary tone)**:
```
The library remembers when its books were read for salvation rather than secrets.

You enter a three-story chamber where silence has weight. Wooden shelves 
climb toward vaulted ceilings, packed with leather-bound volumes whose 
spines are cracked from centuries of faithful consultation—and recent, 
furtive examination. Dust dances in the light filtering through narrow 
windows, each mote catching like a question left unasked.

At the far end, a man in priest's robes organizes volumes with methodical 
precision. He doesn't look up when you enter, but his hands pause mid-shelving, 
just for a moment. Behind him, an iron gate blocks a section of shelves. 
The books beyond it are older, darker, bound in materials you'd rather not 
identify.

The air tastes of old paper and candle wax and something underneath—the 
metallic tang of secrets kept too long.

What draws your attention first?
```

## Common Requests

**"Make it shorter"**: Reduce to 1-2 paragraphs, focus on essential details only
**"Make it more atmospheric"**: Add additional sensory layer, lean into tone's characteristics
**"Less purple prose"**: Simplify language, reduce metaphor, move toward terse
**"Add specific detail"**: Incorporate user-provided element (NPC appearance, specific object)
**"Match my setting's style"**: Apply literary tone with specific setting vocabulary/themes
