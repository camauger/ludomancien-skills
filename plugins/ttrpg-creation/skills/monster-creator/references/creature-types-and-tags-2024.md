# Creature Types and Tags — D&D 5e 2024

The 14 official creature types of D&D 5e 2024, with their tag
conventions, type-specific implications, and the French vocabulary
for stat block labels.

---

## 1. The 14 Creature Types

Every monster has exactly one type, which determines how spells and
abilities interact with it (e.g., *hold person* affects only Humanoid;
*banishment* affects most types; *protection from evil and good* affects
Aberration, Celestial, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, and Undead).

### Aberration

Alien, otherworldly, mind-warping creatures. Often from beyond the
material plane (Far Realm).

- **Examples:** Aboleth, Beholder, Mind Flayer, Gibbering Mouther,
  Slaad
- **Typical alignment:** Lawful Evil or Chaotic Evil; some Neutral
- **Common traits:** Telepathy, Truesight, mental attacks (charm,
  fear, psychic damage), nonstandard anatomy
- **Common immunities:** Charmed (for some — they *are* the charmers),
  Frightened
- **Typical languages:** Deep Speech, Undercommon, telepathy
- **Design notes:** Aberrations should *feel wrong*. Avoid making them
  read as monsters-with-extra-eyes; they should challenge the players'
  assumptions about how the world works.

### Beast

Natural, mundane animals — even fantastical ones, as long as they're
non-magical and non-sentient at a humanoid level.

- **Examples:** Wolf, Dire Wolf, Owlbear, Giant Spider, Sabre-Tooth
  Tiger
- **Typical alignment:** Unaligned (almost always)
- **Common traits:** Keen Senses (Smell, Sight, or Hearing), Pack
  Tactics, Pounce, Charge
- **Languages:** Usually none (some understand a Druidic-related
  command)
- **Design notes:** Beasts are the foundation of low-CR encounters
  and wilderness threats. They should feel naturalistic; a Beast
  with spell-like abilities probably wants to be a Monstrosity or
  Fey instead.

### Celestial

Heavenly beings: angels, couatls, pegasi, devas. Native to upper planes.

- **Examples:** Couatl, Pegasus, Solar, Unicorn, Deva, Planetar
- **Typical alignment:** Lawful Good, Neutral Good, Chaotic Good
- **Common traits:** Resistance to radiant damage, magic resistance,
  innate spellcasting (good-aligned), telepathy, Flying
- **Common immunities:** Charmed, Frightened (for higher-CR
  Celestials), specific damage types (radiant immunity for some)
- **Languages:** Celestial, telepathy, all common languages for
  high-CR
- **Design notes:** Celestials are rarely random encounters. They
  appear with purpose. Even hostile Celestials (corrupted, in
  judgment) act with moral framing.

### Construct

Animated objects, automatons, golems, modrons. Built or created
rather than born.

- **Examples:** Animated Armor, Iron Golem, Shield Guardian, Modron,
  Homunculus
- **Typical alignment:** Unaligned (no will) or Lawful Neutral (modrons)
- **Common traits:** Immunity to poison and psychic damage; immunity
  to many conditions (charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed,
  petrified, poisoned); often vulnerable to fire or lightning
- **Languages:** Usually understand the creator's languages but
  don't speak
- **Design notes:** Constructs are not subject to attrition (no
  exhaustion, no need to sleep) but are vulnerable to creative
  player tactics (rust monster against iron, water against fire-
  imbued construct).

### Dragon

True dragons, dragon-related, and pseudo-dragons. Iconic monsters of
the genre.

- **Examples:** Red Dragon (chromatic), Gold Dragon (metallic),
  Pseudodragon, Faerie Dragon, Dragonborn (player type, also a
  Humanoid in stat block)
- **Typical alignment:** Chromatic Evil (Red/Blue/Green/Black/White),
  Metallic Good (Gold/Silver/Bronze/Brass/Copper), Gem Neutral
- **Common traits:** Breath Weapon (Recharge 5–6), Legendary Actions
  (Ancient and Adult dragons), Lair Actions, Flying, Frightful
  Presence
- **Languages:** Common, Draconic
- **Design notes:** Dragons by definition have lairs and ecology
  that extends regional effects. Even Young dragons should have a
  meaningful territorial range.

### Elemental

Beings of elemental matter: fire, water, earth, air, plus more
exotic elements (smoke, magma, ice).

- **Examples:** Air Elemental, Fire Elemental, Earth Elemental,
  Water Elemental, Salamander, Galeb Duhr, Magmin
- **Typical alignment:** Neutral; some lean Chaotic
- **Common traits:** Damage immunity or resistance to their
  element; vulnerability to the opposing element; immunity to
  poison and several conditions (exhaustion, paralyzed, petrified,
  poisoned, unconscious)
- **Languages:** Aquan, Auran, Ignan, Terran (one per element); some
  speak Primordial
- **Design notes:** Elementals are *embodied force* — they don't have
  cunning. Sentient elementals (genies, salamanders) are the
  exception, and they have personality.

### Fey

Otherworldly beings of magic and nature. Tricksters, nature spirits,
witches.

- **Examples:** Pixie, Sprite, Dryad, Satyr, Centaur, Hag (Night,
  Sea, Green), Quickling
- **Typical alignment:** Chaotic Neutral, Chaotic Evil, Neutral Good
- **Common traits:** Magic Resistance, innate spellcasting (often
  illusion or charm), Fey ancestry (advantage on saves against
  charm)
- **Languages:** Sylvan, Common; some speak Druidic or Elvish
- **Design notes:** Fey *bargain*. They rarely fight to kill if a
  deal is possible. Most Fey encounters should have a non-violent
  resolution option, even if hidden.

### Fiend

Beings of evil from the lower planes: devils (Lawful Evil) and
demons (Chaotic Evil), plus rakshasas, yugoloths, and night hags.

- **Examples:** Imp, Pit Fiend, Balor, Glabrezu, Quasit, Erinyes
- **Typical alignment:** Lawful Evil (devils) or Chaotic Evil
  (demons); some Neutral Evil
- **Common traits:** Damage resistances (cold, fire, lightning;
  resistance to non-magical weapons), Magic Resistance, innate
  spellcasting, telepathy, fly, teleport
- **Common immunities:** Charmed (for many), Frightened (for
  higher-CR), poison damage
- **Languages:** Infernal (devils) or Abyssal (demons); telepathy
- **Design notes:** Fiends should have *agendas* aligned with their
  origin plane. Devils make corrupting bargains; demons spread
  chaos. A Fiend with no purpose is just a damage-dealer.

### Giant

Large to huge humanoid-shaped creatures with culture and society.

- **Examples:** Hill Giant, Stone Giant, Frost Giant, Fire Giant,
  Cloud Giant, Storm Giant, Ogre, Ettin
- **Typical alignment:** Varies by giant type (Chaotic Evil for
  hills, Neutral for stone, etc.)
- **Common traits:** Brute melee damage, Rock Throwing, advantage
  on Strength checks
- **Languages:** Giant, sometimes Common
- **Design notes:** Giants have culture (the ordning, runic
  traditions). Even feral hill giants live in clans. Use this for
  flavor — a "lone giant" is unusual and meaningful.

### Humanoid

The most numerous type. PCs, NPCs, and standard intelligent
species: humans, elves, dwarves, halflings, orcs, goblins, gnolls,
kobolds, lizardfolk.

- **Examples:** Cultist, Bandit, Bandit Captain, Knight, Mage,
  Tribal Warrior, Berserker
- **Typical alignment:** Any
- **Common traits:** Skill-based (Stealth, Athletics), trained
  weapons, organized tactics (Pack Tactics for goblinoids)
- **Languages:** One or more per species
- **Design notes:** Humanoids are *susceptible to charm and fear* —
  this is a defining vulnerability for parties with enchantment
  casters. A Humanoid monster that's effectively immune to charm
  (without a tag or trait explaining why) breaks expectations.

### Monstrosity

Catch-all for unnatural creatures that aren't Beasts but aren't from
elsewhere either — chimerical, magical, or evolutionary outliers.

- **Examples:** Owlbear (wait, this is debated), Manticore, Bulette,
  Lamia, Hippogriff, Centaur (Fey in 5e, but debated)
- **Typical alignment:** Unaligned (most), some Lawful or Chaotic
  intelligent
- **Common traits:** Wide variety; often Tremorsense, Pounce,
  Magic Resistance
- **Languages:** Common for intelligent Monstrosities; none for
  bestial ones
- **Design notes:** Monstrosities are the "natural fantasy" bucket
  — creatures that fit in the world without being alien (Aberration)
  or planar (Fiend/Celestial). Most invented creatures default
  here unless they fit cleanly elsewhere.

### Ooze

Slimes, jellies, puddings, and amorphous absorbers.

- **Examples:** Gelatinous Cube, Black Pudding, Ochre Jelly, Gray
  Ooze, Slithering Tracker
- **Typical alignment:** Unaligned
- **Common traits:** Amorphous (can squeeze through small gaps),
  blindsight, no eyes/no ears (immune to gaze attacks and noise-
  based abilities), corrosive or digestive damage on touch
- **Common immunities:** Charmed, blinded, deafened, exhaustion,
  frightened, prone
- **Languages:** None
- **Design notes:** Oozes are *environmental hazards as much as
  enemies*. They lurk in dungeon corridors. They're slow but
  punishing on contact. Players who don't notice them get hurt.

### Plant

Plant-based creatures: walking trees, vine horrors, blights, shambling
mounds. Sentient vegetation.

- **Examples:** Awakened Shrub, Awakened Tree, Treant, Shambling
  Mound, Blight (any), Myconid
- **Typical alignment:** Varies; Treants Chaotic Good; Blights
  Neutral Evil; Myconids Lawful Neutral
- **Common traits:** Damage resistance to lightning (or vulnerability
  to fire); Tremorsense; immunity to charm and exhaustion
- **Languages:** Sylvan, Common, or none
- **Design notes:** Plants tie to ecology — they're tied to a place,
  not roaming threats. Use them for forest, swamp, jungle, or
  Underdark biomes.

### Undead

Animated remains and unliving spirits: zombies, skeletons, ghosts,
liches, vampires, wraiths.

- **Examples:** Zombie, Skeleton, Ghoul, Wraith, Lich, Vampire,
  Ghost, Banshee, Mummy, Death Knight
- **Typical alignment:** Neutral Evil (most), Lawful Evil (Lich,
  Death Knight), Chaotic Evil (some Vampires); rare Neutral
- **Common traits:** Damage resistance to necrotic; vulnerability or
  resistance to radiant (varies); immunity to poison and several
  conditions (charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned)
- **Languages:** Languages they spoke in life, plus often Necril or
  the language of their creator
- **Design notes:** Undead come in three flavors: *mindless* (zombies,
  skeletons; cannon fodder), *driven* (ghouls, wights; predatory),
  and *sentient* (vampires, liches, ghosts; planning antagonists).
  Pick the flavor and design around it.

## 2. Type Tags

Tags qualify a creature's type without changing it. They're shown in
parentheses after the type: e.g., `Humanoid (Goblinoid)`.

### Common tags

| Tag | Applies to | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Demon | Fiend | Chaotic Evil planar origin |
| Devil | Fiend | Lawful Evil planar origin |
| Yugoloth | Fiend | Neutral Evil mercenary fiend |
| Goblinoid | Humanoid | Goblin, Hobgoblin, Bugbear family |
| Orc | Humanoid | Orc lineage |
| Elf | Humanoid | Elf lineage |
| Dwarf | Humanoid | Dwarf lineage |
| Human | Humanoid | Human lineage |
| Shapechanger | Any | Can alter form (lycanthropes, ditto-style) |
| Titan | Any | Cosmic-scale, planar-significance creature |
| Any race | Humanoid | "Use any humanoid species" for generic blocks |

### Tag effects on play

Some spells and abilities target specific tags. For instance, an
abjurer's ability might affect only Fiend (Devil), not all Fiends.
A ranger's Favored Enemy might apply to Humanoid (Orc) specifically.
Tag your creature to enable these interactions.

## 3. Alignment Conventions

Most monsters list a *typical* alignment, not a binding one. Some
are *Unaligned* (Beasts, most Constructs, most Oozes).

### Alignment combinations

- **Lawful Good** — Devotion to order and benevolence (paladin-type)
- **Neutral Good** — Pure benevolence without dogma
- **Chaotic Good** — Benevolent rebel (Robin Hood-type)
- **Lawful Neutral** — Order without moral concern (modrons, judges)
- **True Neutral** — Balance, indifference, naturalistic
- **Chaotic Neutral** — Freedom without moral concern (some Fey)
- **Lawful Evil** — Tyranny, cruel order (devils, mind flayers)
- **Neutral Evil** — Self-interested evil (many fiends, ghouls)
- **Chaotic Evil** — Destruction, sadism (demons, many undead)
- **Unaligned** — No moral capacity (most Beasts, Oozes, Plants,
  mindless Undead, mindless Constructs)
- **Any alignment** — When the type spans the chart (humanoids
  often are)

### Alignment as design

A monster's typical alignment shapes how it acts but doesn't bind
individual creatures. A "Chaotic Evil" demon can be bargained with;
a "Lawful Good" angel can be wrathful. Use alignment as a guide for
default behavior, not as a script.

## 4. Languages

A creature's known languages signal its intelligence and culture.

### Standard languages

| Language | Speakers |
|---|---|
| Common | Most humanoids, civilized species |
| Dwarvish | Dwarves |
| Elvish | Elves |
| Giant | Giants, Ogres, Ettins |
| Gnomish | Gnomes |
| Goblin | Goblinoids |
| Halfling | Halflings |
| Orc | Orcs |
| Abyssal | Demons, Chaotic Evil planar |
| Celestial | Celestials, devout clerics |
| Draconic | Dragons, dragon-touched humanoids |
| Deep Speech | Aberrations, Underdark inhabitants |
| Infernal | Devils, Lawful Evil planar |
| Primordial | Elementals (with element-specific dialects) |
| Sylvan | Fey, dryads, treants |
| Undercommon | Underdark surface species |

### Special communication

- **Telepathy (range)** — silent mental communication, doesn't require
  the recipient's language; common for Aberrations, high-CR Fiends,
  and some Celestials.
- **Understands but doesn't speak** — common for Beasts trained as
  familiars; the creature comprehends commands but can't speak in
  the target language.
- **No languages (—)** — the creature is non-verbal; mindless or
  bestial. The em-dash is the standard 2024 notation.

## 5. Size Categories

| Size | Space | Reach | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tiny | 2.5 × 2.5 ft. | 0 ft. or 5 ft. | Sprite, Pixie, mouse, imp |
| Small | 5 × 5 ft. | 5 ft. | Halfling, Goblin, Kobold |
| Medium | 5 × 5 ft. | 5 ft. | Human, Elf, Dwarf, Orc |
| Large | 10 × 10 ft. | 10 ft. (typical) | Ogre, Horse, Owlbear |
| Huge | 15 × 15 ft. | 15 ft. (typical) | Giant, Hill Giant, T-Rex |
| Gargantuan | 20 × 20 ft. (or more) | 20 ft. or more | Dragon (Ancient), Tarrasque |

**Design note:** size drives a lot of tactical feel. A Large creature
in a 10-ft. corridor blocks the whole party from passing. A Huge
creature in a normal dungeon room dominates the space. Pick size
deliberately, not by gut.

## 7. Sense Conventions

| Sense | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Darkvision | 60 ft. (standard), 120 ft. (deep-darkness species) | Sees in dim light as bright, darkness as dim |
| Blindsight | 10–60 ft. | Sees without sight; oozes, bats, blind oracles |
| Tremorsense | 30–120 ft. | Detects vibrations through ground; bulettes, purple worms |
| Truesight | 30–120 ft. | Sees through illusions, invisibility, ethereal; sees true form of shapechangers |

### Combination conventions

- **Aboleths, Mind Flayers:** Darkvision 120 ft. (deep Underdark)
- **Bulettes, Purple Worms:** Tremorsense 60–120 ft. (burrowers)
- **Beholders:** Darkvision 120 ft. (no Truesight despite many eyes)
- **Liches, Greater Demons/Devils:** Truesight 60–120 ft.
- **Oozes:** Blindsight 60 ft., usually no other vision

## 8. Quick Type Selection Guide

When unsure what type to assign to a new creature:

| Concept | Most likely type |
|---|---|
| Mundane animal, even strange | Beast |
| Magical animal, sentient enough to plot but not human-like | Monstrosity |
| Human-like culture and language | Humanoid |
| Huge human-shape with society | Giant |
| Plant-shaped or fungal | Plant |
| Animated rock / fire / water | Elemental |
| Built by a creator | Construct |
| Walking dead or spirit of the dead | Undead |
| Goo / slime | Ooze |
| Mind-bending / from beyond | Aberration |
| Heaven-born | Celestial |
| Hell- or Abyss-born | Fiend |
| Magical, tricky, nature-tied | Fey |
| Reptilian, breath-weapon-capable, magical | Dragon |
