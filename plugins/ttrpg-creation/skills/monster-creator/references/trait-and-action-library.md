# Trait and Action Library — 5e 2024

A library of recurring D&D 5e traits, actions, bonus actions,
reactions, legendary actions, and lair action patterns. Use as
building blocks when designing a stat block — don't reinvent what
already works.

---

## 1. Recurring Traits

Traits are passive features that shape how the creature interacts
with combat. The classic 5e traits group into about a dozen patterns.

### Pack Tactics
> The [creature] has advantage on attack rolls against a creature if
> at least one of the [creature]'s allies is within 5 feet of the
> creature and the ally isn't incapacitated.

**When to use:** Group hunters, swarms of small humanoids, units that
work as a team (goblins, hobgoblins, wolves, kobolds).
**Power cost:** Adds ~10–20% effective offense; account for in CR.

### Magic Resistance
> The [creature] has advantage on saving throws against spells and
> other magical effects.

**When to use:** Beings of inherent magic (Fey, Fiends, Celestials,
high-CR Aberrations). Stop using on naturalistic creatures.
**Power cost:** Significant. Adds ~+1 to effective CR. Standard for
CR 5+ planar creatures.

### Charge
> If the [creature] moves at least 20 feet straight toward a target
> and then hits it with a [attack] on the same turn, the target takes
> an extra [N]d[X] [damage type] damage. If the target is a creature,
> it must succeed on a DC [X] Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.

**When to use:** Brutes with momentum (rams, bulettes, tribal cavalry,
charging beasts).
**Power cost:** Conditional bonus; adds ~5% effective DPR.

### Keen Senses (Smell / Sight / Hearing)
> The [creature] has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely
> on [smell/sight/hearing].

**When to use:** Predators (Smell), birds and far-seeing creatures
(Sight), bats and nocturnal hunters (Hearing). Combines naturally with
Lurker role.
**Power cost:** Nominal.

### Amphibious
> The [creature] can breathe air and water.

**When to use:** Aquatic creatures that operate in both environments.
**Power cost:** Nominal; expands ecology rather than combat.

### Magic Weapon Resistance (Resistant to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage from Nonmagical Attacks)
> The [creature] is resistant to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing
> damage from nonmagical attacks.

**When to use:** Planar beings (high-CR Fiends, Celestials, Elementals),
some Undead (ghosts, wraiths). Should be CR 5+ to avoid trivializing
low-level encounters.
**Power cost:** ×1.5 effective HP for CR calculation up to CR 10;
adjust the listed HP downward accordingly OR keep HP and bump CR.

### Innate Spellcasting
> The [creature]'s spellcasting ability is [Cha/Wis/Int] (spell save
> DC [X], +[Y] to hit with spell attacks). It can innately cast the
> following spells, requiring no material components:
> At will: [spells]
> 3/day each: [spells]
> 1/day each: [spells]

**When to use:** Magic creatures whose abilities are *innate* rather
than learned (dragons, fiends, fey, celestials).
**Power cost:** Each spell tier contributes to offensive CR; calculate
the most-used effective spell as base damage.

### Spellcasting
> The [creature] is an [Nth]-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting
> ability is [Cha/Wis/Int] (spell save DC [X], +[Y] to hit with spell
> attacks). It has the following [class] spells prepared:
> Cantrips (at will): [spells]
> 1st level (Nslots): [spells]
> ...

**When to use:** Trained casters (mages, clerics, sorcerers as
monsters). Distinct from Innate Spellcasting in that components are
required and slots are tracked.
**Power cost:** Use the highest-impact prepared spell as the
offensive anchor.

### Shapechanger
> The [creature] can use its action to polymorph into a Small or
> Medium humanoid it has seen, or back into its true form. Its
> statistics, other than its size, are the same in each form. Any
> equipment it is wearing or carrying isn't transformed. It reverts
> to its true form if it dies.

**When to use:** Lycanthropes, dopplegangers, succubi, devils with
masquerade.
**Power cost:** Mostly narrative; combat impact is usually low.

### Aggressive
> As a bonus action, the [creature] can move up to its speed toward a
> hostile creature it can see.

**When to use:** Brutes and feral predators that should close distance
quickly (orcs, hellhounds).
**Power cost:** Small; bonus action that's almost always used.

### Stench
> Any creature other than a [creature type] that starts its turn
> within 5 feet of the [creature] must succeed on a DC [X] Constitution
> saving throw or be poisoned until the start of its next turn. On a
> successful saving throw, the creature is immune to the [creature]'s
> Stench for 24 hours.

**When to use:** Vile beings (troglodytes, ghouls). Creates a slight
debuff aura.
**Power cost:** Low; Poisoned condition is moderate.

### Frightful Presence
> Each creature of the [creature]'s choice within [60–120] feet of the
> [creature] and aware of it must succeed on a DC [X] Wisdom saving
> throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the
> saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on
> itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or
> the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the [creature]'s
> Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.

**When to use:** Dragons, demon lords, titanic threats. CR 8+.
**Power cost:** Significant; treat as a controller-tier ability.

### Legendary Resistance (3/Day)
> If the [creature] fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed
> instead.

**When to use:** Solo bosses CR 11+. Counters party save-or-suck
spells that would end the fight in one round.
**Power cost:** Critical for boss fights. Without it, parties shut down
solo bosses in round 1.

### Regeneration
> The [creature] regains [N] hit points at the start of its turn if it
> has at least 1 hit point.
> [Optional clause: If the [creature] takes [damage type] damage, this
> trait doesn't function at the start of its next turn.]

**When to use:** Trolls, hags, hydras, some undead. The clause about
specific damage type negates regen for one round, creating a
known-vulnerability mechanic.
**Power cost:** Significant. Calculate as added HP across expected
combat length.

### Pounce
> If the [creature] moves at least 20 feet straight toward a creature
> and then hits it with a Claw attack on the same turn, that target
> must succeed on a DC [X] Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
> If the target is prone, the [creature] can make one Bite attack
> against it as a bonus action.

**When to use:** Large cats, predators. Combines mobility, control,
and bonus action.
**Power cost:** Conditional; ~10% effective DPR.

### Sneak Attack (1/turn)
> The [creature] deals an extra [N]d6 damage when it hits a target
> with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when
> the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the [creature] that isn't
> incapacitated and the [creature] doesn't have disadvantage on the
> attack roll.

**When to use:** Roguish foes (drow assassins, thugs, scouts).
**Power cost:** Conditional; ~10–15% effective DPR.

### Reckless
> At the start of its turn, the [creature] can gain advantage on all
> melee weapon attack rolls it makes during that turn, but attack
> rolls against it have advantage until the start of its next turn.

**When to use:** Berserkers, frenzied creatures.
**Power cost:** Net neutral if used; ~5% bonus DPR for a defensive
penalty.

### Cunning Action
> On each of its turns, the [creature] can use a bonus action to take
> the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

**When to use:** Skirmishers, scouts, rogue-styled NPCs.
**Power cost:** Adds versatility, not raw damage; nominal CR cost.

### Telepathy (Range)
> The [creature] can communicate telepathically with any creature
> within [60–120] feet of it that has a language.

**When to use:** Aberrations, fiends, high-intelligence beings.
**Power cost:** Narrative; no combat impact.

### Darkvision / Blindsight / Tremorsense / Truesight

See `creature-types-and-tags-2024.md` Section 7 for ranges and use cases.

## 2. Multiattack Patterns

Multiattack is the engine of monster offense. The pattern shapes how
the creature feels in combat.

### Pattern: Standard Double
> The [creature] makes two [weapon] attacks.

Most common. Easy to balance, easy to GM.

### Pattern: Asymmetric Double
> The [creature] makes one [primary weapon] attack and one [secondary
> weapon] attack.

Adds variety. Primary is high-damage, secondary is utility (knockdown,
grapple, status).

### Pattern: Standard Triple
> The [creature] makes three [weapon] attacks.

CR 8+. Higher single-target potential.

### Pattern: Asymmetric Triple
> The [creature] makes one Bite attack and two Claw attacks.

Classic apex predator pattern (dragon, bear, manticore).

### Pattern: Quad+
> The [creature] makes four attacks: two Claw attacks, one Bite, and
> one Tail.

CR 17+. Boss-tier action economy.

### Pattern: Mobile Multiattack
> The [creature] makes two [weapon] attacks. It can move up to half
> its speed between attacks without provoking opportunity attacks.

Skirmisher signature. Lets the GM peel in and out.

### Pattern: Multiattack with Spell
> The [creature] makes two [weapon] attacks. It can replace one attack
> with a use of its [spell]/[cantrip].

Gish-pattern monsters (battle-mages, druid-warriors). Flexibility.

### Pattern: Forced-Multiattack
> The [creature] makes two attacks: one Bite and one Tail. If both hit
> a single target, the target must succeed on a DC [X] Constitution
> saving throw or be [stunned/poisoned/grappled].

Heavy-control on a successful double-hit. Tactical pressure.

## 3. Recharge Abilities

Recharge X–Y means the ability is usable, then unavailable, then
recharges on a roll of X–Y on a d6 at the start of each turn.

### Recharge 5–6 (33% chance per turn)

The standard "signature spike attack" pattern. Statistically fires
~once per 3 rounds (and almost always at least once in a 6-round
combat).

**Examples:**
- Dragon breath weapon
- Bulette's Leap and Crush
- Beholder's Anti-Magic Cone activates differently but is a similar
  category
- Manticore's Tail Spike volley
- Gibbering Mouther's Blinding Spittle

**Damage budget:** Treat the recharge attack as ~1.5× the creature's
single-attack damage. It's the "big move" of the stat block.

### Recharge 6 (17% chance per turn)

Rarer, for very powerful effects (single-target instant-kill,
massive AoE, transformation).

**Examples:**
- Beholder's central eye disintegrate
- Tarrasque's Reflective Carapace

**Damage budget:** Up to 2× single-attack damage, or a
condition-imposing effect that's encounter-changing.

### Recharge after Short or Long Rest

Most often used for legendary/lair actions or abilities that should
fire once per encounter. The creature uses it, then waits until the
next encounter.

## 4. Reaction Patterns

Reactions are limited-use (one per round) but powerful because they
trigger off the party's actions.

### Parry
> The [creature] adds [N] to its AC against one melee attack that
> would hit it. To do so, the [creature] must see the attacker and
> be wielding a melee weapon.

**Power cost:** Significant defensive boost; effectively raises AC
by ~3–5 against the right kind of attack.

### Riposte
> When a creature misses the [creature] with a melee attack, the
> [creature] can make one melee weapon attack against that creature.

**Power cost:** Punishes melee attackers; adds ~10–15% effective DPR
in melee-heavy combat.

### Mage Slayer
> When a creature within 5 feet of the [creature] casts a spell, the
> [creature] can make one melee weapon attack against that creature.
> If the attack hits, the creature must succeed on a [DC X]
> Constitution save or lose its Concentration on a spell.

**Power cost:** Heavy if the party has casters; nothing if it doesn't.
Spoiler-class reaction.

### Opportunist
> When a creature within 5 feet of the [creature] makes a melee
> attack against an ally of the [creature], the [creature] can use its
> reaction to make a melee attack against that creature.

**Power cost:** Punishes flankers and gang-up tactics.

### Spell Resistance (Counterspell)
> When the [creature] is hit by a spell, the [creature] can use its
> reaction to attempt to negate the spell, as if the [creature] had
> cast counterspell.

**Power cost:** Significant. CR 11+ casters and Spoilers.

### Disrupting Strike
> When the [creature] is hit by a melee attack, the [creature] can
> use its reaction to make one melee weapon attack against the
> attacker.

Brute / Soldier signature. Punishes melee.

### Vanish (or Cloak of Shadows)
> When the [creature] takes damage, the [creature] can use its
> reaction to teleport up to [30] feet to an unoccupied space it can
> see.

Lurker signature; emergency mobility.

## 5. Bonus Action Patterns

### Bite (Bonus action attack on prone target)
> If the [creature] knocks a target prone with its Pounce/Charge
> attack, it can make one Bite attack against that target as a bonus
> action.

Predator follow-up.

### Cunning Action
See Section 1. Standard for Skirmishers.

### Reposition / Teleport
> The [creature] can use a bonus action to teleport up to [30] feet to
> an unoccupied space it can see.

Skirmisher / lurker mobility.

### Command
> The [creature] uses a bonus action to issue a command. One ally
> within 30 feet that can hear or see the [creature] can use its
> reaction to make one weapon attack.

Leader signature.

### Inspire / Buff
> The [creature] uses a bonus action to inspire one ally within 30
> feet. That ally gains [N] temporary HP, OR has advantage on its
> next attack roll, OR can move up to its speed.

Leader signature.

### Quickdraw
> The [creature] can draw a weapon and make an attack with it as a
> bonus action.

For dual-wielding and rogue-pattern monsters.

## 6. Legendary Action Menus

Legendary creatures take 3 legendary actions per round (typically),
distributed across their actions. Each menu item has a cost (usually
1, 2, or 3 actions). The creature regenerates 3 actions at the start
of its turn.

### Pattern: Move + Attack Menu

```
The [creature] can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options
below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the
end of another creature's turn. The [creature] regains spent
legendary actions at the start of its turn.

[Action] Detect. The [creature] makes a Perception check.
[Action] Move. The [creature] moves up to half its speed.
[Action] Attack (Costs 2 Actions). The [creature] makes one melee
attack.
```

This is the *standard* legendary action menu. It gives the creature
positional flexibility and a damage option, while keeping the action
economy manageable.

### Pattern: Heavy Hitter Menu

```
[Action] Move. The [creature] moves up to half its speed.
[Action] Tail Sweep (Costs 2 Actions). The [creature] makes one Tail
attack. On a hit, the target makes a Dexterity save or is knocked
prone.
[Action] Wing Buffet (Costs 3 Actions). Each creature within 15 feet
must succeed on a DC [X] Dexterity save or take [N]d6 damage and be
pushed 15 feet.
```

For Brutes and Boss creatures. Each option is impactful.

### Pattern: Control Menu

```
[Action] Frightful Glare. The [creature] targets one creature within
60 feet. The target must succeed on a Wisdom save or be frightened
until the end of its next turn.
[Action] Mist Cloud (Costs 2 Actions). The [creature] becomes
obscured by mist; attacks against it have disadvantage until the
end of its next turn.
[Action] Banish Echo (Costs 3 Actions). The [creature] forces one
creature within 30 feet to succeed on a Charisma save or be banished
to the Border Ethereal until the end of its next turn.
```

For Controllers and Caster Bosses. Each option imposes a condition
or disrupts party tactics.

### Design notes

- Menu of 3–5 options is typical.
- At least one option should be an Attack equivalent (so legendary
  actions contribute to DPR).
- At least one option should be Movement or Repositioning (so the
  creature stays mobile).
- Avoid menus where every option is a no-cost "free attack" — the
  creature ends up overwhelming.

## 7. Lair Action Templates

Lair actions occur at initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties).
Three options is the standard count. The creature can't use the same
option two rounds in a row.

### Lair Action Menu Pattern

```
On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the [creature] takes
a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the [creature]
can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

- [Effect 1: environmental / terrain manipulation, no damage]
- [Effect 2: offensive, dealing damage or imposing condition]
- [Effect 3: defensive / atmospheric, supporting the creature]
```

### Example: Swamp Hag's Lair

```
On initiative count 20:
- Reeds and Roots. Roots erupt from the muddy ground in a 20-foot
  radius centered on a point the hag can see within 60 feet. Each
  creature in that area must succeed on a DC 14 Strength save or be
  restrained until the end of the next round.
- Whispered Secret. The hag whispers a single secret from a target's
  past, audible to all within 30 feet. The target must succeed on a
  DC 14 Wisdom save or have disadvantage on attack rolls until the
  end of its next turn.
- Drowning Air. The air thickens with bog-stench in a 30-foot radius
  centered on the hag. Each creature within takes 7 (2d6) poison
  damage and must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution save or be poisoned
  until the end of its next turn.
```

### Lair Action design principles

- Effects should reflect the *place*, not just the creature.
- One option should be **memorable** even if not damaging.
- One option should be **defensive** (helps the creature avoid party
  focus).
- One option should be **offensive** (deals damage or imposes a
  serious condition).
- DC should match the creature's save DC.
- Damage on offensive options should be ~half the creature's
  single-attack damage (because it's free action economy).

## 8. Regional Effects Templates

Regional effects extend the creature's presence beyond its lair into
the surrounding 1–6 miles. They're environmental flavor that signals
the creature's existence to traveling parties.

### Effect categories

- **Weather:** unnatural storms, perpetual mist, killing frost
- **Flora:** plants warp, bloom out of season, become aggressive
- **Fauna:** local animals are agitated, missing, or twisted
- **Atmosphere:** silence, oppressive heat, light bending strangely
- **Sound:** whispers carry on the wind, bells tolling without cause
- **Smell:** sulfur, rotting, sweetness without source
- **Time:** clocks run slow or fast, days feel longer

### Persistence

When the creature dies, regional effects fade over 1d10 days.

### Example for a Lich

```
- Within 6 miles of the lich's lair, plants wither and animals die
  more than seems natural.
- Within 1 mile of the lair, ghosts and other Undead are more
  common; the lich attracts them and they linger.
- Skeletal and zombie remains found in the region often rise and
  attack travelers who disturb them.
```

## 9. Mythic Actions (CR 21+)

Mythic actions trigger when a creature reaches a specified HP
threshold (often 50% HP). The creature gains a second wind of
legendary actions for the rest of the encounter.

### Pattern

```
[Mythic Trait Name].  If the [creature]'s Mythic trait is active and
the [creature] fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed
instead.

The [creature] regains 3 legendary action charges and can use the
following additional Mythic Actions:

- [Mythic Action 1]
- [Mythic Action 2]
- [Mythic Action 3]
```

Used for cosmic-tier threats where the creature genuinely has a
"second phase" of combat. Rare. Use sparingly.
