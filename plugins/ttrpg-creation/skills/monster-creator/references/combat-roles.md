# Combat Roles for D&D 5e Monsters

A creature's CR sets its power level. A creature's **combat role** sets
how it *plays* in combat. Two CR 7 monsters can feel completely
different at the table depending on their role.

This file defines eight roles, gives stat distribution patterns,
suggests signature traits, and shows how to vary the same CR across
roles.

---

## 1. The Eight Combat Roles

| Role | Tactical function | One-line behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Skirmisher | Mobile damage-dealer | Strikes and disengages, fights on the move |
| Brute | Stationary tank | Wades into melee, eats damage, dishes damage |
| Artillery | Ranged damage from distance | Stays at range, kites, hates being engaged |
| Lurker | Burst from stealth, retreat to hide | Damage spike + escape; punishes inattention |
| Soldier | Frontline anchor with multi-attack | Locks down zones, threatens area |
| Controller | Battlefield manipulator | Adds conditions, restricts movement, debuffs |
| Leader | Force multiplier for allies | Buffs minions, grants extra actions, commands |
| Spoiler | Disruptor of party tactics | Counter-magic, anti-caster, mage slayer effects |

---

## 2. Role Profiles in Detail

### Skirmisher

**Tactical function:** Hit-and-run damage. Stays mobile to avoid being
locked in melee. Trades sustained damage for positional flexibility.

**Stat distribution (relative to baseline for CR):**
- HP: −10% (lighter)
- AC: standard or +1 (Dexterity-based)
- Damage: standard or +5% (compensates for less time on target)
- Movement: +10–20 ft. above standard, OR fly speed if biologically
  appropriate
- Dexterity: high (16–20)
- Strength: average to high

**Signature traits commonly used:**
- *Cunning Action*-equivalent (Bonus Action to Dash, Disengage, Hide)
- *Mobile*-equivalent (no opportunity attacks against melee targets
  attacked)
- *Hit-and-Run* attack pattern (attack + move without opportunity
  attack)
- Climb or Fly speed

**Signature actions:**
- Multiattack with mobile component ("Attack twice, then move up to
  half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks")
- Throwing weapons or short-range ranged option

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 1: Goblin Skirmisher (variant), Tribal Skirmisher
- CR 5: Manticore (ranged + flying), Veteran Scout
- CR 10: Drow Captain, Sword Wraith Commander
- CR 15: Adult Bronze Dragon (mobile + breath weapon)

**Plays best in:** open terrain with movement room, multi-level
environments (catwalks, rooftops), encounters where the GM uses the
movement deliberately.

**Fails in:** tight corridors, single-room encounters with no
elevation.

### Brute

**Tactical function:** Stationary damage soak that hits hard in
melee. Trades mobility and Dex for HP and damage.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: +15–25% (more)
- AC: standard or −1 (natural armor vs Dex armor)
- Damage: +10–15% (high-damage swings)
- Movement: standard or 5 ft. less
- Strength: very high (18–24)
- Constitution: high

**Signature traits:**
- *Brute*-equivalent (extra damage die on melee attacks)
- *Reckless*-equivalent (advantage on melee attacks, attacks against
  it have advantage)
- *Charge*-equivalent (extra damage on first attack after a Dash)
- *Crushing Blow* (chance to knock prone on hit)

**Signature actions:**
- Multiattack: 2 attacks of high damage each
- Single signature attack with high damage (Maul, Slam, Bite)
- Recharge ability: area damage breath or thunderous attack

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 1: Ogre, Berserker
- CR 5: Hill Giant, Bulette
- CR 10: Fire Giant, Stone Giant
- CR 15: Mammoth-mounted Warlord, Ancient White Dragon (Brute slant)

**Plays best in:** focal point of room, choke points the brute can
hold, encounters where the party can't avoid melee.

**Fails in:** mobile parties that kite, ranged-heavy parties that
outrange the brute's threat.

### Artillery

**Tactical function:** Ranged damage from a safe distance. Mobile
enough to maintain range but vulnerable in melee.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: −15–20% (lower)
- AC: standard or −1
- Damage: ranged DPR matches CR; melee DPR significantly lower
- Movement: standard
- Dexterity: very high (18–20)
- Often: Intelligence or Wisdom high (for spells / focus)

**Signature traits:**
- *Sharpshooter*-equivalent (no disadvantage at long range; ignore
  partial cover)
- *Spellcasting* or *Innate Spellcasting* (for caster artillery)
- *Quickdraw*-equivalent (reload as part of attack)

**Signature actions:**
- Multiattack: 2–3 ranged attacks
- Signature spell or area ranged ability (fireball-equivalent at CR
  5+, breath weapons for dragon-like artillery)
- A weak melee option for emergencies

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 1: Goblin Boss (with shortbow), Bandit Captain
- CR 5: Mage, Elf Archer, Spellweaver
- CR 10: Adult Black Dragon (acid breath), War Mage
- CR 15: Archmage, Adult Blue Dragon

**Plays best in:** elevated positions, behind cover, layered defenses
(brute in front, artillery behind).

**Fails in:** flanking parties that get melee fast, single-room
encounters where range is meaningless.

### Lurker

**Tactical function:** Burst damage from stealth, retreat to hide,
repeat. Punishes parties that don't watch their backs.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: −10% (relies on stealth, not sponging)
- AC: standard or +1
- Damage: burst — high single-attack damage with surprise multiplier
- Movement: standard or higher; usually climb or burrow
- Dexterity: very high (18+)
- Stealth: proficiency expertise (very high modifier)

**Signature traits:**
- *Surprise Attack*-equivalent (extra damage on first attack vs
  surprised target)
- *Stealth*-equivalent (advantage on Stealth checks; can hide as
  bonus action)
- *Pounce* or *Sneak Attack*
- *Camouflage* (advantage on Stealth in specific terrain)
- *Lurker in Darkness* (advantage in dim/dark; disadvantage in bright)

**Signature actions:**
- Heavy single-attack with surprise bonus
- *Hide* as bonus action after attacking
- Often: poisons, paralysis, or restrain effects on hit

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 1/4: Giant Spider (web ambusher), Stirge
- CR 5: Mimic-variant, Drow Inquisitor (assassin-build)
- CR 10: Adult Green Dragon (forest stalker), Slithering Tracker
- CR 15: Roper, Mind Flayer (ambush variant)

**Plays best in:** environments with hiding spots (dungeons, forests,
fog, dim corridors), encounters where the lurker can choose its
moment.

**Fails in:** open daylight, parties with passive Perception above
the lurker's Stealth roll.

### Soldier

**Tactical function:** Frontline anchor. Holds a zone, threatens
adjacent squares, locks down enemies in melee. The "default" combat
monster.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: standard
- AC: standard or +1 (well-armored)
- Damage: standard, distributed across multiattack
- Movement: standard
- Strength + Dexterity: balanced and high

**Signature traits:**
- *Polearm Master*-equivalent (opportunity attack with reach when
  enemy enters reach)
- *Sentinel*-equivalent (reduce target's speed to 0 on opportunity
  attack hit)
- *Pack Tactics* if part of a unit
- *Combat Training* (advantage on attacks of opportunity)

**Signature actions:**
- Multiattack: 2–3 attacks of standard damage each
- Reaction: counterattack on miss, or shove as a bonus action

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 1/2: Hobgoblin Soldier (Pack Tactics + javelin/longsword)
- CR 3: Knight, Veteran (standard fantasy fighter)
- CR 5: Hobgoblin Captain, Gladiator
- CR 10: War Priest, Elite Soldier
- CR 15: Warden, Ancient Bronze Dragon (Soldier slant)

**Plays best in:** front-line of formations, defensive positions,
encounters with multiple monsters where the Soldier locks down
characters while others act.

**Fails in:** mobility-focused parties that don't engage in melee.

### Controller

**Tactical function:** Battlefield manipulation. Imposes conditions,
restricts movement, debuffs party. Damage is secondary.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: standard
- AC: standard
- Damage: −10–15% (gives up damage for control)
- Movement: standard
- Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma: high (mental-stat-driven)

**Signature traits:**
- *Spellcasting* with control-focused spells (web, hold person,
  banishment, hypnotic pattern, slow)
- *Innate Spellcasting* equivalents (charm gaze, frighten cone,
  stunning shriek)
- Aura effects (within 30 ft., creatures have disadvantage on X)
- *Restraining Bind* on attack hit (web, vine grasp, tentacle, etc.)

**Signature actions:**
- Charm / fear / paralysis ability on Recharge 5–6
- Multiattack with control rider (Bite + Grapple, Tentacle + Restrained)
- AoE control spell (Hypnotic Pattern, Web, Black Tentacles)

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 2: Sea Hag (charm gaze), Banshee (frighten cone)
- CR 5: Mind Flayer (Stunning Squid), Banshee
- CR 10: Adult Brass Dragon (sleep + deep speech), Glabrezu (Spellcasting)
- CR 15: Vampire (Charm + Dominate Person), Adult Green Dragon
  (Suggestion in dragon form)

**Plays best in:** with allies who benefit from disabled party
members (a controller + a brute is the classic boss combo),
encounters with terrain the controller can manipulate (chokepoints
to web, water to hold-person across).

**Fails in:** parties with strong condition saves, parties that focus
fire the controller down before it gets to cast.

### Leader

**Tactical function:** Force multiplier. Doesn't deal big damage
directly; instead buffs allies, grants extra actions, or commands
the field.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: standard or +5%
- AC: standard or +1
- Damage: −15–20% (the damage comes from allies)
- Movement: standard
- Charisma or Intelligence: high (leadership-driven)

**Signature traits:**
- *Leadership*-equivalent (allies within 30 ft. add d4 to attack/save
  rolls)
- *Cunning Action*-equivalent given to allies via command
- *Inspiring Presence* (allies regain HP or save against fear)
- *Tactician* (an ally within 30 ft. can use its reaction to make a
  weapon attack at the Leader's command)

**Signature actions:**
- Multiattack including a *Command* action that lets an ally take an
  Attack action
- *Rally* or *Inspire* (heal or buff allies)
- Personal attack option that's average for CR

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 3: Hobgoblin Captain, Knight Commander
- CR 5: Hobgoblin Warlord, War Priest
- CR 10: Knight Lord, Bandit Lord
- CR 15: General, Cult Leader (Bonded with a Fiend), Black Knight

**Plays best in:** encounters with minions (the Leader is force-
multiplying *something*), formations of 5+ creatures.

**Fails in:** solo encounters (a Leader with no one to lead is a
Soldier with fewer features).

### Spoiler

**Tactical function:** Counter-tactics. Specifically punishes one
type of party strategy: anti-magic, anti-stealth, anti-charm, anti-
caster, etc.

**Stat distribution:**
- HP: standard
- AC: standard
- Damage: standard
- Movement: standard
- Often: Intelligence or Wisdom high (countering)

**Signature traits:**
- *Magic Resistance* (advantage on saves vs spells)
- *Mage Slayer*-equivalent (opportunity attack on adjacent casters
  casting; disadvantage on Concentration)
- *Truesight* (sees through illusion and invisibility)
- *Antimagic Aura* (suppress spellcasting in 30 ft.)
- *Counterspell*-equivalent as reaction
- *Blind Sense* (negates stealth)

**Signature actions:**
- Mage-slayer style melee that hits Concentration
- Reaction that disrupts the action being taken against it

**Examples by CR:**
- CR 5: Mage Slayer, Inquisitor
- CR 10: Beholder (anti-caster eyes), Mind Flayer (anti-mental)
- CR 15: Death Knight (Magic Resistance + Counterspell)
- CR 20: Lich (massive anti-party toolkit)

**Plays best in:** encounters where the Spoiler counters a specific
party strength. A Spoiler vs. a caster-heavy party is a memorable
threat; a Spoiler vs. a martial-heavy party is just a Soldier.

**Fails in:** parties whose strategies the Spoiler doesn't counter.
Match the Spoiler to the party.

## 3. Role Selection by Concept

When deciding a creature's role, look at the concept:

| Concept clue | Most likely role |
|---|---|
| "Hunts in packs" | Skirmisher or Soldier (with Pack Tactics) |
| "Stalks from the shadows" | Lurker |
| "Charges into battle" | Brute |
| "Casts spells / hurls magic" | Artillery or Controller |
| "Commands a horde" | Leader |
| "Wears heavy armor and a shield" | Soldier |
| "Hates wizards / clerics" | Spoiler |
| "Disables and captures rather than kills" | Controller |
| "Fast and slippery" | Skirmisher |
| "Massive and slow" | Brute |
| "Sniper / archer / ranged hunter" | Artillery |
| "Web-weaver / trapper" | Controller or Lurker |
| "Battlefield commander / king" | Leader |

## 4. Mixed Roles

A creature can blend roles, but the blend should be intentional:

- **Skirmisher + Lurker:** stealth-mobile assassin (Drow Spider Cultist)
- **Brute + Controller:** grapple-tank (Mountain Troll, Bulette)
- **Artillery + Leader:** spellcasting general (Archmage with command)
- **Soldier + Spoiler:** anti-caster knight (Antipaladin)
- **Brute + Lurker:** ambush bruiser (Owlbear from undergrowth)

When blending, ensure the creature still has *one* primary role.
A creature trying to do everything does nothing well.

## 5. Same CR, Different Roles

To show how different roles feel at the same CR, here are five CR 5
sketches:

### CR 5 Skirmisher: Wind Strider (centaur cousin, hill galloper)
- AC 14, HP 100, Speed 50 ft.
- Multiattack: 2 lance strikes; can move 25 ft. between attacks
  without provoking opportunity attacks
- *Hit and Run.* When the Wind Strider hits with a melee attack, it
  can move up to 15 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

### CR 5 Brute: Stoneback (giant turtle-bear hybrid)
- AC 17 (natural armor), HP 180, Speed 25 ft.
- Multiattack: 1 bite (3d10+5) + 1 claw (2d6+5, knocked prone on hit)
- *Heavy Frame.* Disadvantage on Dexterity saves; advantage on
  saves vs being moved.

### CR 5 Artillery: Heralded Cantor (cult magic-user)
- AC 13, HP 110, Speed 30 ft.
- Spellcasting (4th level): fireball 1/day, scorching ray 3/day,
  fire bolt at will
- *Combat Magic.* Cantor can cast a cantrip as a bonus action after
  casting a leveled spell.

### CR 5 Lurker: Veiled Stalker (drow-derived shadow predator)
- AC 15, HP 100, Speed 30 ft.; advantage on Stealth in dim/dark
- Surprise Attack adds 3d8 damage on first attack
- *Shadow Step* (bonus action): teleport up to 30 ft. between two
  areas of dim light or darkness

### CR 5 Controller: Vinekin Choirmaster (plant cultist)
- AC 13, HP 120, Speed 30 ft.; spellcasting (3rd level)
- *Entangling Hymn* (Recharge 5–6): 30-ft. cone, DC 15 Strength save,
  on failure restrained until end of next turn
- *Vine Lash* attack: melee, restrained on hit (DC 14 Strength to
  break free)

All five are CR 5. All five demand different party tactics. None
feel like the same monster.

## 6. Role-Driven Design Checklist

When building a new monster, after picking role:

- [ ] Stat distribution adjusted to role profile (HP, AC, damage,
      ability scores)
- [ ] At least one signature trait that flags the role (Pack Tactics
      for Soldier, Stealth expertise for Lurker, Spellcasting for
      Controller / Artillery)
- [ ] Multiattack pattern fits the role (mobile for Skirmisher,
      high single attack for Brute, ranged for Artillery)
- [ ] One signature action that *demonstrates the role at the table*
      (something a GM will *use* and players will *remember*)
- [ ] Bonus action that supports the role (Disengage for Skirmisher,
      Command for Leader, Hide for Lurker)
- [ ] Reaction (optional) that punishes the wrong tactic against the
      role
- [ ] If CR 11+ with legendary actions: the menu reinforces the role
      (Lurker has stealth-restoring legendary, Brute has extra-attack
      legendary, etc.)
