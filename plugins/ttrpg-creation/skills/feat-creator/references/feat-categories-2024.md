# Feat Categories — D&D 5e 2024

The 2024 Player's Handbook organizes feats into **four categories**, each
locked to a specific moment in a character's progression and built on a
distinct structural template. A custom feat that ignores its category's
template reads as homebrew at first glance; one that follows it reads as
official. Get the template right before worrying about balance.

## Category Comparison Table

| Category | Access | ASI | Prerequisites | Power position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | Level 1 (via background) | none | none | Floor — useful but never must-pick |
| **General** | Level 4+ (ASI slots) | +1 to one listed score | Level 4+, sometimes score/feature | The homebrew reference point |
| **Fighting Style** | Via Fighting Style feature | none | Fighting Style feature | Narrow, combat-only |
| **Epic Boon** | Level 19+ | +1, max raised to 30 | Level 19+ | Capstone, identity-defining |

---

## Origin Feats

**Header template:** `*Origin Feat*` — no prerequisite line, no Ability
Score Increase benefit.

**Acquisition.** Every 2024 background grants one specific Origin feat at
level 1. Humans gain an additional Origin feat of their choice. This means
an Origin feat is something a character gets *for free, before the game
has started* — its power must assume zero opportunity cost.

**The 10 official Origin feats, one-line analysis each:**

| Feat | What it teaches about the category |
|---|---|
| **Alert** | Initiative swap + PB to Initiative — strong but invisible outside round 1 |
| **Crafter** | Tool proficiencies + discount + fast crafting — pure downtime economy |
| **Healer** | Reroll 1s on healing dice + battlefield first aid — narrow niche, always relevant in it |
| **Lucky** | Luck Points equal to PB per Long Rest — the resource ceiling for the category |
| **Magic Initiate** | 2 cantrips + 1 level-1 spell, 1 free cast/Long Rest — the dipping ceiling |
| **Musician** | Heroic Inspiration to allies after a rest — situational, social-flavored |
| **Savage Attacker** | Reroll weapon damage 1/turn — small, passive, combat floor |
| **Skilled** | 3 proficiencies (skills or tools) — the proficiency yardstick |
| **Tavern Brawler** | Unarmed rerolls + push — build-enabling for a narrow archetype |
| **Tough** | +2 HP per level — the pure-numbers yardstick |

**Dissection — Magic Initiate.** Choose a spell list (Cleric, Druid, or
Wizard): two cantrips plus one level-1 spell, castable once per Long Rest
for free, and again with any slots you have. Marked **Repeatable** (each
time, a different list). Lessons: (1) Origin feats may grant *real*
resources if they are once-per-Long-Rest; (2) Repeatable is safe when
each instance forces a *different choice*; (3) the spell list choice, not
a fixed spell, is what keeps it from being a must-pick.

**Dissection — Skilled.** Three proficiencies, any mix of skills and
tools. Marked **Repeatable**. This is the category's exchange rate: one
Origin feat ≈ three proficiencies. Any custom Origin feat granting more
than that in pure proficiency value is over budget.

**What an Origin feat may grant:** proficiencies, once-per-Long-Rest
resources (PB-scaled at most, cf. Lucky), rerolls of damage or healing
dice, flat HP (cf. Tough), narrow situational riders, downtime economy,
social texture.

**What an Origin feat must never grant:** an Ability Score Increase, a
flat numeric combat bonus that is always on (+X to hit, +X AC, +X damage
with no condition), Extra Attack or anything resembling it, Advantage on
a broad and frequent roll type, anything a General feat sells at level 4.

## General Feats

**Header template:** `*General Feat (Prerequisite: Level 4+[, …])*` —
and the first benefit is always **Ability Score Increase.**

**The universal half-feat.** In 2024, *every* General feat includes
"+1 to one ability score from a short thematic list, max 20." The 2014
distinction between full feats and half-feats is gone. Consequence for
design: the +1 is *half the budget*; the remaining benefits of a custom
General feat should be worth roughly one Origin feat.

**Legal prerequisite types** (combine sparingly, one or two at most):

- **Level.** `Level 4+` is the floor for every General feat. Nothing
  higher appears on official General feats except via ability gates.
- **Ability score.** `Strength 13+`, `Dexterity or Strength 13+` — gates
  the feat to characters already invested in the concept (Grappler,
  Polearm Master pattern).
- **Class feature.** `Spellcasting or Pact Magic feature` — the War
  Caster / Ritual Caster pattern.
- **Training.** Armor training or weapon proficiency prerequisites
  (Heavily Armored, Medium Armor Master pattern).

**Dissection — Great Weapon Master.** +1 Strength; once per turn, add PB
to damage with a heavy weapon when you hit; Hew (Bonus Action attack
after a crit or a kill). Lessons: the damage rider is *conditional and
once per turn*, scaled on PB rather than a flat number; the Bonus Action
attack has a trigger condition. The 2014 −5/+10 mechanic is gone —
do not import it into custom feats.

**Dissection — Resilient.** +1 to one score *and* saving throw
proficiency in that score. Two benefits, both passive, no text longer
than three lines. The lesson: a General feat can be *small and clean* —
the floor is "ASI + one solid passive."

**Dissection — War Caster.** +1 Int/Wis/Cha; Advantage on Constitution
saves for Concentration; somatic components with hands full; opportunity
attack replaced by a spell. Three benefits, all serving ONE identity
(the frontline caster). The lesson: multiple benefits are fine when they
all point at the same fantasy — that is coherence, not a grab bag.

## Fighting Style Feats

**Header template:** `*Fighting Style Feat (Prerequisite: Fighting Style
Feature)*` — no Ability Score Increase.

**Acquisition.** Granted by the Fighting Style class feature (Fighter
level 1, Paladin level 2, Ranger level 2) and replaceable when those
classes level up. A character without the feature can never take one,
which is what licenses their always-on numeric bonuses.

**The 10 official Fighting Style feats:** Archery (+2 ranged attack
rolls), Blind Fighting (Blindsight 10 ft), Defense (+1 AC in armor),
Dueling (+2 damage, one-handed alone), Great Weapon Fighting (treat 1s
and 2s as 3 on damage), Interception (Reaction, reduce ally damage),
Protection (Reaction, impose Disadvantage), Thrown Weapon Fighting
(draw + thrown damage), Two-Weapon Fighting (add modifier to off-hand),
Unarmed Fighting (d6/d8 unarmed, grapple damage).

**Dissection — Defense.** +1 AC while wearing armor. The category's
*ceiling for passive value*: always on, universally useful. Any custom
Fighting Style stronger than an unconditional +1 AC is over budget.

**Dissection — Protection.** Reaction, impose Disadvantage on an attack
against a creature within 5 feet while you hold a shield. The category's
*floor*: conditional, costs your Reaction, demands positioning. A custom
style weaker than this will never be picked over Defense.

**Category rules:** combat effects only; one mechanic, not a package
(a single benefit block, the shortest texts in the chapter); no
resources, no per-rest limits — styles are *always on* or
Reaction-based; the narrow weapon/armor condition IS the balance lever.

## Epic Boon Feats

**Header template:** `*Epic Boon Feat (Prerequisite: Level 19+[, …])*` —
and the ASI reads "increase one ability score by 1, **to a maximum
of 30**."

**Position.** Taken in the level 19 feat slot (and DMs may award more).
Epic Boons are allowed to *break the game's normal rules*: permanent
flight-grade mobility, teleportation every turn, auto-hits, raising the
ability cap itself. Calibrate against "the campaign ends at 20," not
against long-term balance.

**The 12 official Epic Boons, one-line analysis each:**

| Boon | What it grants |
|---|---|
| **Combat Prowess** | Once per turn, turn a missed melee attack into a hit |
| **Dimensional Travel** | After Attack/Magic action, teleport 30 ft for free |
| **Energy Resistance** | Resistance to 2 damage types + Reaction redirect |
| **Fate** | d10 (add or subtract) on a nearby d20 Test, recharges on Initiative |
| **Fortitude** | +40 HP and regain HP when healing others |
| **Irresistible Offense** | Ignore all resistance to Bludgeoning/Piercing/Slashing; crit = double extra |
| **Recovery** | 1/day self-revive at half HP + dice pool self-heal |
| **Skill** | Proficiency in ALL skills + Expertise in one |
| **Speed** | +30 ft Speed + Disengage as a Bonus Action |
| **Spell Recall** | Free chance (d4) to not expend slots on level 1–4 spells |
| **the Night Spirit** | Merge with shadows: Invisibility + resistance in Dim Light/Darkness |
| **Truesight** | Truesight 60 ft, permanent |

**Dissection — Boon of Combat Prowess.** Once per turn, when you miss
with a melee attack, hit instead. No save, no resource, no recharge —
a standing rewrite of the d20. This is the offensive ceiling.

**Dissection — Boon of Fate.** Reaction-flavored d10 swing on someone
else's d20 Test, recharging when you roll Initiative. The lesson: even
at epic tier, *reactive narrative control* uses a recharge clock, not
an unlimited toggle.

**Category rules:** the +1 ASI may exceed 20 (to 30) — always include
it; one spectacular signature effect, not three medium ones; permanence
and rule-breaking are acceptable, infinite action-economy loops are not.

## Choosing the Right Category

A short decision tree for routing a concept:

1. **Is it tied to who the character was before adventuring** (trade,
   upbringing, training)? → **Origin**, and it should still matter at
   level 12 (proficiencies, rerolls, PB-scaled resources age well).
2. **Is it a build choice an optimizer weighs against +2 ASI at
   level 4?** → **General**, +1 ASI included, 1–3 benefits serving one
   identity.
3. **Is it a pure combat technique tied to a weapon/armor stance,
   always on, one block of text?** → **Fighting Style** — but only if
   gating it behind the Fighting Style feature is acceptable.
4. **Does it break a fundamental rule, and would it be absurd at
   level 8?** → **Epic Boon**.

When a concept straddles two categories, build the *smaller* version
and note the larger one as a variant — a General feat can almost always
be re-pitched as the "trained" version of an Origin feat, and an Epic
Boon as its apotheosis (see the thematic set mode in SKILL.md).
