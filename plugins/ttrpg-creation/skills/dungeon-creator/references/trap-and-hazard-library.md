# Trap and Hazard Library

Trap categories, DCs by tier, telegraphing rules, the
disable/bypass/suffer triad, hazard catalogue, anti-patterns.

---

## Trap vs Hazard

| Trap | Hazard |
|---|---|
| Single trigger event | Persistent environmental condition |
| Affects whoever triggers it | Affects everyone in the area |
| Reset condition (one-shot, manual, magical) | Continuous until removed or area is left |
| Disable / bypass / suffer | Mitigation / route choice / endurance |
| Example: pressure plate dart trap | Example: thick toxic mist in a chamber |

Both have a role. Traps reward caution; hazards reward planning.

---

## The Disable / Bypass / Suffer Triad

Every trap should have all three paths:

- **Disable:** A skill check (Thieves' Tools, Investigation, Arcana,
  Sleight of Hand) that neutralizes the trap. Failure may trigger.
- **Bypass:** An alternative route or method (climb over, jump
  across, fly past, dispel, freeze the trigger, use a held action
  to time it).
- **Suffer:** The party may simply trigger and take the
  consequence. The consequence must be **survivable** for the
  party's tier.

A trap with only "suffer" is a "gotcha." A trap with only "disable"
locks out characters without the right tools. The triad ensures
multiple solutions.

---

## Telegraphing Rules

**Every trap is telegraphed.** The strength of the telegraph
scales inversely with the trap's lethality.

- **Mild trap (1–2d6 damage):** Subtle telegraph — a slight color
  shift, a faint sound, a worn spot. Passive Perception (DC equal
  to detect DC) catches it.
- **Moderate trap (3–6d6):** Clear telegraph — a stain, a scorch
  mark, a corpse, an obvious mechanism, a previously-triggered
  remnant. Active Perception almost always finds it.
- **Severe trap (7–10d6 or save-or-X):** Heavy telegraph — visible
  damaged predecessors, scorched walls, warning signs, or a
  pre-known reputation. Passive Perception finds the warning
  signs; Investigation finds the mechanism.
- **Instakill trap (Tier 4+):** Heavy telegraph + a save with a
  failure consequence (rather than no save). Tier 4 PCs can
  *probably* save; rolling a 1 on a 50/50 save = 1 death per 20
  rolls, which is acceptable design.

Avoid: **invisible damage that just happens.** "You take 4d6 fire
damage" with no narrative cue is bad design.

## DCs by Party Tier

| Tier | Levels | Notice DC | Disable DC | Save DC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1–4 | 12 | 12 | 12 |
| 2 | 5–10 | 15 | 15 | 15 |
| 3 | 11–16 | 17 | 17 | 17 |
| 4 | 17–20 | 19 | 19 | 19 |

Vary by ±1–2 for non-signature traps. Use the lower DCs for
common-grade traps; upper DCs for boss-tier and sealed-tomb traps.

## Trap Categories

### 1. Mechanical Traps

Triggered by physical action. No magic.

| Trap | Trigger | Damage / Effect | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pressure plate dart | Weight on plate | 2d6 piercing + DC X Con save vs 1d6 poison | 1–2 |
| Swinging blade | Tripwire | 3d6 slashing | 1–3 |
| Pit (covered) | Step on cover | 1d6 per 10 ft + landing condition | 1–3 |
| Pit (spiked) | Same | 1d6 fall + 2d6 piercing | 2–4 |
| Collapsing ceiling | Tripwire / weight | DC X Dex save, half on save, 4d10 bludgeoning | 2–4 |
| Crushing walls | Pressure plate | DC X Str save each round to escape, 6d10 bludgeoning | 3–4 |
| Falling block | Tripwire | DC X Dex save, 5d10 bludgeoning | 3–4 |
| Poisoned needle (lock) | Lockpicking failure | 1 piercing + DC X Con save vs 2d10 poison | 1–3 |
| Crossbow turret | Sensor / sound | 1d10 piercing per round, 3 rounds, AC 15 to disable | 2–3 |
| Net snare | Tripwire | DC X Dex save or be Restrained until freed | 1–3 |

### 2. Magical Traps

Triggered by proximity, condition, or specific action.

| Trap | Trigger | Damage / Effect | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Glyph of warding (damage) | Specific approach | 5d8 of chosen damage type, DC X save half | 2–4 |
| Glyph of warding (spell) | Specific approach | Cast spell (suggestion, hold person, fireball) | 2–4 |
| Symbol of pain | Look at | DC X Con save or disadvantage on attack rolls / Concentration checks | 3–4 |
| Symbol of fear | Look at | DC X Wis save or Frightened | 2–4 |
| Symbol of sleep | Look at | DC X Wis save or Unconscious | 2–4 |
| Alarm (audible) | Crossing ward | Alerts faction; no damage | 1–4 |
| Alarm (mental) | Crossing ward | Mental ping to bound caster | 2–4 |
| Magic missile turret | Hostile in area | 3d4+3 force, 3 rounds | 2–3 |
| Antimagic ward | Crossing | Suppresses magic in 10-ft area | 3–4 |
| Banishment ward | Crossing | DC X Cha save or banished 1 minute | 3–4 |
| Forcecage | Crossing | DC X Cha save or trapped | 4 |

### 3. Environmental Traps

Use the dungeon itself as the trap.

| Trap | Trigger | Damage / Effect | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rolling boulder | Floor tile / sound | Charge attack, 6d10 bludgeoning, DC X Dex save half | 1–3 |
| Flooding room | Door close / valve | Drowning rules apply; 1d4 rounds to fill | 2–4 |
| Suffocation chamber | Door seals | DC X Con save / round vs Exhaustion | 2–3 |
| Spike floor (lowered) | Pressure plate | Floor recedes to reveal spikes, 5d10 piercing | 2–4 |
| Lava jet | Random / mechanism | 3d10 fire, DC X Dex save | 2–4 |
| Acid drip (continuous) | Persistent | 1d4 acid per round in marked tile | 1–3 |
| Wall of ice / stone (closing) | Trip | Splits party for X rounds | 2–4 |

### 4. Social-Pressure Traps

Trap by deception or compulsion, not damage.

| Trap | Trigger | Effect | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| False guide | NPC trusted | Leads party to ambush | 1–3 |
| Cursed bargain | Accept offered deal | Geas-like restriction | 2–4 |
| Illusory exit | Walk into | Banishment, mirror trap, infinite hall | 2–4 |
| Demon-bound contract | Read aloud | Geas / Mark of damnation | 3–4 |
| Memory thief | Trigger spoken word | Wis save or lose key memory | 3–4 |

## Hazard Catalogue

Persistent environmental conditions that affect everyone in the
area.

### Atmospheric Hazards

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| Toxic mist | Con save / round vs exhaustion or poison damage |
| Choking dust | Disadvantage on Con checks; cough audibly (alert) |
| Magical darkness | Negates non-magical light; harder spell effects |
| Freezing air | Cold damage / round; Exhaustion accumulates |
| Sickening smell | Wis save vs Frightened or DC Con vs Poisoned |

### Surface Hazards

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| Slick floor | DC Dex save / round to maintain footing |
| Brittle ice | Movement causes Dex save vs Prone / break-through |
| Loose stones | Movement creates noise; potential collapse |
| Sticky tar | Half movement; potential Restrained |
| Hot coals | 1d6 fire / 5 ft moved |

### Magical Hazards

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| Wild magic field | Wild Magic Surge on spell cast |
| Necrotic taint | Healing reduced 50% in area |
| Antimagic veil | Spells suppressed in area |
| Time slip | 1 hour outside = 1 minute inside (or vice versa) |
| Reality flux | Random elemental effects each minute |

### Vertical / Movement Hazards

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| Crumbling ledge | DC Athletics to traverse; falling damage |
| Strong current (water) | Movement halved; drift towards drain |
| Updraft | Falling damage reduced; ranged attacks disadvantage |
| Gravity reversal | All "down" becomes "up"; fall accordingly |
| No-clip floor | Specific tiles drop creatures one level |

## Trap Density Guidelines

| Theme | Traps per 10 rooms |
|---|---|
| Casual dungeon (raiders' camp, beast lair) | 0–1 |
| Defended site (cult temple, garrison) | 2–3 |
| Paranoid mage's tower / wizard's lab | 4–5 |
| Sealed tomb / ancient ruin | 3–5 |
| Hostile dungeon (bandit-trapped, dwarven mine) | 2–4 |
| Living dungeon (organic, dungeon-as-creature) | 4–6 (organic hazards) |

If trap density exceeds 1 per 4 rooms, **establish the dungeon as
trap-heavy in Room 1**. Telegraph the theme early so players
adjust their play (slower advance, more Investigation rolls, more
caution).

## Anti-Patterns

### "Gotcha" Trap
No warning, no save, instant death or major character loss.
**Why bad:** Erodes player trust. Trap design becomes feared
rather than respected.
**Fix:** Always telegraph + always allow save.

### Trap Density Overload
Trap every 1–2 rooms in a non-trap-themed dungeon.
**Why bad:** Pacing collapses. Every door becomes a 10-minute
Investigation slog.
**Fix:** Trap density follows theme. Most dungeons: 1 trap per 4–5
rooms.

### Single-Solution Trap
Only Thieves' Tools at DC 18 disables it. The rogue is sick today.
**Why bad:** Locks out the table for arbitrary reasons.
**Fix:** Always provide alternative (bypass, second skill, social
solution).

### Damage-Only Trap
Trap deals damage and nothing else. Boring after the second one.
**Why bad:** Becomes math, not drama.
**Fix:** Add narrative consequence — trap alerts faction, blocks
retreat, separates party, reveals lore, drops resource.

### Invisible Trap
No telegraph in any form.
**Why bad:** Players cannot make informed decisions.
**Fix:** Always include at least one sensory cue (visual, auditory,
olfactory, tactile).

### Identical Trap Reused
Same pressure-plate dart trap, three rooms in a row.
**Why bad:** Feels lazy. Players solve it once and stop engaging.
**Fix:** Vary mechanism, damage type, telegraph, trigger across a
dungeon.
