# Settlement Toolkit Creator

## Overview

This skill creates complete, publication-ready settlement toolkits for tabletop RPGs. Each toolkit is a standalone 10-20 page product that Game Masters can drop directly into their campaigns with minimal preparation.

## When to Use This Skill

**Use this skill when:**
- Creating a complete village, town, or city setting supplement
- Developing a publishable settlement toolkit (10-20 pages)
- The user asks for a "setting," "location supplement," or "settlement guide"
- Creating content intended for DriveThruRPG or similar marketplaces

**Do not use for:**
- Single locations within a larger campaign (use general TTRPG skill)
- Dungeon crawls or wilderness adventures
- Broad campaign settings (those need different structure)

## Core Product Structure

Every settlement toolkit follows this exact structure:

### Page 1: Cover & Overview
- Settlement name and tagline (one compelling sentence)
- Visual hook (art description for layout)
- Page count, compatibility, content warnings if needed

### Pages 2-3: Settlement Overview
1. **At a Glance** (bullet points):
   - Population and demographics
   - Government type
   - Primary industries/economy
   - Notable features (1-2 sentences each)
   - Current conflicts or tensions

2. **Atmosphere & Themes**:
   - What makes this place unique? (2-3 paragraphs)
   - Tone and feel
   - Key tensions or mysteries

3. **Using This Toolkit**:
   - How to integrate into existing campaigns
   - Level range recommendations
   - Suggested adventure types

### Pages 4-9: Key Locations (5-8 locations)

Each location gets 1/2 to 1 page:

**Location Name**
*One-sentence description*

**Description**: 2-3 paragraphs of atmospheric detail
- What PCs see, hear, smell
- Important features
- Who's usually here

**NPCs Present**: Names and roles (detailed NPCs get full entries later)

**Secrets/Opportunities**:
- Hidden information PCs can discover
- Plot hooks connected to this location
- Potential encounters

**Game Mechanics**:
- Any special rules (if applicable)
- DCs for relevant checks
- Random encounter table (if appropriate)

### Pages 10-14: NPCs (8-12 characters)

Each NPC gets 1/3 to 1/2 page:

**[Name]**
*[Race/Species], [Role/Profession], [Alignment]*

**Appearance**: 1-2 sentences (concrete, memorable details)

**Personality**: 1-2 sentences (behavior, speech patterns, quirks)

**Motivation**: What do they want? What are they trying to achieve?

**Secret**: Something hidden about them (optional but recommended)

**Roleplaying Notes**:
- Key phrases or speech patterns
- How they react to different approaches
- Connections to other NPCs

**Game Statistics**:
- Use existing stat blocks when possible (e.g., "Use Knight stats, MM p. 347")
- Only create custom stats if truly necessary
- Quick reference for key abilities

### Pages 15-17: Quest Hooks & Adventure Seeds

**6-10 quest hooks**, each with:
- **Hook**: How PCs get involved (2-3 sentences)
- **The Truth**: What's actually going on (2-3 sentences)
- **Complications**: 2-3 potential twists
- **Reward**: XP, gold, items, or faction reputation
- **Connected NPCs**: Who's involved
- **Level Range**: Suggested party level

Mix of:
- Quick side quests (1 session)
- Medium adventures (2-3 sessions)
- Campaign starters (ongoing)

### Pages 18-19: Items, Lore & Rumors

**Special Items** (3-5):
- Magic items unique to the settlement
- Use standard magic item format
- Tie to local lore or NPCs

**Local Lore** (3-5 entries):
- Legends, histories, or traditions
- Each is 2-4 sentences
- At least one should be false or distorted

**Rumor Table** (d10 or d12):
- Mix of true, partially true, and false rumors
- Each is 1-2 sentences
- Some should connect to quest hooks

### Page 20: Appendices & Maps

- Settlement map (description for artist)
- Random encounter tables (if needed)
- Quick reference table (NPCs, locations, key facts)
- Credits and license information

## Writing Guidelines

### Atmosphere Over Explanation
**Bad**: "The Broken Wheel Tavern is a cozy establishment where many travelers stop to rest and enjoy the excellent food prepared by the talented chef."

**Good**: "Smoke curls from the Broken Wheel's chimney day and night. The floorboards slope toward the bar, worn smooth by three generations of boots. Old Marta's stew tastes the same as it did thirty years ago—her daughter uses the same pot."

### Concrete Details
- Names for everything: streets, districts, buildings
- Specific numbers: "population 847" not "small village"
- Sensory details: sights, sounds, smells
- Cultural touchstones: local sayings, customs, festivals

### Interconnected Content
- NPCs should reference each other
- Quest hooks should involve multiple NPCs
- Locations should connect logically
- Secrets should tie into multiple elements

### Modular Design
- Each section should work independently
- GMs should be able to use parts without reading everything
- No required reading order (except overview)
- Cross-reference liberally

## Settlement Types & Themes

### Village (Population 50-500)
- 3-5 key locations
- 6-8 NPCs
- 4-6 quest hooks
- Themes: Isolation, tradition, secrets, survival

### Town (Population 500-5,000)
- 5-7 key locations
- 8-10 NPCs
- 6-8 quest hooks
- Themes: Trade, faction conflict, growth, change

### City District (Part of larger city)
- 6-8 key locations
- 10-12 NPCs
- 8-10 quest hooks
- Themes: Politics, crime, diversity, opportunity

## Theme Examples

Strong themes make settlements memorable:

- **Haunted Village**: Living with restless dead
- **Trade Crossroads**: Where cultures collide
- **Religious Pilgrimage Site**: Faith and fanaticism
- **Mining Boomtown**: Greed and desperation
- **Fishing Village**: Secrets from the deep
- **Border Outpost**: Frontier justice
- **Artisan Haven**: Craft and competition
- **Scholarly Retreat**: Knowledge and intrigue
- **Criminal Hideout**: Honor among thieves
- **Plague Survivors**: Aftermath and mistrust

## Page Count Guidelines

**10-12 pages**: Village
- Simpler structure
- Fewer NPCs (6-8)
- Focused theme
- 4-6 quest hooks

**15-17 pages**: Town
- More locations (6-7)
- More NPCs (8-10)
- Multiple themes
- 6-8 quest hooks

**18-20 pages**: City District
- Complex locations (7-8)
- Larger NPC cast (10-12)
- Layered themes
- 8-10 quest hooks

## Publication Checklist

Before considering a toolkit complete:

- [ ] Clear unique hook (can be stated in one sentence)
- [ ] All NPCs referenced in multiple places
- [ ] Every quest hook involves at least 2 NPCs
- [ ] Locations feel connected and logical
- [ ] At least 3 secrets/mysteries for PCs to discover
- [ ] Map description provided
- [ ] All stat blocks referenced (MM page numbers)
- [ ] Writing is direct and atmospheric (not verbose)
- [ ] Rumors table included
- [ ] Level range clearly stated
- [ ] Modular (sections work independently)
- [ ] Proper D&D 5e compatibility statement

## Format for Output

When creating a toolkit, output in this order:

1. Settlement Overview (show title, tagline, at-a-glance info)
2. Ask: "Should I continue with locations, or would you like to adjust the overview first?"
3. Create locations one at a time (or in pairs)
4. Create NPCs one at a time (or in pairs)
5. Create quest hooks (all together or in groups)
6. Create items, lore, and rumors
7. Provide final summary and publication checklist

This allows for iteration and adjustment without having to regenerate everything.

## Example Snippets

### Good Settlement Tagline
"Cragmoor: Where the condemned mine obsidian to earn their freedom—or die trying."

### Good Location Hook
"The Gallows Market stands in the shadow of the mine entrance. Vendors sell supplies at triple price. Nobody haggles. Everyone knows the alternative is going back into the dark."

### Good NPC Introduction
**Kess Ironhand**
*Dwarf, Mine Overseer, Lawful Neutral*

Kess lost her left hand in a collapse. The iron replacement hasn't rusted in fifteen years—she claims it's because she washes it in blood every new moon. She's probably joking.

### Good Quest Hook
**The Silent Prisoner**
Level 3-5 | Side Quest

**Hook**: A prisoner arrived yesterday who hasn't spoken since entering the mine. Kess needs someone to find out what they witnessed below.

**The Truth**: The prisoner saw something that shouldn't exist in worked stone—a door carved with symbols that predate the kingdom. They're terrified that speaking about it will draw *its* attention.

## Quality Standards

Every toolkit should:
- Work as a standalone product
- Provide immediate value to GMs
- Require minimal prep to use
- Include unexpected elements
- Reward player investigation
- Support multiple play styles
- Be worth the price point
