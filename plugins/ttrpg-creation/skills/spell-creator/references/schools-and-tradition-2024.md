# Schools and Tradition (D&D 5e 2024)

The 8 schools detailed: thematic conventions, mechanical tendencies,
pitfalls, anti-patterns, signature spells. French vocabulary.

A spell's school is **not a flavor tag** — it informs which class
features and feats interact with the spell, which counter-magic
applies, and which thematic conventions readers expect.

---

## The 8 Schools

### 1. Abjuration (Abjuration FR: Abjuration)

**Thematic core:** Protection, dispelling, warding, banishing,
sealing.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Often grants saving-throw bonuses, resistance, immunity.
- Often *prevents* an effect rather than *causing* one.
- Counter-spell, dispel-magic effects.
- Wards on specific creatures, areas, or items.
- Banishment (sending back).

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Shield, Protection from Evil and
Good, Counterspell, Dispel Magic, Banishment, Magic Circle,
Globe of Invulnerability, Antimagic Field.

**Pitfalls:**
- "Damage spell mis-labeled as Abjuration" — Abjuration deals
  damage rarely, and usually only as a retaliatory effect (Fire
  Shield).
- Over-broad ward: an Abjuration spell that protects against
  *everything* is a balance leak.

**Anti-pattern:** Abjuration that *deals primary damage*. Re-classify
as Evocation.

### 2. Conjuration (FR: Invocation / Conjuration)

**Thematic core:** Summoning, teleportation, creation of objects
or creatures, calling things from elsewhere.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Summons a creature (the *Summon X* line in 2024: Summon Beast,
  Summon Fey, Summon Undead, Summon Aberration, Summon Construct,
  Summon Celestial, Summon Fiend, Summon Dragon).
- Teleports (Misty Step, Dimension Door, Teleport, Plane Shift).
- Creates objects (Create Food and Water, Mage's Magnificent
  Mansion).
- Calls an effect from "elsewhere" (Cloudkill is technically
  Conjuration of a poison cloud).

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Find Familiar, Misty Step, Web,
Summon Beast, Dimension Door, Conjure Animals (replaced by Summon
Beast pattern), Teleport, Plane Shift, Gate.

**Pitfalls:**
- Summon spells in 2024 use a *single statblock per spell* with
  level-based scaling, not "pick from a list." Follow the 2024
  Summon Beast template.
- Teleportation should have *clear range and target rules* (must
  see destination? Touch? Familiar location?).

**Anti-pattern:** Conjuration that just damages (without summoning,
teleporting, or creating). Re-classify.

### 3. Divination (FR: Divination)

**Thematic core:** Knowing, seeing, finding, scrying, predicting.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Reveals information: location, identity, condition, intent.
- Often non-combat ritual-eligible.
- Detect-type effects (Detect Magic, Detect Thoughts).
- Scrying (Clairvoyance, Scrying, Arcane Eye).
- Truth-revealing (Zone of Truth, Detect Lies).
- Prediction (Augury, Divination, Foresight).

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Detect Magic, Identify, Augury,
See Invisibility, Clairvoyance, Divination, Scrying, True Seeing,
Foresight.

**Pitfalls:**
- Spells that grant *complete answers* should be high level (5th+).
- Lower-level Divinations give *partial* information (a yes/no,
  a direction, an aura type).

**Anti-pattern:** Divination that controls or damages. Re-classify.

### 4. Enchantment (FR: Enchantement)

**Thematic core:** Mind-affecting, charm, compulsion, sleep,
emotional manipulation.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Targets minds (almost always with a Wisdom or Charisma save).
- Imposes Charmed, Friendly, or Frightened conditions.
- Compulsion effects (Command, Suggestion, Dominate).
- Emotional manipulation (Calm Emotions).
- Memory effects (Modify Memory).
- Most don't affect creatures with Charm immunity.

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Charm Person, Command, Sleep,
Suggestion, Calm Emotions, Hypnotic Pattern, Modify Memory, Geas,
Dominate Monster, Power Word Kill, Mass Suggestion.

**Pitfalls:**
- Enchantment with no save is too strong — almost all
  Enchantments grant a save, often with end-of-turn re-save.
- Enchantment on immune creatures (most undead, constructs) is
  worth noting in the spell.

**Anti-pattern:** Enchantment that damages without mind-affecting
component. Re-classify.

### 5. Evocation (FR: Évocation)

**Thematic core:** Energy manipulation, raw damage, elemental
blasts, controlled illumination.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Direct damage of an elemental type (acid, cold, fire,
  lightning, thunder, force, radiant).
- Often AoE (Fireball, Cone of Cold, Lightning Bolt).
- Shields and damage-reflectors (Shield, Fire Shield).
- Light effects (Daylight, Sunburst).
- Force effects (Magic Missile, Disintegrate).

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Fire Bolt, Magic Missile, Burning
Hands, Fireball, Lightning Bolt, Fire Shield, Wall of Fire,
Disintegrate, Sunburst, Meteor Swarm.

**Pitfalls:**
- "Just damage" Evocation needs identity — what's the *texture*
  of this damage? Cold that lingers? Fire that splits? Force that
  bypasses cover?
- AoE shape choice should match concept (cone for breath-like,
  line for bolt-like, sphere for explosion).

**Anti-pattern:** Evocation that doesn't deal damage and doesn't
involve raw energy. Re-classify.

### 6. Illusion (FR: Illusion)

**Thematic core:** Sensory deception, false images, hidden things,
mind-creating-its-own-reality.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Creates false sensory data (visible, audible, tactile, scent).
- Often allows Investigation check to disbelieve.
- Effects vanish if disbelieved or interacted with non-trivially.
- Invisibility, concealment.
- Mirror images, decoys.

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Minor Illusion, Disguise Self,
Silent Image, Invisibility, Mirror Image, Major Image, Mirage
Arcane, Programmed Illusion, Project Image, Weird.

**Pitfalls:**
- Illusion that *damages* without being justified by belief (a
  hit) is technically not Illusion — it's Evocation flavored as
  illusion.
- Illusion's power lies in *belief*; mechanical interaction (a
  successful save, an Investigation check) breaks the illusion.

**Anti-pattern:** Illusion that *bypasses disbelief*. Re-classify
as Evocation or Enchantment.

### 7. Necromancy (FR: Nécromancie)

**Thematic core:** Death, undeath, life-force manipulation,
necrotic damage, draining.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Necrotic damage type prevalent.
- Animates dead (Animate Dead, Create Undead).
- Life-drain effects (Vampiric Touch, Enervation).
- Curse / weaken effects (Bane, Bestow Curse).
- Healing-suppression (Hollowing Touch-pattern: target can't
  regain HP).
- Speak with Dead.

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Chill Touch, Inflict Wounds, Ray
of Sickness, Vampiric Touch, Bestow Curse, Animate Dead, Speak
with Dead, Finger of Death, Power Word Kill.

**Pitfalls:**
- Necromancy stigmatized in many settings — note moral / legal
  context in spell lore.
- "Healing the caster by harming the target" is a Necromancy
  motif — drain effects should mirror this.

**Anti-pattern:** Necromancy that doesn't involve death, undeath,
necrotic damage, or life-force. Re-classify.

### 8. Transmutation (FR: Transmutation)

**Thematic core:** Changing form, properties, or essence of things.

**Mechanical conventions:**
- Changes a creature's form (Polymorph, Shapechange).
- Changes a creature's stats (Enlarge / Reduce, Haste, Slow).
- Changes an object's properties (Mending, Animate Object,
  Transmute Rock).
- Changes terrain (Move Earth, Stone Shape).
- Changes elements (Transmute Water-to-Wine, Wall of Stone).
- Buffs that *change* the recipient (Haste = supernaturally
  accelerated; Bull's Strength flavor).

**Signature PHB 2024 spells:** Feather Fall, Enlarge / Reduce,
Spider Climb, Haste, Slow, Polymorph, Stone Shape, Animate
Objects, Move Earth, True Polymorph, Shapechange.

**Pitfalls:**
- Transmutation that *just* deals damage (without form-change) is
  mis-classified. Damage effects from Transmutation should be
  *consequence* of transformation (the target's flesh becomes
  brittle stone, then shatters).
- Form-change spells should specify what carries over (HP, items,
  ability scores).

**Anti-pattern:** Transmutation with no transformation. Re-classify.

## School Choice Decision Tree

Given a concept, ask:

1. **Is the spell's main mechanism mind-affecting?** → Enchantment.
2. **Does the spell deal damage as its primary purpose?** →
   Evocation (or Necromancy if necrotic / life-draining).
3. **Does the spell create or summon something?** → Conjuration.
4. **Does the spell reveal information or sense things?** →
   Divination.
5. **Does the spell create false sensory data?** → Illusion.
6. **Does the spell protect, dispel, or ward?** → Abjuration.
7. **Does the spell change a creature or object's form / property?** →
   Transmutation.
8. **Does the spell involve death, undeath, or life-force drain?** →
   Necromancy.

If two schools apply equally, choose the one matching the spell's
**primary mechanism**, not its flavor. A "fire ward" that prevents
fire damage is Abjuration, not Evocation, despite the fire flavor.
