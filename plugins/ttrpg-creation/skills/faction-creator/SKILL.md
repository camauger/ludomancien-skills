# Faction Creator

Create complete, playable factions with narrative depth, mechanical systems, and plot integration for any TTRPG setting.

## What This Skill Does

Generate factions at multiple complexity levels:

**Basic:** Core identity, goals, structure, and a few hooks (5-10 minutes)
**Standard:** Everything in Basic + resources, relationships, key NPCs, multiple hooks (15-30 minutes)
**Comprehensive:** Everything in Standard + detailed history, internal factions, extensive hooks, mechanical systems (45+ minutes)

Works across:
- **Settings:** Velnaris (noir fantasy), Zarathar (volcanic cultures), generic fantasy, homebrew
- **Tones:** Heroic, grimdark, noir, pulp, epic, morally complex
- **Scales:** Local gang to continental empire
- **Types:** 8+ faction templates from criminal to religious to revolutionary

## Workflow

### 1. Receive Faction Concept

Accept any input format:
- Brief: "Thieves guild in the Pleasure Quarter, morally gray"
- Detailed: Complete faction idea with specific requirements
- Template: "Use the Religious Order template with X modifications"
- Conversion: "Turn this NPC into a faction they lead"

### 2. Determine Faction Template

Ask user if not specified, or recommend based on concept (see `references/faction-templates.md`):

**Available Templates:**
- Criminal Organization (guilds, cartels, smugglers)
- Religious Order (churches, cults, monasteries)
- Noble House/Dynasty (aristocracy, merchant dynasties)
- Revolutionary Movement (rebels, reformers, resistance)
- Merchant Consortium (trade guilds, corporations)
- Arcane Society (wizard colleges, magical orders)
- Military Order (knights, mercenaries, elite guards)
- Scholarly/Artistic Circle (universities, artist collectives)

Templates can be combined: "Religious Order + Military = Crusading Templars"

### 3. Confirm Complexity and Mechanics

Ask user:

**Complexity Level:**
- Basic (quick reference for session prep)
- Standard (full faction for campaign use)
- Comprehensive (central campaign faction with extensive detail)

**Mechanical System** (optional):
- None (pure narrative)
- Narrative Tiers (qualitative power levels)
- Resource Tracks (3-5 resources rated 1-10)
- Domain Control (territory-based)
- Influence Web (relationship-focused)
- Action Economy (complex, turn-based)

See `references/power-and-influence.md` for system details.

### 4. Build Faction Structure

Generate based on template and complexity:

**Core Elements (All Levels):**

**Identity**
- Name (fits setting and tone)
- Symbols (heraldry, colors, icons)
- Public face (how they present themselves)
- Reputation (how others see them)

**Purpose**
- Stated goals (public mission)
- Actual goals (true objectives, may differ)
- Methods (how they pursue goals)
- Values (what matters to them)

**Structure**
- Leadership (who's in charge)
- Hierarchy (organizational tiers)
- Membership (how one joins)
- Size (scale appropriate to setting)

**Resources**
- Assets (what they own/control)
- Income (how they fund operations)
- Capabilities (what they can do)
- Weaknesses (vulnerabilities, limitations)

**Standard Additions:**

**Key NPCs (2-5)**
- Faction leader
- Notable lieutenants
- Interesting members
- Use your NPC creator skill or brief descriptions

**Relationships**
- Allies (who they work with)
- Enemies (who opposes them)
- Neutral (potential future relationships)
- Complex (simultaneous cooperation and conflict)

**Secrets (2-4)**
- Internal secrets (members don't all know)
- External secrets (hidden from outsiders)
- Vulnerabilities (exploitable weaknesses)
- Hidden agendas (true goals vs. stated)

**Comprehensive Additions:**

**History**
- Founding (how faction began)
- Key events (defining moments)
- Evolution (how they've changed)
- Current situation (where they stand now)

**Internal Factions**
- Moderate vs. radical wings
- Reformers vs. traditionalists
- Competing power bases
- Secret cabals

**Detailed Operations**
- Day-to-day activities
- Recruitment process
- Training/advancement
- Standard operating procedures

### 5. Generate Plot Hooks

Create 3-10 hooks depending on complexity (see `references/hook-generation.md`):

**Hook Categories:**
- Recruitment (faction wants PCs)
- Conflict (PCs caught between factions)
- Investigation (mystery involves faction)
- Betrayal (deception and double-cross)
- Protection (guard someone from faction)
- Sabotage (disrupt faction operations)
- Rescue/Extraction (save someone from faction)
- Succession (leadership crisis)
- Discovery (PCs find faction secrets)
- Resource (faction needs what PCs have)

**Scale hooks to party level:**
- Low level: Local, personal, direct
- Mid level: Regional, political, complex
- High level: National, historic, world-shaping

**Ensure variety:**
- Mix hook types
- Some favor different PC approaches (combat, social, stealth)
- Different urgency levels
- Multiple entry points for different PC backgrounds

### 6. Add Setting Integration

Customize for specific setting if requested:

**Velnaris (Noir Fantasy):**
- Emphasize moral complexity
- Corruption touches everyone
- Sensuality, desire, and secrets as motivators
- Intimate, personal stakes

**Zarathar (Volcanic Island Cultures):**
- Memory forms as faction resources/techniques
- Cultural diversity across eight distinct groups
- Volcanic activity as resource and threat
- Colonial tensions

**Generic Fantasy:**
- Flexible moral alignment
- Standard fantasy power structures
- Clear good/evil or complex as desired
- Traditional tropes or subversions

**Custom Setting:**
- Incorporate setting-specific elements
- Use established lore and NPCs
- Honor tone and themes
- Maintain consistency with setting rules


## Key Principles

**Setting Agnostic Core:** Templates work anywhere. Customize for specific settings in final step only.

**Morally Complex:** Avoid cartoon villains. Even evil factions should have understandable motivations, relatable members, and internal disagreement.

**Playable Depth:** Include enough detail for GM confidence, not so much it's overwhelming.

**Integration Hooks:** Factions should create problems and opportunities for PCs, not exist as background flavor.

**Dynamic Organizations:** Show factions as living, changing entities. Leadership struggles, resource problems, external pressures.

**NPC Faces:** Abstract organizations are forgettable. Give them specific people to interact with.

**Scalable Threat:** Faction should challenge PCs at multiple levels—physical danger, social manipulation, moral dilemmas.

## Common Requests

**"Make them more morally complex"**: Add internal reformers, sympathetic members, understandable motivations, gray-area goals

**"Add more hooks"**: Generate additional hook types, scale existing hooks to different levels

**"Integrate with existing NPCs"**: Connect faction to NPCs from your NPC creator skill

**"Make them more powerful/threatening"**: Increase resources, add capabilities, expand territory

**"Create rival faction"**: Generate opposing faction with mirrored strengths/weaknesses

**"Connect multiple factions"**: Build relationship web showing alliances, conflicts, secrets

## Setting-Specific Guidance

**Velnaris:** Emphasize corruption, desire, secrets. Use noir tone, moral complexity, intimate scales even for large factions.

**Zarathar:** Integrate Forms of Memory, volcanic resources, cultural specificity, colonial tensions.

**Generic Fantasy:** Flexible alignment, traditional or subversive as desired, scalable to any fantasy setting.

**Grimdark:** No purely good factions, survival trumps ideals, victory requires compromise.

**Heroic:** Clear moral lines possible (but internal complexity recommended), honor and ideals matter, hope persists.

**Custom:** Request setting-specific elements, tone matching, lore integration, consistency with established facts.
