# Faction Templates

Setting-agnostic frameworks for different faction types. Each template provides structure while remaining flexible for tone, setting, and theme.

## Core Faction Elements

Every faction should define:
1. **Identity** - Name, public face, symbols
2. **Purpose** - Goals (stated vs. actual)
3. **Structure** - Leadership, hierarchy, membership
4. **Resources** - Assets, capabilities, weaknesses
5. **Relationships** - Allies, enemies, complex connections
6. **Secrets** - Hidden agendas, vulnerabilities, forbidden knowledge
7. **Hooks** - Ways PCs can interact or become involved

---

## Template 1: Criminal Organization

**Archetype:** Thieves' Guild, Smugglers, Black Market Cartel, Organized Crime

**Identity:**
- Name: [Colorful or coded]
- Symbols: [Often hidden or dual-purpose]
- Public Face: [Legitimate business front]
- Reputation: [Fear, respect, necessity]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: [Money, territory, "victimless crimes"]
- Actual: [Power, survival, filling systemic gaps, personal enrichment]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Boss/Guildmaster + Inner Circle
- Tiers: Lieutenants → Crew Leaders → Soldiers → Associates
- Membership: Recruitment through skill/connection, initiation rituals
- Codes: [Rules about violence, betrayal, territory]

**Resources:**
- Assets: Safe houses, corrupt officials, information networks
- Income: [Theft, smuggling, protection, contraband]
- Capabilities: Violence, stealth, infiltration, coercion
- Weaknesses: Internal rivalries, law enforcement pressure, dependence on illegal activity

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Law: Cat and mouse, bribes, persecution
- With Other Criminals: Competition, turf wars, uneasy alliances
- With Civilians: Fear and resentment, or folk hero status
- With Elite: Blackmail, secret partnerships, class tension

**Common Secrets:**
- Leadership identity unknown to most members
- Infiltrated by law enforcement or rival organization
- Originally founded for noble purpose, corrupted over time
- Holding powerful figure's dark secrets as insurance

**Example Hooks:**
- PC is recruited for specialized job
- Faction war threatens innocent bystanders
- Leadership succession creates power vacuum
- PC's contact is actually faction member

## Template 2: Religious Order

**Archetype:** Church, Cult, Monastic Order, Faith Militia, Heretical Sect

**Identity:**
- Name: [Often related to deity, virtue, or founder]
- Symbols: Holy symbols, vestments, architecture
- Public Face: Spiritual guidance, charity, moral authority
- Reputation: [Trusted, feared, controversial, fringe]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Serve deity, save souls, spread truth
- Actual: [May include power consolidation, social control, genuine devotion, reform, revolution]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Hierarch, Prophet, High Priest, Council of Elders
- Tiers: Clergy → Ordained → Initiates → Lay Followers
- Membership: Conversion, birth, calling, ordination
- Doctrine: Sacred texts, interpretations, heresies

**Resources:**
- Assets: Temples, monasteries, religious texts, artifacts
- Income: Donations, tithes, land holdings, pilgrimages
- Capabilities: Social influence, divine magic, moral authority, zealots
- Weaknesses: Dogma inflexibility, schism potential, public scandal

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With State: Partnership, tension, separation, theocracy
- With Other Faiths: Tolerance, competition, holy war, syncretism
- With Arcane: Suspicious, accepting, or synthesizing
- With Common Folk: Beloved, feared, depended upon, resented

**Common Secrets:**
- Divine magic actually works differently than doctrine claims
- Leadership doesn't truly believe but maintains power
- Prophecy is fabricated or misinterpreted
- Deity is dead/changed/not what followers think

**Example Hooks:**
- Religious artifact stolen or corrupted
- Schism threatens to split faithful
- Leadership secretly corrupt or heretical
- Prophecy involves the PCs
- Faith's practices harm innocents

## Template 3: Noble House/Dynasty

**Archetype:** Royal Family, Merchant Dynasty, Landed Aristocracy, Corporate Family

**Identity:**
- Name: [Family name, often historic]
- Symbols: Heraldry, colors, motto
- Public Face: Legitimacy, tradition, noblesse oblige
- Reputation: [Ancient, nouveau riche, declining, ascending]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Stewardship, governance, family honor
- Actual: [Power preservation, wealth accumulation, legacy, survival, restoration]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Patriarch/Matriarch, Heir Apparent
- Tiers: Main Line → Cadet Branches → Retainers → Servants
- Membership: Birth, marriage, adoption, elevation
- Succession: [Primogeniture, election, merit, intrigue]

**Resources:**
- Assets: Land, titles, wealth, heirlooms, magic items
- Income: Taxes, rents, trade, investments
- Capabilities: Political influence, military forces, social capital
- Weaknesses: Succession disputes, scandal, overextension, rebellion

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Peers: Alliance through marriage, rivalry, blood feuds
- With Lower Classes: Paternalism, exploitation, genuine care, fear
- With Monarchy: Loyalty, ambition, rebellion, irrelevance
- With Merchants: Disdain, secret partnership, competition

**Common Secrets:**
- Heir is illegitimate, adopted, or not who they claim
- Family wealth built on atrocity or dark pact
- Blood curse or genetic defect
- One branch conspiring against another
- Secretly bankrupt, maintaining appearances

**Example Hooks:**
- Succession crisis threatens civil war
- Family heirloom must be recovered
- PCs hired to investigate scandal
- Marriage alliance requires protection
- Dark family secret resurfaces

## Template 4: Revolutionary Movement

**Archetype:** Rebels, Reformers, Underground Resistance, Freedom Fighters, Terrorists

**Identity:**
- Name: [Inspirational, coded, or reclaimed slur]
- Symbols: [Often illegal, defiant, martyrs]
- Public Face: [Often hidden, propaganda, sympathizers]
- Reputation: Heroes to some, criminals to others

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Freedom, justice, equality, change
- Actual: [May include revenge, power grab, genuine reform, chaos]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Council, Charismatic Leader, or Collective
- Tiers: Core → Cells → Sympathizers → Unwitting Supporters
- Membership: Ideology, grievance, recruitment
- Organization: Decentralized (security) vs. Centralized (efficiency)

**Resources:**
- Assets: Safe houses, printing presses, weapons caches
- Income: Donations, theft, expropriation, external support
- Capabilities: Guerrilla tactics, propaganda, popular support, sacrifice
- Weaknesses: Infiltration, resource scarcity, internal division, extremism

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Establishment: Hunted, demonized, negotiated with
- With Common Folk: Support base or alienated by methods
- With Other Movements: Coalition or sectarian split
- With Foreign Powers: Support, manipulation, or intervention

**Common Secrets:**
- Leadership using movement for personal gain
- Infiltrated by the very power they oppose
- Methods more brutal than public knows
- Funded by entity with different agenda
- Victory plan involves catastrophic cost

**Example Hooks:**
- PCs witness injustice that recruits them
- Revolution threatens collateral damage
- Leadership assassination creates chaos
- PC's loved one joins or is targeted
- Victory is within reach but at terrible price

## Template 5: Merchant Consortium

**Archetype:** Trading Company, Guild, Cartel, Corporation, Economic Power

**Identity:**
- Name: [Professional, sometimes grandious]
- Symbols: Seals, quality marks, brands
- Public Face: Prosperity, quality, economic engine
- Reputation: [Essential, exploitative, innovative, monopolistic]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Profit, quality, market stability
- Actual: [Monopoly, political influence, generational wealth, survival]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Board, Guildmaster, CEO, Partners
- Tiers: Executives → Master Craftsmen → Journeymen → Apprentices/Workers
- Membership: Wealth, skill, inheritance, sponsorship
- Standards: Quality control, pricing, territories

**Resources:**
- Assets: Goods, ships, warehouses, routes, patents
- Income: Trade, services, licensing, investments
- Capabilities: Economic pressure, information networks, logistics
- Weaknesses: Market disruption, competition, scandal, over-leverage

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Competitors: Price wars, buyouts, sabotage, cartels
- With Government: Lobbying, bribes, symbiosis, conflict
- With Workers: Exploitation, paternalism, partnership
- With Customers: Service, dependency, manipulation

**Common Secrets:**
- Shoddy practices behind quality facade
- Criminal activities funding legitimate business
- Consortium controls government through debt
- Internal embezzlement or fraud
- Product causes harm they're concealing

**Example Hooks:**
- Trade route threatened by bandits/monsters
- Workers strike or revolt
- Corporate espionage mission
- Hostile takeover with collateral damage
- Product defect causes catastrophe

## Template 6: Arcane Society

**Archetype:** Wizard College, Secret Order, Magical Academy, Research Collective

**Identity:**
- Name: [Scholarly, mystical, founder's name]
- Symbols: Magical sigils, colors, geometric patterns
- Public Face: Knowledge preservation, education, consultation
- Reputation: [Respected, feared, eccentric, dangerous]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Advance magical knowledge, train mages, protect secrets
- Actual: [Power through knowledge, prevent magical catastrophe, personal ascension, or enable catastrophe]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Archmage, Council of Masters, Triumvirate
- Tiers: Masters → Mages → Apprentices → Novices
- Membership: Magical talent, sponsorship, testing
- Specializations: Schools, research fields, dangerous vs. safe magic

**Resources:**
- Assets: Towers, libraries, artifacts, dimensional spaces
- Income: Tuition, services, patronage, discovery
- Capabilities: Powerful magic, research, consultation, defense
- Weaknesses: Arrogance, internal rivalry, ethical blindness, dangerous experiments

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Non-Mages: Superior, protective, isolated, dependent
- With Other Societies: Academic rivalry, cooperation, warfare
- With Government: Advisors, threat, partnership, independence
- With Forbidden Magic: Temptation, prohibition, secret study

**Common Secrets:**
- Researching forbidden magic "for safety"
- Leader made dark pact for power
- Experiment went wrong, still contained (barely)
- Society split between factions (not public knowledge)
- Ancient founder wasn't human/is still alive

**Example Hooks:**
- Magical disaster requires containment
- Artifact theft threatens security
- Dangerous research needs oversight
- Apprentice goes rogue
- Society war collateral damage

## Template 7: Military Order

**Archetype:** Knightly Order, Military Company, Elite Guard, Mercenary Band

**Identity:**
- Name: [Martial, honor-focused, or practical]
- Symbols: Heraldry, insignia, battle standards
- Public Face: Discipline, honor, protection, victory
- Reputation: [Elite, brutal, honorable, mercenary]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Protect the realm, uphold honor, victory
- Actual: [Glory, employment, power, survival, ideology]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Commander, General, Captain-General
- Tiers: Officers → Veterans → Soldiers → Recruits
- Membership: Skill, birth, oath, contract
- Code: Rules of engagement, honor, chain of command

**Resources:**
- Assets: Fortresses, weapons, armor, warhorses, siege equipment
- Income: Service contracts, spoils, land grants, patronage
- Capabilities: Combat, strategy, discipline, intimidation
- Weaknesses: Rigid hierarchy, expensive, trauma, obsolescence

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Employers: Loyalty or mercenary pragmatism
- With Civilians: Protectors or occupation force
- With Enemy: Respect, hatred, war crimes, rivalry
- With Other Orders: Competition, alliance, honor duels

**Common Secrets:**
- Won famous battle through dishonorable means
- Leader is coward or incompetent (hidden by subordinates)
- Committed atrocity in past campaign
- Secretly planning coup
- Magic or monsters power their effectiveness

**Example Hooks:**
- Recruitment or conscription
- Escort or protection mission
- War crimes investigation
- Coup or mutiny
- Last stand scenario

## Template 8: Scholarly/Artistic Circle

**Archetype:** University, Artists' Collective, Philosophical Society, Scientific Academy

**Identity:**
- Name: [Founder, place, ideal]
- Symbols: Tools of trade, colors, patron deity
- Public Face: Culture, progress, enlightenment
- Reputation: [Elite, pretentious, brilliant, radical]

**Purpose:**
- Stated: Advance knowledge/art, preserve culture
- Actual: [Legacy, patronage, revolution, truth regardless of cost]

**Structure:**
- Leadership: Dean, Master, Elected Chair
- Tiers: Masters → Fellows → Students → Patrons
- Membership: Talent, sponsorship, achievement
- Standards: Quality, innovation, tradition

**Resources:**
- Assets: Libraries, studios, laboratories, collections
- Income: Patronage, tuition, commissions, grants
- Capabilities: Innovation, influence through art/ideas, historical knowledge
- Weaknesses: Impractical, internal feuds, requires patronage, elitism

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- With Patrons: Dependency, manipulation, genuine support
- With Authority: Subversive or court favorite
- With Public: Education or irrelevant to daily life
- With Traditional: Preserving or destroying

**Common Secrets:**
- Research threatens powerful interests
- Artistic movement is political cover
- Society discovered something they shouldn't have
- Internal forgery or plagiarism
- Dark experiment/artwork with real power

**Example Hooks:**
- Commission with sinister implications
- Research leads to danger
- Rival society sabotage
- Revolutionary ideas threaten order
- Lost masterwork/discovery must be found

## Combining Templates

Many factions blend multiple templates:

- **Thieves' Guild + Merchant Consortium** = Black market traders with legitimate front
- **Religious Order + Military** = Crusaders, templars, inquisition
- **Noble House + Arcane Society** = Magical dynasty
- **Revolutionary Movement + Scholarly Circle** = Intellectual resistance
- **Criminal Organization + Noble House** = Organized crime family

Blend templates to create unique factions that serve your narrative needs.

## Setting Adaptation Notes

**Velnaris (Noir Fantasy):**
- Emphasize moral complexity, no clear heroes/villains
- Corruption touches everyone
- Intimate, personal stakes alongside political
- Sensuality and desire as motivators

**Zarathar (Volcanic Island, Memory Forms):**
- Factions tied to different cultures/memory traditions
- Volcanic activity as resource and threat
- Colonial tensions, indigenous resistance
- Memory magic creating unique capabilities

**Generic Fantasy:**
- Clear moral alignments if desired
- Traditional power structures
- Magic availability varies by setting
- Epic scale or intimate depending on campaign

**Grimdark:**
- No faction is truly good
- Victory requires compromise
- Power corrupts visibly
- Survival is achievement enough

**Heroic:**
- Clear good vs. evil (but factions within each)
- Honor and ideals matter
- Sacrifice is meaningful
- Hope persists despite darkness
