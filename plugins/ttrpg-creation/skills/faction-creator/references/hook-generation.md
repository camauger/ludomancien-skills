# Hook Generation

Creating plot hooks that connect PCs to factions and drive narrative forward.

## Hook Philosophy

Good faction hooks:
- Give PCs meaningful choices
- Create complications, not solutions
- Reveal faction character through action
- Scale to party level and campaign tone
- Connect to PC backgrounds when possible

---

## Hook Categories

### 1. Recruitment Hooks
**PC involvement:** Faction wants to hire/recruit PCs

**Variations:**

**Direct Recruitment**
> Faction approaches openly with offer. Transparent about goals. Emphasizes mutual benefit.
>
> *Example:* "The Merchant Consortium needs protection for a caravan through dangerous territory. Payment: 500 gold plus introductions to influential buyers."

**Covert Recruitment**
> Faction tests PCs without revealing identity. If they pass, recruitment offer follows.
>
> *Example:* PCs are offered mysterious job. Success leads to meeting with Thieves' Guild representative: "We've been watching you..."

**Desperate Recruitment**
> Faction in crisis needs help NOW. May not have time for proper vetting. Reveals vulnerability.
>
> *Example:* Revolutionary cell safe house is being raided. Leader grabs PCs off street: "Please, help us! They'll kill everyone!"

**Conditional Recruitment**
> "We'll hire you IF..." Faction has specific requirement first.
>
> *Example:* "The Church hires only those who've proven pious. Complete a pilgrimage first, then we'll discuss employment."

---

### 2. Conflict Hooks
**PC involvement:** PCs caught between faction conflicts

**Variations:**

**Crossfire**
> PC location becomes battlefield between factions. Must choose side or escape.
>
> *Example:* PCs are dining in tavern. Assassin targets crime boss at next table. Guards burst in. Everyone draws weapons.

**Ultimatum**
> Faction forces PCs to choose loyalty. "You're either with us or against us."
>
> *Example:* "The Watch knows you've associated with known criminals. Give us names or we arrest you for conspiracy."

**Proxy War**
> Factions use intermediaries (including PCs) to fight without direct confrontation.
>
> *Example:* Noble houses hire separate adventuring parties. Competition for artifact reveals they're rival proxies.

**Domino Effect**
> PC action for one faction automatically makes enemies of another.
>
> *Example:* Completing job for Merchants means crossing Thieves' Guild territory. Now Guild sees PCs as merchant assets.

---

### 3. Investigation Hooks
**PC involvement:** Mystery requires understanding factions

**Variations:**

**Whodunit Faction**
> Crime occurred. Multiple factions had motive. Determine which is guilty.
>
> *Example:* Magistrate murdered. Church, criminals, and revolutionaries all benefit. Each faction claims innocence, accuses others.

**Faction in Shadows**
> Someone is pulling strings. PCs must identify hidden faction.
>
> *Example:* Series of seemingly unrelated events (theft, murder, fire) form pattern. Unknown faction orchestrating chaos.

**Internal Purge**
> Faction member seeks PC help investigating their own organization. Requires discretion.
>
> *Example:* "I'm a priest, but I think our bishop is corrupt. I need outsiders to investigate without alerting him."

**Missing Member**
> Faction wants PCs to find disappeared member. May reveal faction secrets PCs weren't supposed to learn.
>
> *Example:* "Our courier vanished with sensitive documents. Find them before rivals do."

---

### 4. Betrayal Hooks
**PC involvement:** Faction betrays PCs or vice versa

**Variations:**

**Setup**
> Faction hired PCs for "legitimate" job. Actually used them as patsies for crime.
>
> *Example:* "Delivery job" was actually moving stolen goods. Watch arrests PCs. Real criminals nowhere to be found.

**Frame**
> Faction creates evidence PCs committed crime against rival faction.
>
> *Example:* PCs wake up in room with dead faction lieutenant. Their weapons are the murder weapons. They don't remember how they got here.

**Forced Betrayal**
> Faction holds leverage (hostage, blackmail, debt) forcing PCs to betray someone.
>
> *Example:* "We have your mentor. Do this job against your allies, or they die."

**PC Betrays Faction**
> PC must choose: honor faction commitment or do what's right.
>
> *Example:* PCs work for noble house. Discover house plans genocide. Do they warn victims (betraying employer) or stay silent?

---

### 5. Protection Hooks
**PC involvement:** Someone needs protecting from faction

**Variations:**

**Witness Protection**
> Person with faction secrets needs guarding until testimony/escape.
>
> *Example:* "This accountant embezzled from the crime syndicate. They want him dead. Get him to the border."

**Faction Target**
> PCs must protect someone faction wants dead/captured.
>
> *Example:* "Revolutionary leader is marked for execution. Smuggle them out of the city before inquisitors find them."

**Collateral Protection**
> Innocent person endangered by faction conflict. May not even know why they're targeted.
>
> *Example:* "This child is an unknowing heir to noble house. Rivals will kill them to prevent inheritance claim."

**Location Defense**
> Faction will attack location. PCs must defend.
>
> *Example:* "The orphanage is in territory the guild wants for expansion. They'll burn it down tomorrow unless someone stops them."

---

### 6. Sabotage Hooks
**PC involvement:** Disrupt faction operations

**Variations:**

**Asset Destruction**
> Destroy faction's critical resource/location.
>
> *Example:* "The cult meets in abandoned warehouse. Burn it before their ritual tomorrow night."

**Operation Disruption**
> Ruin faction's current plan without destroying organization.
>
> *Example:* "Merchants are smuggling weapons to both sides of conflict, profiting from war. Intercept next shipment."

**Reputation Damage**
> Make faction look bad publicly without direct confrontation.
>
> *Example:* "The Church claims purity. Get proof of their corruption and publish it."

**Internal Chaos**
> Create distrust, conflict, or confusion within faction ranks.
>
> *Example:* "Plant evidence that faction's lieutenants are embezzling. Let them tear themselves apart."

---

### 7. Rescue/Extraction Hooks
**PC involvement:** Someone needs rescuing from faction

**Variations:**

**Prisoner Rescue**
> Extract someone faction captured/arrested.
>
> *Example:* "My brother is in the Church's dungeons for heresy. He's innocent. Get him out before execution."

**Defector Extraction**
> Faction member wants out. Needs help escaping.
>
> *Example:* "I'm a guild assassin. I'm done killing. Help me disappear, and I'll give you everything I know about the guild."

**Hostage Situation**
> Faction holds hostages. Negotiate or raid.
>
> *Example:* "Revolutionaries seized the magistrate's house. His family is inside. End this before people die."

**Deep Infiltration**
> Person went undercover, now trapped. Need extraction before cover is blown.
>
> *Example:* "Our spy infiltrated the cult months ago. They've stopped reporting. Either extract them or confirm they've defected."

---

### 8. Succession Hooks
**PC involvement:** Faction leadership transition creates opportunity

**Variations:**

**Power Vacuum**
> Leader died/disappeared. Multiple candidates vie for control. Chaos and opportunity.
>
> *Example:* "The guild master was assassinated. Three lieutenants claim leadership. The one who controls the most territory in a week wins."

**Coup Support**
> Faction member planning overthrow needs help.
>
> *Example:* "The current high priest is corrupt. Help us install reformer, and the Church will support your causes."

**Assassination Contract**
> Hired to kill faction leader. Success creates succession crisis.
>
> *Example:* "The tyrant king must die. We'll pay 10,000 gold. What happens after isn't our concern."

**Inheritance Dispute**
> Multiple heirs claim legitimacy. Pick a side or manipulate from shadows.
>
> *Example:* "Both nobles claim their father's title. Each offers you land and gold to support their claim."

---

### 9. Discovery Hooks
**PC involvement:** PCs find evidence of faction activity

**Variations:**

**Accidental Discovery**
> PCs stumble onto faction secret/operation unintentionally.
>
> *Example:* Exploring dungeon for treasure, PCs find revolutionary weapons cache. Now revolutionaries know someone knows.

**Planted Evidence**
> Faction deliberately leaves clues for PCs to find. Might be trap or recruitment test.
>
> *Example:* Dead body with map in pocket. Map leads to faction meeting location. Coincidence or invitation?

**Research Revelation**
> Investigating something else, PCs uncover faction involvement.
>
> *Example:* "You're researching ancient artifact. Documents reveal local cult has been seeking same artifact for decades."

**Inherited Problem**
> PC mentor/family/contact dies. Their faction connections become PC's responsibility.
>
> *Example:* "Your dead uncle's will includes his membership in secret society. They expect you to take his place."

---

### 10. Resource Hooks
**PC involvement:** Faction needs specific resource PCs have/can get

**Variations:**

**Artifact Retrieval**
> Faction needs specific item. PCs are capable of acquiring it.
>
> *Example:* "Sacred relic stolen from Church is in bandit fortress. Retrieve it, and the Archbishop will grant you favor."

**Skill Requirement**
> Faction lacks specific expertise. PCs have it.
>
> *Example:* "We need someone who speaks Giant to negotiate with hill giant tribe threatening our trade route."

**Access Trading**
> Faction will trade their access/influence for PC access to something.
>
> *Example:* "You want information about the cult? We'll tell you everything, if you get us into the noble's masquerade ball."

**Leverage Exchange**
> "We have something you want. You have something we want. Let's deal."
>
> *Example:* "Your friend is in our custody. The magistrate we want silenced is your employer. A life for a life."

## Scaling Hooks to Party Level

### Level 1-4 (Local Impact)
- Single faction representative
- District/neighborhood scope
- Personal stakes
- Concrete, immediate problems
- Direct solutions possible

**Example:** "Crime boss threatens your tavern owner friend. Stop local extortion."

### Level 5-10 (City/Regional Impact)
- Multiple faction members
- City or regional scope
- Political implications
- Complex problems with consequences
- Multiple viable approaches

**Example:** "Faction war threatens entire merchant district. Broker peace or pick winning side."

### Level 11-16 (Kingdom Impact)
- Faction leadership involvement
- Kingdom or multi-city scope
- Historical significance
- Problems affecting thousands
- Solutions require resources and allies

**Example:** "Religious schism threatens civil war. Unite church or prevent war through other means."

### Level 17-20 (World Impact)
- Faction destinies determined
- Continental or planar scope
- Legendary consequences
- Problems affecting nations
- Solutions reshape power structures

**Example:** "Faction war goes global. Your choice determines which faction rules the known world."

## Multi-Hook Campaigns

**Serial Hooks:** One hook leads to another
> PC job for faction reveals bigger conspiracy, which leads to facing faction leadership, which reveals ancient secret

**Parallel Hooks:** Multiple simultaneous faction hooks
> While dealing with criminal guild, Church also recruits PCs, and noble house offers conflicting contract

**Nested Hooks:** Big hook contains smaller hooks
> Stop faction war (big hook) requires multiple smaller missions: rescue hostage, retrieve artifact, assassinate warmonger

**Branching Hooks:** PC choice creates new hooks
> Helping criminals makes enemies of law. Helping law makes enemies of criminals. Each branch has unique hooks.

---

## GM Tips for Hook Deployment

**Start Personal:** First faction hook should connect to PC background or immediate problem.

**Give Choice:** Offer hooks from competing factions. Let PCs choose allies.

**Consequences Matter:** Completing hooks changes faction relationships and world state.

**Fail Forward:** Failed hooks don't end story—they complicate it.

**NPC Faces:** Every faction hook should have specific NPC contact, not abstract organization.

**Urgency Varies:** Not every hook is time-sensitive. Mix urgent and patient hooks.

**Reward Appropriately:** Faction assistance, information, and influence are rewards, not just gold.

**Signal Danger:** Make clear when hook is above party level. Let them decide to attempt anyway or seek help.

**Connect Hooks:** Hooks should interact. Helping one faction should complicate relationship with another.

**Player Agency:** Factions can create problems, but PCs should determine solutions.

## Example: Multi-Faction Hook Cascade

**Setup:** City with four major factions

**Hook 1 (Criminal Guild):** "Retrieve stolen ledger from rival gang."

**Complication:** Ledger contains evidence of Church corruption.

**Hook 2 (Church):** "Give us the ledger to stop heresy" OR "We'll pay double what criminals offered."

**Complication:** Noble house discovers PCs have ledger, also wants it (proves their rival's treason).

**Hook 3 (Noble House):** "That ledger is ours. Surrender it or face legal consequences."

**Complication:** Revolutionary cell contacts PCs—ledger proves oppression, should be published.

**Hook 4 (Revolutionaries):** "Expose all of them. This is your chance to fight tyranny."

**Resolution:** PCs choose:
- Sell to highest bidder (wealth, faction hatred)
- Give to "righteous" faction (alliance, enemy of others)
- Publish publicly (all factions become enemies, popular support)
- Destroy it (all factions mildly annoyed, problem "solved")
- Keep it (leverage over all factions, constant danger)

Each choice creates entirely different campaign trajectory.
