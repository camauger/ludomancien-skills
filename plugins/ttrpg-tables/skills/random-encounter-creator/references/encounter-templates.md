# Encounter Templates

Detailed templates and examples for each tier and type combination.

## Tier Guidelines

### Tier 1: Vignettes
**Length**: 1-2 sentences (15-40 words)
**Purpose**: Atmosphere, background, world texture
**Mechanics**: None
**Interaction**: Passive observation

**Formula**: [Sensory detail] + [Subject] + [Action/State] + [Implication (optional)]

### Tier 2: Developed
**Length**: 3-5 sentences (50-100 words)
**Purpose**: Potential interaction, minor hook, NPC glimpse
**Mechanics**: Optional skill check, minor consequence
**Interaction**: Can engage or ignore

**Formula**: [Setup] + [NPC/Situation] + [Motivation hint] + [What happens if engaged] + [Consequence hint]

### Tier 3: Mini-scenes
**Length**: 2-3 paragraphs (100-200 words)
**Purpose**: Encounter with stakes, multiple outcomes
**Mechanics**: Stats, DCs, rewards
**Interaction**: Requires decision

**Formula**: [Vivid setup] + [Stakes clear] + [Flash stats] + [If X then Y options] + [Consequences]

## Type Templates

### SOCIAL

#### Tier 1 Social
```
A merchant argues with a customer over the quality of silk, their voices rising until 
a guard's approach sends both into exaggerated politeness.
```
```
Two servants gossip in a doorway, falling silent and finding sudden interest in their 
shoes as strangers pass.
```
```
A noble's palanquin blocks the street while its occupant haggles with a street vendor 
over something wrapped in cloth.
```

#### Tier 2 Social
```
A well-dressed woman stops you, mistaking one of you for someone else. "You're late. 
The meeting's moved—the old warehouse by the third pier." She presses a token into 
your hand and hurries away before questions. The token bears an unfamiliar guild 
mark. Someone's expecting whoever you've been mistaken for.
```
```
A street preacher has gathered a crowd, ranting about corruption in the Temple. Two 
guards push through, but the crowd's mood is turning. The preacher locks eyes with 
you: "You! You've seen it too, haven't you? Tell them!" Staying silent makes enemies 
of the crowd; speaking up makes enemies of the Guard.
```

#### Tier 3 Social
```
A man in merchant's clothes staggers from an alley, blood spreading across his 
stomach. "They took... the letters. Blue door. Chandler's Lane." He presses a 
signet ring into your hands and dies.

**The Ring**: Bears a minor noble house's seal. Worth 50gp to a fence, but returning 
it to the house earns their gratitude (and questions). The "letters" could be love 
letters, blackmail, or proof of conspiracy.

**Blue Door on Chandler's Lane**: A safehouse for the Syndicate. 4 thugs (AC 12, 
HP 11, +3 shortsword). The letters are there—correspondence between a noble house 
and a foreign power.

**If Reported to Guard**: Investigation stalls. Someone's paid to bury this.
**If Letters Delivered to Noble House**: 200gp reward, but you've made enemies of 
whoever wanted them stolen.
**If Letters Sold to Syndicate**: 100gp, no questions, but the noble house's agents 
will eventually trace the sale.
```

### DANGER

#### Tier 1 Danger
```
A patrol of guards marches past with a bound prisoner whose eyes frantically scan 
the crowd, seeking anyone who might help.
```
```
Glass shatters above as someone throws a chamber pot—or something heavier—from 
a high window.
```
```
A dog growls from the shadows of an alley, guarding something it doesn't want to share.
```

#### Tier 2 Danger
```
Three youths block the alley mouth, one spinning a length of chain. "Toll road, friend. 
Nothing personal." Behind you, two more have appeared. They want coin, not blood, 
but they're nervous enough to make mistakes. A loud enough commotion might draw the 
Guard—or their rivals.
```
```
A woman runs past, followed moments later by two men in guild colors. "Stop her! 
Thief!" But the woman's terrified, not guilty. Intervening means fighting guild 
enforcers; ignoring it means watching them catch her. What she "stole" is a ledger 
she was never meant to see.
```

#### Tier 3 Danger
```
The street empties in seconds. Locals know the signs: the chalk marks, the way 
shutters close in sequence. This is Syndicate business.

Four figures emerge from doorways. The leader smiles without warmth. "Wrong 
place, wrong time. But we're reasonable. Tell us what you saw—nothing—and walk 
away. Or..."

**The Collectors** (4 enforcers): AC 13, HP 16, shortsword +4 (1d6+2). Leader has 
hand crossbow (+4, 1d6+2, poisoned bolt DC 12 Con or paralyzed 1 minute). Won't 
fight to death; will retreat and remember faces.

**What's Happening**: They're about to execute an informant in the building behind 
them. The informant has information about Syndicate operations the Guard would pay 
for.

**If "Saw Nothing"**: They let you go. You hear a scream five minutes later.
**If Fight**: Noise brings Guard (3 rounds). Informant can be saved but is too 
terrified to talk without guarantees.
**If Alert Guard First**: Syndicate escapes. Informant dies. You're marked.
```

### MYSTERY

#### Tier 1 Mystery
```
A perfect circle of dead birds lies in the square, their heads all pointing inward 
toward something no longer there.
```
```
Identical posters cover every surface on this street—but the ink is still wet and 
no one remembers seeing them hung.
```
```
A child's chalk drawing on the cobblestones depicts the building across the street 
in flames. The building burned three days ago.
```

#### Tier 2 Mystery
```
A woman stands at the fountain, feeding pigeons. She's been there every time you've 
passed today—morning, noon, now evening—wearing the same dress, making the same 
gestures. When approached, she smiles blankly: "Have we met? I just arrived." A 
baker nearby insists he's never seen her before, but there's recognition in his eyes 
before he looks away.
```
```
Someone's painted numbers on buildings along this street: 7, 4, 2, 9. No pattern 
you can see. A beggar says they appeared last night. "Count 'em up and divide by 
the dead. That's what she said." Who is "she"? He doesn't remember saying it.
```

#### Tier 3 Mystery
```
The same man has walked past you three times in the last hour—once heading north, 
once south, once west. Same clothes, same gait, same scar on his cheek. The third 
time, he's dead. His body lies in an alley, cold for hours.

**The Corpse**: Well-dressed, no identification, pockets emptied. The scar is 
fresh—within the week. A copper coin has been placed under his tongue (old burial 
rite, unused for centuries).

**Investigation (DC 14)**: The coin bears the mark of a mint that closed fifty 
years ago. His boots have clay on them—red clay from the Necropolis district.

**Witnesses**: No one saw the body dumped. A fortune teller across the street saw 
"the dead man walking" and won't elaborate.

**What's Actually Happening**: The man stole something from the Necropolis—something 
that doesn't want to stay buried. The walking versions were echoes; the body is 
real. Whatever he took is no longer on his person.
```

### HORROR

#### Tier 1 Horror
```
Every mirror on this street is covered with cloth. No one will explain why.
```
```
A smell of flowers drifts from the boarded-up house—lilies, specifically. 
Funeral lilies.
```
```
The shadow of a hanged figure swings beneath the gallows, though the gallows 
stands empty.
```

#### Tier 2 Horror
```
A child approaches, offering a flower. "The gray lady said to give you this. She 
said you'd understand when the bells stop." The child doesn't remember any gray 
lady when pressed. The flower is nightshade—deadly but beautiful. In the distance, 
temple bells ring. They've been ringing all day. If they stop...
```
```
The butcher's shop displays its usual wares, but something's wrong. Every cut of 
meat is wrong—too long, too lean, too pale. The butcher insists it's pork, won't 
meet anyone's eyes, and keeps glancing at the cellar door. His daughter went 
missing two weeks ago.
```

#### Tier 3 Horror
```
The plague house has been sealed for three months. Now its door stands open, 
swinging in a breeze that doesn't exist. From inside: the sound of a child 
singing. The nursery rhyme stopped being popular twenty years ago.

**The House**: Three floors, upper windows boarded. Previous occupants: a 
merchant family of seven. All died. Bodies were burned in the house per 
quarantine law.

**Inside (requires DC 12 Wisdom save or Frightened 1 minute)**: Furniture 
untouched by fire, covered in ash-like dust. Handprints—child-sized—mark the 
walls. The singing comes from upstairs. It stops when you reach the nursery.

**The Nursery**: Seven dolls arranged in a circle. Their porcelain faces have 
been carved to show screaming. One doll is missing.

**What's Happening**: The merchant made a deal to save his family from plague. 
He was misled about the terms. They're not dead—they're changed. And one of 
them has left the house.

**If Dolls Disturbed**: DC 14 Wisdom save. Failure: visions of the family's 
last moments. Success: see the merchant's deal-making with something in the 
cellar.
```

### ENVIRONMENTAL

#### Tier 1 Environmental
```
Fog rolls in from the harbor, thick as milk, swallowing buildings one by one 
until visibility drops to arm's length.
```
```
Scaffolding collapses across the street with a sound like breaking bones. 
Workers shout; someone screams.
```
```
The cobblestones here glisten with something that isn't rain—too red, too thick, 
running downhill from somewhere unseen.
```

#### Tier 2 Environmental
```
A building settles with a groan of timber and stone. Cracks spider across its 
face. Inside, a lamp burns in a window. The building was condemned last month; 
supposedly no one lives there. But the lamp moves, floor to floor, as if 
someone's checking rooms—or fleeing something that rises.
```
```
The crowd's mood shifts from festival to fear in seconds. No one knows why; 
everyone feels it. Something's wrong. Something's coming. The temple bells 
begin to ring—the warning pattern, three-one-three, that no one's heard 
since the plague years.
```

#### Tier 3 Environmental
```
The street buckles. Cobblestones heave and split as something beneath pushes 
upward. Sinkholes open—two feet wide, then five, then ten. The stink of old 
sewage and older death rises from below.

**What's Happening**: Ancient tunnels beneath the city are collapsing. Or 
something is making them collapse—something large that's been dormant and 
is now moving.

**Immediate Dangers**:
- Falling (DC 13 Dex save or fall into 20ft sinkhole, 2d6 damage)
- Debris (DC 12 Dex save or 1d6 bludgeoning)
- Toxic air (DC 11 Con save or poisoned 1 hour)

**The Tunnels Below**: Older than the city above. Walls carved with symbols 
no one recognizes. Something large has passed through recently—scrape marks 
on walls too high for any man.

**If Explored**: The tunnels lead to a chamber where something is sealed. 
Was sealed. The seal is cracked now.
```

### EVENT

#### Tier 1 Event
```
A wedding procession winds through the streets, the bride's face hidden behind 
a veil she keeps clutching closed against the wind—or against being seen.
```
```
The night market opens with the lighting of a hundred lanterns, transforming 
the square into a maze of shadow and gold.
```
```
Temple acolytes march past, flagellating themselves, leaving a trail of blood 
on the cobblestones. The faithful avert their eyes. Others watch with expressions 
hard to read.
```

#### Tier 2 Event
```
The execution draws a crowd. The condemned woman stands silent as charges are 
read: "murder of a Temple priest." But something's wrong—her hands are clean, 
her bearing noble, and three hooded figures in the crowd are far too interested 
in watching her die. She's mouthed a phrase, twice now, looking at you: 
"Cobbler's Lane. The red door."
```
```
Festival fireworks explode above, but one falls short. Screams; a fire spreading. 
The guard rushes to contain it, leaving a whole district unwatched for ten 
precious minutes. Every thief in the city knows it. So does everyone with 
secrets to move.
```

#### Tier 3 Event
```
The Duke's funeral procession fills the street—carriages, mourners, soldiers, 
priests. The coffin passes, draped in black silk. But the Duke isn't in it. 
You know because he's standing beside you, disguised as a commoner, watching 
his own funeral with an expression somewhere between grief and satisfaction.

"Ah. You can see me. That's... inconvenient."

**The "Dead" Duke**: Faked his death to escape something—or someone. He's 
carrying documents he needs to get out of the city. He'll pay 500gp for safe 
passage to the docks. He'll pay more to silence anyone who knows he's alive.

**What He's Fleeing**: The woman in the lead carriage—his wife—and the men 
around her. They orchestrated his assassination. He escaped but can't prove 
it. Yet.

**Complications**: 
- The wife's men (8 guards, AC 14, HP 16) are looking for anyone suspicious.
- The Duke's mistress is in the crowd; she'll recognize him.
- The Guard captain leading the procession was bribed to "find" the Duke 
  if he surfaces.

**If Help Refused**: He slips away. Two days later, his body is found—the 
real body, this time.
**If Helped**: He reaches a ship. The wife's agents trace the help to you.
```

## Quality Markers

### Strong Entries Include
- Specific sensory details (not "something smells bad" but "the copper smell of old blood")
- Implication without explanation (let GM/players fill in)
- Actionable detail for GMs (physical tell, specific words, clear stakes)
- Connection potential (can this become more?)
- Appropriate tier complexity (don't overwrite vignettes)

### Weak Entries Avoid
- Vague descriptions ("a suspicious person")
- Complete explanations (leave mystery)
- Modern language or concepts
- Proper nouns (use anchors)
- Repetition of concepts within 20 entries
